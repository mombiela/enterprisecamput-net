Introduction

In this section, we will delve into advanced analysis techniques in Google Analytics. These techniques will help you gain deeper insights into your data, allowing for more informed decision-making. We will cover custom reports, segmentation, advanced filters, and more.

Custom Reports

Custom reports allow you to tailor the data you see to meet your specific needs. This is particularly useful for focusing on metrics that are most relevant to your business goals.

  • Creating Custom Reports:

    1. Navigate to the 'Customization' tab in Google Analytics.
    2. Click on 'Custom Reports' and then 'New Custom Report'.
    3. Define the report by selecting metrics and dimensions.
    4. Save and view the report.

  • Example:

    Custom Report: User Engagement
- Metrics: Sessions, Bounce Rate, Average Session Duration
- Dimensions: Source/Medium, Landing Page

Segmentation

Segmentation allows you to isolate and analyze subsets of your data. This can help you understand the behavior of different user groups.

  • Creating Segments:

    1. Click on 'Add Segment' at the top of any report.
    2. Click on 'New Segment'.
    3. Define the segment criteria (e.g., users from a specific country, users who completed a purchase).
    4. Save and apply the segment.

  • Example:

    Segment: High-Value Customers
- Criteria: Transactions > 5, Revenue > $500

Advanced Filters

Advanced filters allow you to include or exclude data based on specific criteria. This can help you clean up your data and focus on what's important.

  • Creating Advanced Filters:

    1. Go to the 'Admin' section.
    2. Under the 'View' column, click on 'Filters'.
    3. Click on 'Add Filter'.
    4. Define the filter criteria (e.g., exclude internal traffic, include only mobile traffic).
    5. Save the filter.

  • Example:

    Filter: Exclude Internal Traffic
- Filter Type: Predefined
- Exclude: Traffic from the IP addresses
- IP Address: 192.168.1.1

Enhanced Ecommerce

Enhanced Ecommerce provides detailed insights into the shopping behavior of users on your site. This includes product impressions, clicks, and transactions.

  • Setting Up Enhanced Ecommerce:

    1. Enable Enhanced Ecommerce in your Google Analytics property settings.
    2. Implement the Enhanced Ecommerce tracking code on your website.
    3. Use the Ecommerce reports to analyze data.

  • Example Code:

    ga('require', 'ec');

// Product Impression
ga('ec:addImpression', {
  'id': 'P12345',
  'name': 'Android Warhol T-Shirt',
  'category': 'Apparel/T-Shirts',
  'brand': 'Google',
  'variant': 'Black',
  'list': 'Search Results',
  'position': 1
});

// Product Click
ga('ec:addProduct', {
  'id': 'P12345',
  'name': 'Android Warhol T-Shirt',
  'category': 'Apparel/T-Shirts',
  'brand': 'Google',
  'variant': 'Black'
});
ga('ec:setAction', 'click', {list: 'Search Results'});
ga('send', 'event', 'UX', 'click', 'Results');

Multi-Channel Funnels

Multi-Channel Funnels (MCF) provide insights into how different marketing channels work together to drive conversions.

  • Understanding MCF Reports:

    • Assisted Conversions: Shows how many conversions were assisted by a particular channel.
    • Top Conversion Paths: Displays the sequences of channel interactions that led to conversions.
    • Time Lag: Shows the time it takes for users to convert after their first interaction.

  • Example:

    Assisted Conversions Report:
- Channel: Social Media
- Assisted Conversions: 50
- Last Click or Direct Conversions: 30

Conclusion

Advanced analysis techniques in Google Analytics allow you to gain deeper insights into your data. By using custom reports, segmentation, advanced filters, enhanced ecommerce, and multi-channel funnels, you can make more informed decisions and optimize your marketing strategies. Practice these techniques regularly to become proficient in advanced data analysis.
