Introduction

In the field of Community Management, measuring the success of strategies and actions is essential to optimize community management and achieve proposed objectives. Key metrics allow for performance evaluation and informed decision-making. In this section, we will explore the most important metrics that every Community Manager should know and use.

Reach Metrics

Reach measures how many people have seen the published content. It is an essential metric to understand the visibility of the posts.

  • Total Reach: Total number of people who have seen the content.
  • Organic Reach: Number of people who have seen the content naturally, without paid promotion.
  • Paid Reach: Number of people who have seen the content through paid advertisements.

Example of reach calculation

If a post has:
- Organic Reach: 500 people
- Paid Reach: 300 people

Then, the Total Reach is:
Total Reach = Organic Reach + Paid Reach
Total Reach = 500 + 300 = 800 people

Interaction Metrics

Interactions reflect how users are engaging with the content. These metrics are crucial for evaluating community engagement.

  • Likes/Reactions: Number of times users have indicated they like the content.
  • Comments: Number of comments made on the posts.
  • Shares: Number of times the content has been shared by users.
  • Link Clicks: Number of clicks on the links included in the posts.

Example of interaction rate calculation

If a post has:
- Likes: 100
- Comments: 20
- Shares: 10
- Link Clicks: 50
- Total Reach: 800

Then, the Interaction Rate is:
Interaction Rate = (Likes + Comments + Shares + Link Clicks) / Total Reach
Interaction Rate = (100 + 20 + 10 + 50) / 800
Interaction Rate = 180 / 800 = 0.225 or 22.5%

Growth Metrics

These metrics help evaluate community growth over time.

  • New Followers: Number of new followers in a specific period.
  • Follower Growth Rate: Percentage increase in followers in a specific period.

Example of follower growth rate calculation

If at the beginning of the month there are 1000 followers and at the end of the month there are 1200 followers:

Follower Growth Rate = ((Final Followers - Initial Followers) / Initial Followers) * 100
Follower Growth Rate = ((1200 - 1000) / 1000) * 100
Follower Growth Rate = (200 / 1000) * 100 = 20%

Conversion Metrics

Conversion metrics are essential to understand how social media actions are contributing to business objectives.

  • Clicks on CTA (Call to Action): Number of clicks on calls to action.
  • Conversions: Number of completed actions that meet objectives, such as registrations, purchases, etc.
  • Conversion Rate: Percentage of users who complete the desired action after interacting with the content.

Example of conversion rate calculation

If a campaign has:
- Clicks on CTA: 500
- Conversions: 50

Then, the Conversion Rate is:
Conversion Rate = (Conversions / Clicks on CTA) * 100
Conversion Rate = (50 / 500) * 100 = 10%

Conclusion

Key metrics for Community Management are indispensable tools for evaluating and improving community management. By understanding and using these metrics, Community Managers can make informed decisions, optimize their strategies, and achieve their objectives more effectively. Always remember to adapt the metrics to the specific objectives of your community and business to obtain the best results.
