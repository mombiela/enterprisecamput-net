Introduction

Community Management is a key discipline for managing the online presence of a brand or company. A well-structured Community Management plan can help build and maintain an active and engaged community. In this section, we will learn how to create an effective Community Management plan.

Objectives of the Community Management Plan

Before starting to develop a plan, it is crucial to define the objectives to be achieved. Some common objectives include:

  • Increase brand visibility.
  • Foster community interaction and engagement.
  • Manage online reputation.
  • Generate traffic to the website or online store.
  • Obtain user feedback.

Analysis of the Current Situation

To create an effective plan, it is necessary to conduct an analysis of the brand's current situation on social media. This analysis should include:

  • Social media audit: Evaluate the performance of current accounts.
  • Competitor analysis: Study competitors' strategies.
  • Identification of the target audience: Define who the current and potential followers are.

Definition of Strategies

Once there is a clear understanding of the current situation, strategies can be defined. Some common strategies include:

  • Content creation: Plan the type of content to be published (images, videos, blogs, etc.).
  • Editorial calendar: Establish a publication schedule.
  • Community interaction: Define how comments and messages will be responded to.
  • Collaborations and partnerships: Identify potential collaborations with influencers or related brands.

Tools and Resources

To implement the Community Management plan, it is important to have the right tools. Some useful tools include:

  • Post scheduling tools: Hootsuite, Buffer, Later.
  • Analysis tools: Google Analytics, Socialbakers, Sprout Social.
  • Design tools: Canva, Adobe Spark.

Example of a Community Management Plan

Below is an example of a Community Management plan for a fictitious sportswear brand:

| Element | Description | |-------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------| | Objective | Increase brand visibility and generate 20% more traffic to the website in 6 months. | | Target Audience | Young people aged 18 to 35 interested in fitness and sports fashion. | | Content Strategy | Post 3 times a week on Instagram and Facebook, focusing on attractive visual content and fitness tips. | | Editorial Calendar | Monday: New product post. Wednesday: Fitness tips. Friday: Customer success stories. | | Interaction | Respond to all comments and messages within 24 hours. | | Collaborations | Collaborate with 3 fitness influencers for monthly promotions. | | Tools | Hootsuite for scheduling, Canva for design, Google Analytics for analysis. |

Practical Exercises

  1. Define Objectives: Write three specific objectives you would like to achieve with your Community Management plan.
  2. Analysis of the Current Situation: Conduct a social media audit of a brand of your choice and present your findings.
  3. Create an Editorial Calendar: Design an editorial calendar for a month, specifying the type of content and publication dates.
  4. Select Tools: Research and choose two post scheduling tools and two analysis tools that you consider useful for your plan.

Conclusion

Creating a Community Management plan is a detailed process that requires a clear understanding of objectives, analysis of the current situation, definition of strategies, and selection of appropriate tools. By following these steps, you can build an active and engaged online community, improving the visibility and reputation of your brand.
