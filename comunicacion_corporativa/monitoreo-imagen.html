Introduction

Corporate image monitoring is an essential process for any organization that wishes to maintain and improve its reputation in the market. This topic focuses on the techniques and tools used to monitor how a company is perceived by its target audiences and how to manage that perception effectively.

Importance of Corporate Image Monitoring

  • Reputation: Corporate image directly affects the company's reputation, which can influence the trust of customers, investors, and employees.
  • Competitiveness: A positive image can differentiate the company from its competitors.
  • Crisis Management: Allows for quick identification and response to situations that may damage the company's image.
  • Feedback: Provides valuable information on how the company's actions and communications are perceived.

Monitoring Methods

Media Monitoring

  • Print Media: Tracking articles and mentions in newspapers and magazines.
  • Digital Media: Monitoring blogs, news websites, and forums.
  • Social Media: Analyzing mentions and comments on platforms like Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, etc.

Surveys and Opinion Studies

  • Satisfaction Surveys: Collecting opinions from customers and employees.
  • Focus Groups: Group discussions to gain deep insights into the company's perception.

Monitoring Tools

  • Google Alerts: Setting up alerts to receive notifications about mentions of the company on the web.
  • Hootsuite: Platform for managing and monitoring social media.
  • Brandwatch: Advanced tool for analyzing online brand perception.

| Tool | Description | Cost | |-----------------|--------------------------------------------------|-------------| | Google Alerts | Email notifications about mentions | Free | | Hootsuite | Social media management and monitoring | Freemium | | Brandwatch | Advanced brand perception analysis | Premium |

Practical Example

Setting Up Google Alerts

  1. Access Google Alerts: Visit Google Alerts.
  2. Create an Alert: Enter your company's name in the search field.
  3. Configure Options: Select the frequency of alerts, sources, and language.
  4. Receive Notifications: Google will send you emails when your company is mentioned.
Alert Example:
- Search Term: "Company Name"
- Frequency: Once a day
- Sources: News, Blogs, Web
- Language: Spanish

Results Analysis

  • Quantitative: Number of mentions, reach of publications.
  • Qualitative: Sentiment of mentions (positive, negative, neutral).

Conclusion

Corporate image monitoring is a continuous and strategic practice that allows companies to stay informed about how they are perceived by their audiences. By using various tools and methods, organizations can proactively manage their reputation, identify areas for improvement, and effectively respond to any crisis that may arise.
