Introduction

Monitoring and adjusting the plan is a crucial phase in corporate communication. This stage ensures that the strategies and tactics implemented are aligned with organizational objectives and that necessary adjustments are made to improve communication effectiveness.

Importance of Monitoring and Adjustment of the Plan

  • Continuous Evaluation: Allows for continuous evaluation of the performance of communication strategies.
  • Problem Identification: Helps identify problems or deviations at an early stage.
  • Resource Optimization: Facilitates resource optimization by adjusting tactics that are not working.
  • Continuous Improvement: Promotes a culture of continuous improvement within the organization.

Monitoring Tools and Methods

Digital Tools

  • Google Analytics: To monitor web traffic and user behavior.
  • Hootsuite: To manage and analyze social media performance.
  • Mailchimp: To track the performance of email campaigns.

Monitoring Methods

  • Surveys and Questionnaires: Collect direct feedback from employees and customers.
  • Metrics Analysis: Evaluation of KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) such as reach, engagement, and conversion.
  • Review Meetings: Periodic sessions to review progress and discuss necessary adjustments.

Plan Adjustment

Identification of Deviations

  • Data Analysis: Use collected data to identify areas that are not meeting objectives.
  • Stakeholder Feedback: Gather opinions and suggestions from key stakeholders.

Implementation of Adjustments

  • Strategy Review: Modify or change strategies that are not working.
  • Resource Redistribution: Allocate additional resources to areas that need more support.
  • Additional Training: Provide additional training to the team to improve specific skills.

Practical Example

Suppose a company has launched an internal communication campaign to improve employee morale. After a month, the following data is collected:

| Metric | Initial Objective | Current Result | |------------------------|-------------------|------------------| | Participation Rate | 70% | 50% | | Positive Feedback | 80% | 60% | | Campaign Reach | 100% | 90% |

Data Analysis

  • Participation Rate: Participation is below the objective, indicating that the campaign is not resonating with employees.
  • Positive Feedback: Although it is the majority, it does not reach the objective, suggesting that there are aspects of the campaign that are not well received.
  • Campaign Reach: Close to the objective, but there is still room for improvement.

Proposed Adjustments

  • Content Review: Modify messages to be more attractive and relevant to employees.
  • Incentives: Introduce incentives to increase participation.
  • Communication Channels: Evaluate if the channels used are the most effective and consider alternatives.

Conclusion

Monitoring and adjusting the plan is an essential practice in corporate communication. It allows organizations to stay aligned with their objectives and quickly adapt to changes. By using appropriate tools and methods, companies can identify problems in time and make the necessary adjustments to improve the effectiveness of their communication strategies.
