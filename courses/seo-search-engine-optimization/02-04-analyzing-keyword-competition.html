Analyzing Keyword Competition

Next ►
►

Analyzing keyword competition is a crucial step in keyword research. It helps you understand how difficult it will be to rank for a particular keyword and whether it's worth targeting. This process involves evaluating various factors that influence the competitiveness of keywords.

Key Concepts

  1. Keyword Difficulty: A metric that indicates how hard it is to rank for a keyword.
  2. Domain Authority (DA): A score that predicts how well a website will rank on search engine result pages (SERPs).
  3. Page Authority (PA): A score that predicts how well a specific page will rank on SERPs.
  4. Backlink Profile: The number and quality of backlinks pointing to a website or page.
  5. Content Quality: The relevance, depth, and uniqueness of the content on a page.
  6. SERP Features: Elements like featured snippets, local packs, and knowledge graphs that can affect organic rankings.

Steps to Analyze Keyword Competition

  1. Use Keyword Difficulty Tools

Several tools can help you determine the difficulty of ranking for a keyword. Some popular ones include:

  • Ahrefs: Provides a keyword difficulty score based on the backlink profiles of the top-ranking pages.
  • Moz: Offers a keyword difficulty score that considers the DA and PA of the top-ranking pages.
  • SEMrush: Provides a keyword difficulty percentage that indicates how hard it is to rank in the top 20 results.

Example:

Keyword: "best running shoes"
Ahrefs Keyword Difficulty: 70
Moz Keyword Difficulty: 65
SEMrush Keyword Difficulty: 75

  1. Analyze the Top-Ranking Pages

Examine the top 10 results for your target keyword to understand what you're up against. Look at:

  • Domain Authority (DA): Higher DA indicates stronger competition.
  • Page Authority (PA): Higher PA means the specific page is well-established.
  • Backlink Profile: More high-quality backlinks make it harder to outrank a page.
  • Content Quality: High-quality, comprehensive content is more challenging to surpass.

Example:

Metric Page 1 Page 2 Page 3
Domain Authority 85 78 80
Page Authority 70 65 68
Backlinks 150 120 130
Content Quality High Medium High

  1. Evaluate SERP Features

Identify any SERP features that might impact your ability to rank. These can include:

  • Featured Snippets: Quick answers at the top of the SERP.
  • Local Packs: Maps and local business listings.
  • Knowledge Graphs: Information boxes on the right side of the SERP.

Example:

For the keyword "best running shoes," the SERP might include a featured snippet, a local pack, and product listings, making it more competitive.

  1. Assess Content Gaps

Identify areas where the top-ranking content might be lacking. This can provide opportunities to create better content that addresses these gaps.

Example:

If the top-ranking pages for "best running shoes" lack detailed reviews or user testimonials, you can create content that includes these elements to stand out.

Practical Exercise

Task:

  1. Choose a keyword related to your niche.
  2. Use Ahrefs, Moz, or SEMrush to find the keyword difficulty score.
  3. Analyze the top 10 results for the keyword, focusing on DA, PA, backlink profile, and content quality.
  4. Identify any SERP features present.
  5. Find content gaps in the top-ranking pages.

Solution:

  1. Keyword: "best vegan protein powder"
  2. Keyword Difficulty:
    • Ahrefs: 60
    • Moz: 55
    • SEMrush: 65
  3. Top 10 Results Analysis:
Metric Page 1 Page 2 Page 3
Domain Authority 80 75 78
Page Authority 68 60 65
Backlinks 140 110 120
Content Quality High Medium High
  1. SERP Features: Featured snippet, product listings.
  2. Content Gaps: Lack of user reviews, detailed ingredient analysis.

Conclusion

Analyzing keyword competition is essential for effective SEO. By understanding the difficulty of ranking for a keyword, evaluating the top-ranking pages, and identifying content gaps, you can make informed decisions about which keywords to target. This process helps you create a more strategic and competitive SEO plan.
