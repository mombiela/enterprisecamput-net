How to Define Mission

Next ►
►

Introduction

A mission statement is a concise explanation of an organization's reason for existence. It describes the organization's purpose and its overall intention. The mission statement supports the vision and serves to communicate purpose and direction to employees, customers, vendors, and other stakeholders.

Key Concepts

  1. Purpose of a Mission Statement

  • Guidance: Provides a clear path for decision-making and strategic planning.
  • Motivation: Inspires and motivates employees by giving them a sense of purpose.
  • Communication: Communicates the organization's purpose and values to stakeholders.

  1. Characteristics of a Good Mission Statement

  • Clear and Concise: Should be easily understood and remembered.
  • Purpose-Driven: Clearly states the organization's purpose and primary objectives.
  • Inspiring: Motivates and inspires stakeholders.
  • Reflective of Values: Aligns with the core values and culture of the organization.
  • Broad but Specific: Broad enough to allow for growth but specific enough to provide direction.

Steps to Define a Mission Statement

Step 1: Identify Core Values

  • List Core Values: Identify the fundamental beliefs and principles that guide the organization.
  • Align with Vision: Ensure these values align with the organization's vision.

Step 2: Define the Purpose

  • Primary Purpose: Determine the primary purpose of the organization. Why does it exist?
  • Target Audience: Identify who the organization serves (customers, community, etc.).

Step 3: Outline Key Objectives

  • Short-Term Goals: Define immediate objectives that align with the mission.
  • Long-Term Goals: Identify long-term goals that the organization aims to achieve.

Step 4: Draft the Statement

  • Use Clear Language: Write the mission statement using clear and concise language.
  • Incorporate Values and Purpose: Ensure the statement reflects the core values and purpose identified earlier.

Step 5: Review and Refine

  • Feedback: Gather feedback from key stakeholders and make necessary adjustments.
  • Consistency: Ensure the mission statement is consistent with the organization's vision and strategic objectives.

Examples of Mission Statements

Example 1: Non-Profit Organization

"To empower underprivileged communities by providing access to quality education and healthcare, fostering sustainable development and social equity."
  • Purpose: Empower underprivileged communities.
  • Values: Quality education, healthcare, sustainable development, social equity.

Example 2: Technology Company

"To innovate and deliver cutting-edge technology solutions that enhance the lives of our customers and drive progress in the digital age."
  • Purpose: Innovate and deliver technology solutions.
  • Values: Innovation, customer enhancement, progress.

Example 3: Retail Business

"To offer high-quality, affordable products that meet the needs of our diverse customer base, while maintaining a commitment to sustainability and ethical practices."
  • Purpose: Offer high-quality, affordable products.
  • Values: Quality, affordability, sustainability, ethics.

Practical Exercise

Exercise: Crafting a Mission Statement

Instructions:

  1. Identify Core Values: List the core values of your organization.
  2. Define Purpose: Write down the primary purpose of your organization.
  3. Outline Objectives: List short-term and long-term objectives.
  4. Draft Statement: Using the information above, draft a mission statement.
  5. Review and Refine: Share the draft with colleagues and gather feedback. Refine the statement as needed.

Example Solution:

  1. Core Values: Innovation, customer satisfaction, sustainability.
  2. Purpose: To provide innovative and sustainable solutions that meet customer needs.
  3. Objectives:
    • Short-Term: Launch new eco-friendly product line.
    • Long-Term: Become a leader in sustainable technology solutions.
  4. Draft Statement: 
    "To provide innovative and sustainable solutions that meet the evolving needs of our customers, while leading the way in environmental responsibility."
  5. Review and Refine: Gather feedback and adjust as necessary.

Conclusion

Defining a mission statement is a crucial step in strategic planning. It provides direction, motivates stakeholders, and communicates the organization's purpose and values. By following the steps outlined above, you can create a mission statement that effectively guides your organization towards its goals.

In the next section, we will explore how to define a vision statement, which complements the mission by outlining the long-term aspirations of the organization.
