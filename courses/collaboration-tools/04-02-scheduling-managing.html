Scheduling and Managing Meetings

Next ►
►

In this section, we will explore how to effectively schedule and manage meetings using Zoom. This includes setting up meetings, inviting participants, managing meeting settings, and utilizing various features to ensure smooth and productive meetings.

  1. Setting Up a Zoom Meeting

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Log in to Zoom:

    • Open the Zoom application or go to the Zoom website.
    • Log in with your credentials.

  2. Schedule a Meeting:

    • Click on the "Schedule" button.
    • Fill in the meeting details:
      • Topic: Enter a descriptive title for the meeting.
      • Description: (Optional) Provide a brief description of the meeting.
      • When: Set the date and time for the meeting.
      • Duration: Specify the expected duration of the meeting.
      • Time Zone: Ensure the correct time zone is selected.
      • Recurring Meeting: Check this box if the meeting will occur regularly.

  3. Meeting ID:

    • Choose to generate automatically or use your Personal Meeting ID.

  4. Security:

    • Passcode: Set a passcode for the meeting.
    • Waiting Room: Enable the waiting room feature to control when participants join the meeting.

  5. Video:

    • Choose whether the host and participants' video will be on or off when joining the meeting.

  6. Audio:

    • Select whether participants can join via telephone, computer audio, or both.

  7. Meeting Options:

    • Enable join before host: Allow participants to join the meeting before the host.
    • Mute participants upon entry: Automatically mute participants when they join.
    • Record the meeting automatically: Choose to record the meeting automatically.

  8. Calendar:

    • Select the calendar service you use (Outlook, Google Calendar, etc.) to add the meeting to your calendar.

  9. Save:

    • Click "Save" to schedule the meeting.

Example

Topic: Weekly Team Sync
Description: Weekly meeting to discuss project updates and blockers.
When: Every Monday at 10:00 AM
Duration: 1 hour
Time Zone: Pacific Time (US and Canada)
Recurring Meeting: Yes
Passcode: 123456
Waiting Room: Enabled
Video: Host - On, Participants - On
Audio: Both
Meeting Options: Mute participants upon entry, Record the meeting automatically
Calendar: Google Calendar

  1. Inviting Participants

Methods to Invite Participants

  1. Email Invitation:

    • After scheduling the meeting, click on "Copy the invitation."
    • Paste the invitation into an email and send it to the participants.

  2. Calendar Invitation:

    • If you selected a calendar service, the meeting details will be added to your calendar.
    • Invite participants through the calendar event.

  3. Direct Link:

    • Copy the meeting link and share it with participants via email, chat, or any other communication tool.

Example Email Invitation

Subject: Invitation to Weekly Team Sync

Hi Team,

You are invited to our Weekly Team Sync meeting.

Date: Every Monday
Time: 10:00 AM Pacific Time (US and Canada)
Duration: 1 hour

Join Zoom Meeting:
[Meeting Link]

Meeting ID: 123 456 789
Passcode: 123456

Looking forward to your participation.

Best,
[Your Name]

  1. Managing Meeting Settings

In-Meeting Controls

  1. Participants Panel:

    • View and manage participants.
    • Mute/unmute participants.
    • Remove participants if necessary.

  2. Chat:

    • Send messages to all participants or privately to specific participants.

  3. Screen Sharing:

    • Share your screen or specific applications.
    • Allow participants to share their screens.

  4. Recording:

    • Start or stop recording the meeting.
    • Choose to record locally or to the cloud.

  5. Breakout Rooms:

    • Create breakout rooms for smaller group discussions.
    • Assign participants to breakout rooms manually or automatically.

Example

During the meeting, you can manage participants by clicking on the "Participants" button. This opens a panel where you can see all attendees, mute/unmute them, and remove any disruptive participants. Use the "Chat" feature to communicate with everyone or specific individuals. If you need to share your screen, click on the "Share Screen" button and select the screen or application you want to share. To record the meeting, click on the "Record" button and choose whether to save the recording locally or to the cloud. For smaller group discussions, use the "Breakout Rooms" feature to create and manage breakout rooms.

  1. Utilizing Advanced Features

Polling

  • Create polls to gather feedback or conduct quick surveys during the meeting.
  • Access the polling feature from the meeting controls and create questions in advance.

Virtual Backgrounds

  • Use virtual backgrounds to maintain privacy or add a professional touch.
  • Access this feature from the video settings.

Reactions

  • Use reactions (e.g., thumbs up, clap) to provide non-verbal feedback during the meeting.

Example

To create a poll, click on the "Polling" button in the meeting controls. You can create single-choice or multiple-choice questions and launch the poll during the meeting. Participants can respond, and you can share the results with everyone. To use a virtual background, click on the "Video" button and select "Choose Virtual Background." You can upload your own images or use the provided options. During the meeting, use the "Reactions" button to send quick feedback like thumbs up or clapping.
