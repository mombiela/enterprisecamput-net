Strategy Adjustment and Review

Next ►
►

In this section, we will explore the processes and methodologies involved in adjusting and reviewing strategies. This is a critical step in strategic management, ensuring that the organization remains aligned with its goals and can adapt to changes in the competitive environment.

Key Concepts

  1. Importance of Strategy Adjustment

  • Adaptability: Organizations must be able to adapt to changes in the market, technology, and competitive landscape.
  • Continuous Improvement: Regular review and adjustment of strategies help in identifying areas for improvement and innovation.
  • Alignment with Objectives: Ensures that the strategies remain aligned with the organization's vision, mission, and objectives.

  1. Triggers for Strategy Adjustment

  • Internal Factors: Changes in leadership, organizational structure, or resource availability.
  • External Factors: Market trends, economic shifts, regulatory changes, and competitive actions.
  • Performance Metrics: Deviations from expected performance indicators can signal the need for strategic adjustments.

  1. Process of Strategy Adjustment

  • Evaluation: Assess the current strategy's effectiveness using performance data and feedback.
  • Analysis: Identify the root causes of any deviations or underperformance.
  • Adjustment: Modify the strategy to address identified issues and align with new opportunities or threats.
  • Implementation: Execute the adjusted strategy with clear communication and resource allocation.
  • Monitoring: Continuously monitor the adjusted strategy to ensure it meets the desired outcomes.

Steps for Strategy Adjustment and Review

Step 1: Evaluate Current Strategy

  • Performance Analysis: Use key performance indicators (KPIs) to measure the success of the current strategy.
  • Feedback Collection: Gather feedback from stakeholders, including employees, customers, and partners.
  • Benchmarking: Compare performance against industry standards and competitors.

Step 2: Identify Areas for Improvement

  • SWOT Analysis: Reassess strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
  • Gap Analysis: Identify gaps between current performance and strategic objectives.
  • Root Cause Analysis: Determine the underlying causes of performance issues.

Step 3: Develop Adjustments

  • Strategic Options: Generate potential adjustments or alternative strategies.
  • Feasibility Study: Assess the feasibility and impact of each option.
  • Decision Making: Select the most viable and effective adjustments.

Step 4: Implement Adjustments

  • Action Plan: Develop a detailed action plan outlining steps, timelines, and responsibilities.
  • Resource Allocation: Ensure necessary resources (financial, human, technological) are allocated.
  • Communication: Clearly communicate the adjustments to all stakeholders.

Step 5: Monitor and Control

  • Continuous Monitoring: Track the implementation and performance of the adjusted strategy.
  • Feedback Loop: Establish a feedback loop for ongoing assessment and further adjustments if needed.
  • Performance Review: Regularly review performance data and make incremental adjustments.

Practical Example

Case Study: Retail Company Strategy Adjustment

Scenario: A retail company has experienced a decline in sales over the past two quarters. The company’s strategy focused on expanding its physical store presence, but market trends indicate a shift towards online shopping.

Evaluation:

  • Performance Analysis: Sales data shows a 15% decline in physical store sales, while online sales have increased by 10%.
  • Feedback Collection: Customer surveys reveal a preference for online shopping due to convenience and better pricing.
  • Benchmarking: Competitors with strong online presence are performing better.

Identification:

  • SWOT Analysis: Weakness in online presence, opportunity in growing e-commerce market.
  • Gap Analysis: Gap between current physical store strategy and market trend towards online shopping.
  • Root Cause Analysis: Insufficient investment in e-commerce platform and digital marketing.

Adjustment:

  • Strategic Options: Increase investment in e-commerce, enhance digital marketing, reduce physical store expansion.
  • Feasibility Study: Assess the cost and potential ROI of enhancing the e-commerce platform.
  • Decision Making: Decide to reallocate budget from physical store expansion to e-commerce development.

Implementation:

  • Action Plan: Develop a detailed plan to enhance the e-commerce platform, including timelines and responsibilities.
  • Resource Allocation: Allocate budget and resources to the IT and marketing departments.
  • Communication: Communicate the new strategy to all employees and stakeholders.

Monitoring:

  • Continuous Monitoring: Track online sales growth and customer engagement metrics.
  • Feedback Loop: Regularly collect customer feedback on the new e-commerce platform.
  • Performance Review: Review performance data quarterly and make further adjustments as needed.

Exercises

Exercise 1: Strategy Adjustment Scenario

Scenario: Your company has launched a new product, but initial sales are below expectations. Conduct a strategy adjustment process to address this issue.

Steps:

  1. Evaluate: Analyze sales data and collect customer feedback.
  2. Identify: Perform a SWOT analysis and identify gaps.
  3. Adjust: Develop and select strategic adjustments.
  4. Implement: Create an action plan and allocate resources.
  5. Monitor: Establish a monitoring system and review performance.

Solution:

  1. Evaluate: Sales data shows low market penetration. Customer feedback indicates lack of awareness about the product.
  2. Identify: SWOT analysis reveals a strong product but weak marketing. Gap analysis shows insufficient marketing efforts.
  3. Adjust: Options include increasing marketing budget, launching a targeted advertising campaign, and enhancing social media presence.
  4. Implement: Develop a marketing campaign plan, allocate additional budget to marketing, and assign tasks to the marketing team.
  5. Monitor: Track marketing campaign performance, customer engagement, and sales data. Adjust the campaign based on performance metrics.

Exercise 2: Case Study Analysis

Scenario: Analyze the provided case study of a technology company facing declining market share. Propose strategic adjustments and outline an implementation plan.

Steps:

  1. Evaluate: Review the case study data and performance metrics.
  2. Identify: Conduct a SWOT analysis and identify key issues.
  3. Adjust: Propose strategic adjustments.
  4. Implement: Outline an action plan and resource allocation.
  5. Monitor: Describe a monitoring and feedback system.

Solution:

  1. Evaluate: Case study data shows declining market share due to increased competition and outdated technology.
  2. Identify: SWOT analysis reveals strengths in R&D but weaknesses in market adaptation. Key issue is outdated product line.
  3. Adjust: Propose investing in new technology development, forming strategic partnerships, and enhancing market research.
  4. Implement: Develop a detailed action plan for R&D investment, partnership formation, and market research initiatives. Allocate resources accordingly.
  5. Monitor: Establish KPIs for new product development, partnership outcomes, and market share growth. Regularly review and adjust based on performance data.

Conclusion

Strategy adjustment and review are essential for maintaining organizational alignment with goals and adapting to changes in the competitive environment. By systematically evaluating performance, identifying areas for improvement, developing and implementing adjustments, and continuously monitoring outcomes, organizations can ensure sustained success and growth.
