Endorsed Brands Strategy

Next ►
►

Introduction

In this section, we will explore the Endorsed Brands Strategy, a common approach in brand architecture where individual brands are supported by a parent brand. This strategy leverages the reputation and credibility of the parent brand while allowing sub-brands to maintain their unique identities.

Key Concepts

Definition

An Endorsed Brands Strategy involves a parent brand endorsing one or more sub-brands. The sub-brands benefit from the parent brand's reputation and trust, while still maintaining their own distinct identity and positioning.

Characteristics

  • Parent Brand Visibility: The parent brand is prominently displayed alongside the sub-brand.
  • Sub-Brand Autonomy: Sub-brands have their own unique identity, target audience, and marketing strategies.
  • Mutual Benefit: Both the parent brand and sub-brands benefit from the association.

Examples of Endorsed Brands Strategy

Example 1: Marriott International

Marriott International endorses several sub-brands, such as Courtyard by Marriott, Fairfield Inn by Marriott, and Residence Inn by Marriott. Each sub-brand targets different market segments but benefits from the trust and reputation of the Marriott name.

Example 2: Nestlé

Nestlé endorses a variety of sub-brands, including KitKat, Nescafé, and Maggi. Each sub-brand operates in different product categories but leverages the Nestlé brand's credibility.

Advantages and Disadvantages

Advantages

  • Enhanced Credibility: Sub-brands gain instant credibility and trust from the parent brand's reputation.
  • Market Penetration: Easier entry into new markets due to the established parent brand.
  • Resource Sharing: Efficient use of resources, such as marketing budgets and distribution channels.

Disadvantages

  • Brand Dilution: Risk of diluting the parent brand if a sub-brand fails or faces negative publicity.
  • Complex Management: Managing multiple sub-brands can be complex and resource-intensive.
  • Confusion: Potential for consumer confusion if the relationship between the parent brand and sub-brands is not clear.

Implementation Steps

Step 1: Analyze the Parent Brand

Evaluate the parent brand's strengths, weaknesses, and market position. Ensure it has a strong reputation that can support sub-brands.

Step 2: Identify Potential Sub-Brands

Select sub-brands that align with the parent brand's values and market positioning. Ensure they have distinct identities and target audiences.

Step 3: Develop Brand Guidelines

Create comprehensive brand guidelines that outline how the parent brand and sub-brands will be presented together. This includes logo usage, color schemes, and messaging.

Step 4: Launch and Promote

Launch the sub-brands with a clear endorsement from the parent brand. Use marketing campaigns to highlight the relationship and build awareness.

Step 5: Monitor and Adjust

Continuously monitor the performance of the sub-brands and their impact on the parent brand. Make adjustments as necessary to optimize the strategy.

Practical Exercise

Exercise: Create an Endorsed Brand Strategy

Scenario: You are the brand manager for a well-known electronics company, TechCorp. TechCorp is planning to launch a new line of smart home devices under the sub-brand SmartHome by TechCorp.

Task: Develop an endorsed brand strategy for SmartHome by TechCorp.

  1. Analyze the Parent Brand:

    • List the strengths and weaknesses of TechCorp.
    • Identify the market position of TechCorp.

  2. Identify Potential Sub-Brands:

    • Describe the target audience for SmartHome by TechCorp.
    • Define the unique identity and positioning of SmartHome by TechCorp.

  3. Develop Brand Guidelines:

    • Create a logo and color scheme for SmartHome by TechCorp.
    • Draft key messaging that highlights the endorsement by TechCorp.

  4. Launch and Promote:

    • Outline a marketing campaign to launch SmartHome by TechCorp.
    • Specify the channels and tactics you will use to promote the sub-brand.

  5. Monitor and Adjust:

    • Describe the metrics you will use to monitor the performance of SmartHome by TechCorp.
    • Suggest potential adjustments based on the performance data.

Solution Example

  1. Analyze the Parent Brand:

    • Strengths: Strong reputation for quality, extensive distribution network, innovative technology.
    • Weaknesses: High price point, limited presence in the smart home market.
    • Market Position: Premium electronics brand known for cutting-edge technology.

  2. Identify Potential Sub-Brands:

    • Target Audience: Tech-savvy homeowners, early adopters of technology, middle to high-income households.
    • Unique Identity: SmartHome by TechCorp focuses on seamless integration, user-friendly interfaces, and advanced security features.

  3. Develop Brand Guidelines:

    • Logo and Color Scheme: Use TechCorp's blue and silver color scheme with a modern, sleek logo for SmartHome.
    • Key Messaging: "Experience the future of home automation with SmartHome by TechCorp. Trusted technology, innovative solutions."

  4. Launch and Promote:

    • Marketing Campaign: Launch at major tech expos, online advertising, social media campaigns, and partnerships with home improvement stores.
    • Channels and Tactics: Use TechCorp's website, tech blogs, influencer partnerships, and targeted online ads.

  5. Monitor and Adjust:

    • Metrics: Sales figures, customer feedback, online engagement, and brand awareness surveys.
    • Adjustments: If sales are low, consider promotional discounts or bundling with other TechCorp products. If feedback indicates usability issues, invest in user experience improvements.

Conclusion

The Endorsed Brands Strategy is a powerful approach that leverages the strengths of a parent brand to support and enhance sub-brands. By understanding the key concepts, advantages, and implementation steps, you can effectively develop and manage an endorsed brand strategy that maximizes brand coherence and value.
