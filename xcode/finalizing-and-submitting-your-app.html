Finalizing and Submitting Your App

In this section, we will cover the essential steps to finalize and submit your app using Xcode. This process includes preparing your app for release, creating an App Store Connect account, and submitting your app for review.

Preparing Your App for Release

Before submitting your app, ensure it is fully prepared for release. This involves several key steps:

  • Testing Your App: Thoroughly test your app on different devices and iOS versions to ensure it works correctly.
  • Optimizing Performance: Optimize your app’s performance by profiling and fixing any bottlenecks.
  • Setting the App Version and Build Number: Update the version and build number in your Xcode project settings.

Setting the App Version and Build Number

  1. Open your project in Xcode.
  2. Select your project in the Project Navigator.
  3. Click on the target for your app.
  4. Go to the "General" tab.
  5. Update the "Version" and "Build" fields.
// Example of setting version and build number in Info.plist
<key>CFBundleShortVersionString</key>
<string>1.0</string>
<key>CFBundleVersion</key>
<string>1</string>

Creating an App Store Connect Account

To submit your app to the App Store, you need an App Store Connect account. Follow these steps to create one:

  1. Go to the App Store Connect website.
  2. Sign in with your Apple ID or create a new one.
  3. Enroll in the Apple Developer Program if you haven't already.

Archiving Your App

Archiving your app is a crucial step before submission. This process packages your app and prepares it for distribution.

  1. In Xcode, select your project.
  2. Choose a target device (not a simulator).
  3. Go to Product > Archive.
  4. Wait for the archiving process to complete.
  5. The Organizer window will open, showing your archived app.

Uploading Your App to App Store Connect

Once your app is archived, you can upload it to App Store Connect.

  1. In the Organizer window, select your archived app.
  2. Click on the Distribute App button.
  3. Choose App Store Connect as the distribution method.
  4. Follow the prompts to upload your app.

Common Issues During Upload

  • Missing Icons: Ensure all required app icons are included in your Xcode project.
  • Invalid Code Signing: Verify that your code signing settings are correct.

Submitting Your App for Review

After uploading your app, you need to submit it for review.

  1. Go to App Store Connect.
  2. Select My Apps and choose your app.
  3. Complete the app information, including description, keywords, and screenshots.
  4. Click on the Submit for Review button.

App Review Guidelines

  • Functionality: Ensure your app functions as described and does not crash.
  • Content: Make sure your app content complies with Apple's guidelines.
  • Metadata: Provide accurate and complete metadata for your app.

Conclusion

Finalizing and submitting your app is a multi-step process that requires careful attention to detail. By following the steps outlined in this section, you can ensure your app is well-prepared for submission and stands the best chance of being approved by Apple. Remember to thoroughly test your app, optimize its performance, and adhere to Apple's guidelines to avoid common pitfalls during the submission process.
