Introduction

Writing press releases is an essential skill in media relations. A well-written press release can capture journalists' attention and ensure favorable media coverage. In this section, we will learn to write effective press releases through practical exercises.

Topic Objectives

  • Understand the basic structure of a press release.
  • Learn to write attractive headlines and subheadings.
  • Develop skills to write the body of the press release.
  • Practice including relevant quotes and data.
  • Improve the ability to review and edit press releases.

Structure of a Press Release

A typical press release includes the following sections:

  1. Headline: The title of the press release.
  2. Subheading: A brief description that complements the headline.
  3. Date and place: Indication of when and where the release is issued.
  4. Introduction: An initial paragraph summarizing the news.
  5. Body: Additional details, including quotes and relevant data.
  6. Contact information: Details of the person or department that can provide more information.

Exercise 1: Writing the Headline and Subheading

Instructions

  1. Headline: Write an attractive headline for a press release about the launch of a new tech product.
  2. Subheading: Write a subheading that complements the headline and provides more context.

Example

Headline: "XYZ Corp. Revolutionizes the Market with Its New 5G Smartphone"

Subheading: "The device promises ultra-fast download speeds and an unparalleled user experience"

Exercise 2: Writing the Introduction

Instructions

  1. Write an introductory paragraph for the press release about the launch of XYZ Corp.'s new smartphone.
  2. Make sure to include the key elements: who, what, when, where, and why.

Example

XYZ Corp. announced today the launch of its latest 5G smartphone, the XYZ Ultra, at an event held in San Francisco. The new device, which will be available starting next month, promises to revolutionize the mobile experience with ultra-fast download speeds and a series of innovative features.

Exercise 3: Developing the Body of the Press Release

Instructions

  1. Write two additional paragraphs that include details about the product's features and quotes from XYZ Corp. executives.
  2. Use data and statistics to support the claims.

Example

The XYZ Ultra is equipped with a 6.7-inch OLED display, a 108-megapixel camera, and a long-lasting battery that supports fast charging. Additionally, the device features a next-generation processor that ensures exceptional performance.

"We are excited to introduce the XYZ Ultra, a smartphone that redefines what is possible in mobile technology," said Jane Doe, CEO of XYZ Corp. "With its innovative design and advanced capabilities, we are confident that the XYZ Ultra will set a new standard in the industry."

Exercise 4: Contact Information

Instructions

  1. Provide the contact information of the person or department responsible for handling media inquiries.

Example

For more information, please contact:

John Smith  
Director of Public Relations  
XYZ Corp.  
Phone: (123) 456-7890  
Email: john.smith@xyzcorp.com

Conclusion

Writing press releases is a fundamental skill in media relations. Through these exercises, we have learned to structure a press release, write attractive headlines and subheadings, develop the body of the release with details and quotes, and provide clear contact information. Regularly practicing these skills will significantly improve the effectiveness of your media communications.
