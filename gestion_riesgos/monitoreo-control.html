Monitoring and risk control is a crucial phase in risk management that focuses on the continuous supervision of identified risks, the detection of new risks, and the implementation of response plans. This phase ensures that risks are managed effectively throughout the project lifecycle.

Objectives of Monitoring and Risk Control

  • Monitor existing risks: Ensure that identified risks remain under control and that planned responses are effective.
  • Identify new risks: Detect and assess new risks that may arise during the project.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of responses: Verify if mitigation and contingency actions are working as expected.
  • Update the risk register: Maintain an updated record of all risks, their statuses, and the actions taken.

Tools and Techniques

1. Risk Review

  • Risk review meetings: Periodic meetings to discuss the status of risks and the responses implemented.
  • Risk status reports: Documents summarizing the current status of risks and the actions taken.

2. Risk Audits

  • Internal audits: Evaluations conducted by the project team to review the effectiveness of risk management.
  • External audits: Evaluations conducted by third parties to provide an impartial perspective on risk management.

3. Variance and Trend Analysis

  • Variance analysis: Compare the actual performance of the project with the planned to identify deviations.
  • Trend analysis: Evaluate patterns in project data to foresee future risks.

4. Risk Management Software

There are various software tools that can help in monitoring and controlling risks. Some of the most popular include:

| Tool | Description | Features | |------|-------------|----------| | JIRA | Project management and issue tracking platform. | Risk tracking, custom reports, integration with other tools. | | RiskWatch | Specialized risk management software. | Risk assessment, audits, detailed reports. | | Microsoft Project | Project management tool with risk tracking capabilities. | Project planning, risk tracking, status reports. |

Practical Example

Suppose we are managing a software development project and have identified a risk of delay due to a potential lack of human resources. Below is how we could monitor and control this risk:

  1. Risk Identification:

    • Risk: Lack of human resources.
    • Impact: High.
    • Probability: Medium.

  2. Response Plan:

    • Mitigation action: Hire additional temporary staff.
    • Contingency plan: Redistribute tasks among the existing team.

  3. Risk Monitoring:

    • Risk review: Hold weekly meetings to review the risk status.
    • Variance analysis: Compare project progress with the planned schedule.
    • Risk register update: Document any changes in the risk status and actions taken.
# Example code to automate the risk register update
import datetime

# Risk register (simulated)
risk_register = {
    'risk_1': {
        'description': 'Lack of human resources',
        'impact': 'High',
        'probability': 'Medium',
        'status': 'Active',
        'last_update': datetime.datetime.now()
    }
}

# Function to update the risk status
def update_risk_status(risk_id, new_status):
    if risk_id in risk_register:
        risk_register[risk_id]['status'] = new_status
        risk_register[risk_id]['last_update'] = datetime.datetime.now()
        print(f"Risk {risk_id} updated to {new_status}")
    else:
        print(f"Risk {risk_id} not found")

# Update the risk status
update_risk_status('risk_1', 'Mitigated')

Conclusion

Monitoring and risk control is a continuous activity that ensures risks are managed effectively throughout the project. Using appropriate tools and techniques, project teams can identify new risks, evaluate the effectiveness of responses, and maintain an updated record of all risks. This not only helps minimize the impact of risks but also contributes to the overall success of the project.
