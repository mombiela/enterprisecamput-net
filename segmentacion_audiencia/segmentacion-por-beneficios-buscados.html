Segmentation by sought benefits is a marketing technique that divides consumers into groups based on the specific benefits they seek in a product or service. This technique is especially useful for identifying and satisfying the needs and desires of different market segments.

Key Concepts

  • Sought Benefits: Features or advantages that consumers expect to obtain from a product or service.
  • Market Segmentation: The process of dividing a market into smaller groups with similar characteristics.
  • Perceived Value: The consumer's perception of the value that a product or service provides.

Importance of Segmentation by Sought Benefits

  • Personalization: Allows companies to personalize their offers to meet the specific needs of different segments.
  • Marketing Efficiency: Improves the efficiency of marketing campaigns by directing efforts towards the most receptive segments.
  • Product Development: Helps in developing products that truly meet the needs of the target market.

Steps to Implement Segmentation by Sought Benefits

  1. Market Research:

    • Conduct surveys and interviews to identify the benefits consumers seek.
    • Analyze purchase behavior data and customer feedback.

  2. Identification of Key Benefits:

    • Group the identified benefits into categories.
    • Prioritize the benefits most valued by consumers.

  3. Creation of Segments:

    • Divide the market into segments based on the sought benefits.
    • Assign demographic and psychographic profiles to each segment.

  4. Development of Marketing Strategies:

    • Create specific marketing messages for each segment.
    • Design products and services that meet the sought benefits of each segment.

Practical Example

Suppose a personal care products company wants to segment its market based on sought benefits. Below is an example of how it might do this:

Step 1: Market Research

The company conducts a survey and discovers that consumers mainly seek the following benefits in personal care products:

  • Hydration
  • Sun protection
  • Anti-aging
  • Pleasant fragrance

Step 2: Identification of Key Benefits

They group the benefits into the following categories:

  • Hydration: Products that keep the skin hydrated.
  • Sun Protection: Products that protect against UV rays.
  • Anti-aging: Products that reduce signs of aging.
  • Fragrance: Products with pleasant and long-lasting fragrances.

Step 3: Creation of Segments

They divide the market into segments based on the sought benefits:

| Segment | Main Benefit | Demographic Profile | |---------------------|------------------------|----------------------------| | Hydration Segment | Hydration | Women aged 25-40 | | Sun Segment | Sun Protection | Men and women aged 20-35 | | Anti-aging Segment | Anti-aging | Women aged 35-50 | | Fragrance Segment | Fragrance | Men and women aged 18-30 |

Step 4: Development of Marketing Strategies

  • Hydration Segment: Create campaigns that highlight the product's hydration capabilities.
  • Sun Segment: Promote sun protection and skin benefits.
  • Anti-aging Segment: Focus messages on reducing wrinkles and signs of aging.
  • Fragrance Segment: Emphasize unique and long-lasting fragrances.

Conclusion

Segmentation by sought benefits is a powerful tool that allows companies to better understand their consumers and offer products and services that truly meet their needs. By following a structured process of research, benefit identification, segment creation, and marketing strategy development, companies can significantly improve their market effectiveness and increase customer satisfaction.
