Introduction

In this case study, we will explore how a retail company can use audience segmentation to improve its marketing strategies and increase sales. We will analyze the available data, identify key segments, and develop specific strategies for each segment.

Case Study Objectives

  • Understand the importance of audience segmentation in retail.
  • Learn to identify and define audience segments.
  • Develop specific marketing strategies for each segment.

Available Data

For this case study, we will use a fictitious dataset that includes the following information:

  • Customer ID
  • Age
  • Gender
  • Annual Income
  • Preferred Product Category
  • Purchase Frequency
  • Total Amount Spent

Identification of Segments

Demographic Segmentation

Demographic segmentation is based on variables such as age, gender, and income. Below is how we could segment customers according to these criteria:

  • Age:
    • Young (18-25)
    • Adults (26-45)
    • Seniors (46+)
  • Gender:
    • Male
    • Female
  • Annual Income:
    • Low (< $30,000)
    • Medium ($30,000 - $70,000)
    • High (> $70,000)

Behavioral Segmentation

Behavioral segmentation is based on how customers interact with the company. Here are some examples of segments based on behavior:

  • Purchase Frequency:
    • Frequent (more than 12 purchases per year)
    • Occasional (6-12 purchases per year)
    • Rare (less than 6 purchases per year)
  • Total Amount Spent:
    • High Spenders (> $1,000 per year)
    • Medium Spenders ($500 - $1,000 per year)
    • Low Spenders (< $500 per year)

Strategy Development

Strategies for Young People with Low Income

  • Special Offers: Discounts on products popular among young people.
  • Loyalty Programs: Points for each purchase that can be redeemed for future discounts.
  • Social Media Marketing: Targeted campaigns on platforms like Instagram and TikTok.

Strategies for Adults with Medium Income

  • Promotions of High-Quality Products: Offers on premium products.
  • Personalized Email Marketing: Sending emails with recommendations based on previous purchases.
  • Exclusive Events: Invitations to product launch events or private sales.

Strategies for Seniors with High Income

  • Personalized Attention: Concierge services and premium customer service.
  • Exclusive Offers: Discounts on luxury products and limited editions.
  • Direct Marketing: Printed catalogs and personalized phone calls.

Code Example: Segmentation with Python

Below is an example of how we could use Python to segment customers according to their purchase frequency and total amount spent:

import pandas as pd

# Load data
data = pd.read_csv('clientes_retail.csv')

# Define segments
def segment_customer(row):
    if row['Frecuencia_Compra'] > 12:
        return 'Frecuente'
    elif row['Frecuencia_Compra'] > 6:
        return 'Ocasional'
    else:
        return 'Raro'

data['Segmento_Frecuencia'] = data.apply(segment_customer, axis=1)

def segment_spending(row):
    if row['Monto_Total_Gastado'] > 1000:
        return 'Alto Gastador'
    elif row['Monto_Total_Gastado'] > 500:
        return 'Gastador Medio'
    else:
        return 'Bajo Gastador'

data['Segmento_Gasto'] = data.apply(segment_spending, axis=1)

# Show the first 5 segmented records
print(data.head())

Conclusion

In this case study, we have learned how a retail company can use audience segmentation to improve its marketing strategies. By identifying and defining key segments, the company can develop specific strategies that better suit the needs and behaviors of its customers, which can result in increased sales and greater customer satisfaction.
