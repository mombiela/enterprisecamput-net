Introduction

The evaluation of the environment and context is a crucial stage in the formulation of an effective communication strategy. This process involves analyzing both internal and external factors that can influence an organization's communication. Understanding these factors allows communication professionals to design more effective messages and select the appropriate channels for their dissemination.

Internal Factors

Internal factors are those elements within the organization that can affect the communication strategy. These include:

  • Organizational Culture: The values, beliefs, and norms that prevail in the organization.
  • Organizational Structure: The hierarchy and distribution of roles and responsibilities.
  • Available Resources: Budget, technology, and personnel available to execute the communication strategy.
  • Communication History: Past experiences and lessons learned from previous campaigns.

Example of Internal Factors Evaluation

**Organizational Culture**: The company values innovation and transparency, suggesting that messages should be open and foster creativity.

**Organizational Structure**: The organization has a hierarchical structure, implying that message approvals may take time and should be planned in advance.

**Available Resources**: There is a limited budget, so the use of low-cost communication channels like social media should be prioritized.

**Communication History**: Previous campaigns have been successful on digital platforms, indicating that this channel should continue to be a priority.

External Factors

External factors are those elements outside the organization that can influence the communication strategy. These include:

  • Economic Environment: The general economic situation that can affect the purchasing power and behavior of the target audience.
  • Political and Legal Environment: Regulations, laws, and policies that can influence communication.
  • Social and Cultural Environment: Social norms, cultural values, and trends that can affect how messages are perceived.
  • Competition: The communication strategies and tactics of competitors.

Example of External Factors Evaluation

**Economic Environment**: The economy is in recession, suggesting that messages should focus on value and affordability.

**Political and Legal Environment**: New data privacy regulations require a review of customer information handling policies.

**Social and Cultural Environment**: There is a growing trend towards sustainability, so messages should highlight the company's efforts in this area.

**Competition**: Competitors are using aggressive digital marketing campaigns, suggesting that investment in online advertising should be increased.

Tools for Evaluating the Environment and Context

To carry out an effective evaluation of the environment and context, various tools can be used:

  • PESTEL Analysis: Evaluates Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Ecological, and Legal factors.
  • SWOT Analysis: Identifies internal Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.
  • Competitor Analysis: Examines the strategies and tactics of competitors.

Comparative Table of Tools

| Tool | Description | Main Use | |-----------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------| | PESTEL Analysis | Evaluates macro-environmental factors affecting the organization. | Identify external influences | | SWOT Analysis | Identifies internal and external strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats. | Evaluate strategic position | | Competitor Analysis | Examines the strategies and tactics of competitors. | Understand the competitive environment |

Conclusion

The evaluation of the environment and context is essential for developing an effective communication strategy. By analyzing both internal and external factors, communication professionals can design more relevant messages and select the appropriate channels for their dissemination. Using tools like PESTEL, SWOT, and competitor analysis can provide a comprehensive view of the environment in which the organization operates, facilitating informed decision-making.

Remember that continuous evaluation of the environment and context is crucial, as these factors can change over time and affect the effectiveness of the communication strategy.
