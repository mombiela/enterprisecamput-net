Introduction

The final evaluation of the Project Management course aims to measure the mastery of soft skills acquired throughout the course. These skills include, but are not limited to, effective communication, problem-solving, teamwork, adaptability, and time management. The evaluation consists of several sections that cover both theoretical questions and practical exercises.

Evaluation Sections

1. Theoretical Questions

This section will assess your understanding of the key concepts discussed during the course. The questions may be multiple-choice, true/false, or short answer.

  • Effective Communication: Questions about communication techniques, active listening, and constructive feedback.
  • Problem-Solving: Questions about methods and approaches to identify and solve problems in a project.
  • Teamwork: Questions about team dynamics, roles, and responsibilities.
  • Adaptability: Questions about how to handle changes and adapt to new circumstances.
  • Time Management: Questions about planning and task prioritization techniques.

2. Practical Exercises

This section will assess your ability to apply soft skills in practical situations. The exercises may include case studies, simulations, and collaborative tasks.

  • Case Study: You will be presented with a project scenario and must identify problems, propose solutions, and plan actions.
  • Meeting Simulation: You will participate in a team meeting simulation where your communication and decision-making skills will be evaluated.
  • Collaborative Task: You will work in a team to complete a specific task, evaluating your collaboration and leadership skills.

3. Self-Assessment and Feedback

In this section, you will be asked to reflect on your own performance and the skills you have developed during the course.

  • Self-Assessment: You will complete a self-assessment questionnaire to identify your strengths and areas for improvement.
  • Instructor Feedback: You will receive personalized comments from the instructor on your performance in the evaluation.

Evaluation Table

| Section | Type of Evaluation | Maximum Score | |------------------------|--------------------------|-------------------| | Theoretical Questions | Multiple choice, T/F | 40 points | | Practical Exercises | Case study, Simulation, Collaborative task | 40 points | | Self-Assessment | Questionnaire | 10 points | | Feedback | Instructor comments | 10 points | | Total | | 100 points |

Conclusion

The final evaluation is an opportunity to demonstrate your mastery of essential soft skills for project management. Through theoretical questions and practical exercises, you will be able to reflect your ability to apply this knowledge in real situations. The self-assessment and instructor feedback will provide you with a clear view of your strengths and areas for improvement, helping you to continue developing your competencies in project management.
