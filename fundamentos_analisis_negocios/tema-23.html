Introduction

Value chain analysis is a fundamental tool in business analysis that allows identifying and optimizing the activities that generate value within an organization. This analysis helps companies better understand their internal processes and find opportunities to improve efficiency and competitiveness.

Key Concepts

  • Value Chain: Set of activities that a company performs to deliver a product or service to the market.
  • Primary Activities: Activities directly related to the creation and delivery of the product or service.
  • Support Activities: Activities that support the primary activities and facilitate their execution.

Primary Activities

Primary activities are those directly involved in the creation and delivery of the product or service. These include:

  1. Inbound Logistics: Receiving, storing, and distributing raw materials.
  2. Operations: Processes of transforming raw materials into final products.
  3. Outbound Logistics: Distribution of finished products to customers.
  4. Marketing and Sales: Activities that promote and sell the product.
  5. Services: Post-sale activities such as support and maintenance.

Support Activities

Support activities facilitate the execution of primary activities and improve their efficiency. These include:

  1. Firm Infrastructure: General management, planning, finance, and quality.
  2. Human Resource Management: Recruitment, training, and development of personnel.
  3. Technology Development: Research and development, innovation, and process improvement.
  4. Procurement: Acquisition of raw materials and other necessary inputs.

Practical Example

To illustrate how value chain analysis is applied, let's consider a bicycle manufacturing company.

Primary Activities

  1. Inbound Logistics: The company receives bicycle components (frames, wheels, etc.) and stores them in its warehouse.
  2. Operations: The components are assembled to form complete bicycles.
  3. Outbound Logistics: The finished bicycles are shipped to distributors and retail stores.
  4. Marketing and Sales: The company conducts advertising campaigns and promotions to attract customers.
  5. Services: The company offers warranties and repair services for the sold bicycles.

Support Activities

  1. Firm Infrastructure: General management oversees all operations and ensures product quality.
  2. Human Resource Management: The HR department hires and trains employees on the assembly lines.
  3. Technology Development: The R&D team works on designing new bicycles and improving existing ones.
  4. Procurement: The procurement department acquires high-quality components necessary for production.

Conclusion

Value chain analysis is a powerful tool for identifying the activities that generate value in an organization and those that can be optimized. By understanding and improving these activities, companies can increase their efficiency, reduce costs, and enhance their competitiveness in the market. This analysis applies not only to manufacturing companies but also to service organizations and other sectors, adapting to the particularities of each industry.
