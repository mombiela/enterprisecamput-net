Root Cause Analysis (RCA) is a methodology used to identify the fundamental causes of problems or adverse events. In the context of business analysis, RCA is crucial for effectively solving problems and preventing their recurrence.

Objectives of Root Cause Analysis

  • Identify the main cause of a problem: Instead of addressing only the symptoms, RCA focuses on finding the root of the problem.
  • Prevent recurrence: By identifying and addressing the root cause, solutions can be implemented to prevent the problem from happening again.
  • Improve processes and systems: RCA can reveal weaknesses in processes and systems that, once corrected, improve overall efficiency and effectiveness.

Common RCA Methodologies

There are several methodologies for conducting an RCA. Below are some of the most common:

5 Whys

The 5 Whys technique involves repeatedly asking "Why?" (usually five times) until reaching the root cause of a problem.

Example:

  1. Problem: The customer received a defective product.
    • Why? The product was damaged during shipping.
      • Why? The packaging was inadequate.
        • Why? Packaging guidelines were not followed.
          • Why? The staff was not trained on packaging guidelines.
            • Why? The necessary training was not provided.

Ishikawa Diagram (Fishbone Diagram)

This diagram helps visualize the possible causes of a problem, categorizing them into different areas such as people, processes, materials, machinery, etc.

Example:

| Category | Possible Causes | |----------------|----------------------------------------| | People | Lack of training, human errors | | Processes | Inadequate procedures | | Materials | Defective materials | | Machinery | Equipment failures | | Environment | Inadequate working conditions |

Pareto Analysis

Pareto analysis is based on the principle that 80% of problems are caused by 20% of the causes. This technique helps prioritize the most significant causes to address them first.

Example:

  1. Problem: Project delivery delays.
    • Causes:
      • Lack of resources (40%)
      • Poor planning (30%)
      • Technical problems (20%)
      • Others (10%)

Practical Example of RCA

Suppose a software company faces recurring issues with application crashes. We will use the 5 Whys technique to identify the root cause.

  1. Problem: The application crashes frequently.
    • Why? There are errors in the code.
      • Why? Exhaustive testing was not performed.
        • Why? The testing team is overloaded.
          • Why? There is not enough staff on the testing team.
            • Why? Additional staff has not been hired despite the increased workload.

Solution: Hire additional staff for the testing team and improve resource planning.

Conclusion

Root Cause Analysis is an essential tool in business analysis for effectively identifying and solving problems. By using methodologies such as the 5 Whys, the Ishikawa Diagram, and Pareto Analysis, analysts can reach the root cause of problems and develop solutions that prevent their recurrence. Implementing RCA in business processes not only improves problem-solving but also contributes to the continuous improvement of systems and processes.
