Introduction

Submitting an app to the App Store is a crucial step in the app development process. This section will guide you through the necessary steps to prepare your Objective-C app for submission, ensuring it meets Apple's guidelines and requirements.

Prerequisites

Before you begin the submission process, ensure you have the following:

  • An Apple Developer account.
  • A completed app that adheres to Apple's App Store Review Guidelines.
  • Necessary app assets (icons, screenshots, etc.).
  • A Mac with Xcode installed.

Preparing Your App for Submission

App Store Connect

App Store Connect is the platform where you manage your apps, including submission, updates, and analytics.

  1. Create an App Store Connect account:

    • Go to App Store Connect and sign in with your Apple Developer account.

  2. Register your app:

    • Navigate to "My Apps" and click the "+" button to add a new app.
    • Fill in the required information such as App Name, Primary Language, Bundle ID, and SKU.

App Information

Fill in the necessary details about your app:

  • App Name: The name of your app as it will appear on the App Store.
  • Description: A detailed description of your app's features and functionality.
  • Keywords: Keywords that describe your app to improve searchability.
  • Support URL: A URL where users can get support for your app.
  • Marketing URL: A URL for marketing your app (optional).

App Assets

Prepare and upload the required assets:

  • App Icon: Ensure your app icon meets Apple's specifications.
  • Screenshots: Provide screenshots for all device sizes your app supports.
  • App Preview (optional): A short video demonstrating your app.

App Version

Specify the version number of your app:

  • Version Number: Follow semantic versioning (e.g., 1.0.0).
  • Build Number: Increment this number with each new build you submit.

Configuring Your Xcode Project

App Identifiers and Provisioning Profiles

Ensure your Xcode project is properly configured:

  • Bundle Identifier: This should match the Bundle ID you registered in App Store Connect.
  • Provisioning Profile: Ensure you have the correct provisioning profile for distribution.

Code Signing

Code signing is essential for app submission:

  • Automatic Signing: Enable automatic signing in Xcode to let Xcode manage your certificates and profiles.
  • Manual Signing: If you prefer manual signing, ensure you have the correct certificates and provisioning profiles.

Archiving Your App

Create an archive of your app for submission:

  1. Open your project in Xcode.
  2. Select a target device (not a simulator).
  3. Go to Product > Archive.
  4. Once the archive is created, the Organizer window will open.

Uploading Your App

Upload your app to App Store Connect:

  1. In the Organizer window, select your archive and click "Distribute App".
  2. Choose "App Store Connect" as the distribution method.
  3. Follow the prompts to validate and upload your app.

Submitting Your App for Review

App Store Connect Submission

  1. Once your app is uploaded, go back to App Store Connect.
  2. Select your app and navigate to the "App Store" tab.
  3. Complete any remaining fields, such as pricing and availability.
  4. Submit your app for review.

Review Process

Apple will review your app to ensure it meets their guidelines:

  • Review Time: The review process can take a few days to a week.
  • Feedback: If your app is rejected, Apple will provide feedback on what needs to be fixed.

Conclusion

Submitting an app to the App Store involves several steps, from preparing your app and assets to configuring your Xcode project and finally submitting your app for review. By following this structured process, you can ensure a smooth submission and increase the chances of your app being approved. Remember to carefully read and adhere to Apple's guidelines to avoid common pitfalls and rejections.
