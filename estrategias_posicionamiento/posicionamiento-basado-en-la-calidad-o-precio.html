Positioning based on quality or price is a marketing strategy that seeks to define how a company wants its product or service to be perceived in the market. This perception can be oriented towards superior quality, the lowest price, or a combination of both.

Key Concepts

  • Positioning: The way a product or service is perceived in the consumer's mind in relation to the competition.
  • Quality: The extent to which a product or service meets customer expectations.
  • Price: The amount of money consumers are willing to pay for a product or service.

Positioning Strategies

Quality-Based Positioning

This strategy focuses on highlighting the superiority of the product or service in terms of features, durability, performance, etc.

  • Advantages:

    • Justification of higher prices.
    • Customer loyalty.
    • Clear differentiation in the market.

  • Disadvantages:

    • Higher production costs.
    • Need to maintain high quality standards.

Example:

Apple is a classic example of quality-based positioning. Its products, such as the iPhone, are perceived as high quality and are associated with innovation and superior design.

Price-Based Positioning

This strategy focuses on offering products or services at a lower price than the competition, attracting price-sensitive consumers.

  • Advantages:

    • Attraction of a broad market segment.
    • Increase in sales volume.

  • Disadvantages:

    • Lower profit margin.
    • Perception of lower quality.

Example:

Walmart uses a price-based positioning strategy, offering products at lower prices than its competitors, which attracts consumers looking to save money.

Strategy Comparison

| Strategy | Advantages | Disadvantages | |---------------------------|-----------------------------------|-----------------------------------| | Quality-Based | Justification of high prices | High production costs | | Price-Based | Attracts price-sensitive consumers| Lower profit margin |

Practical Exercises

  1. Strategy Identification:

    • Analyze three companies from different sectors and determine if their positioning is based on quality, price, or a combination of both. Justify your answer.

  2. Strategy Development:

    • Imagine you are the marketing manager of a new sportswear brand. Develop a quality-based positioning strategy and a price-based positioning strategy. Describe how you would communicate this strategy to your customers.

Conclusion

Positioning based on quality or price is a crucial strategic decision that can determine the success of a product or service in the market. Understanding the advantages and disadvantages of each approach allows companies to make informed decisions aligned with their business objectives and customer expectations.
