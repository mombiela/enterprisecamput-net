Introduction

Positioning is a marketing strategy that focuses on how a brand is perceived in the minds of consumers compared to its competitors. Over the years, this strategy has evolved significantly, adapting to changes in the market and available technologies.

Origins of Positioning

  • 1960s: The concept of positioning began to take shape. Companies started to realize the importance of differentiating their products in an increasingly saturated market.
  • Jack Trout and Al Ries: In 1969, these two advertisers formally introduced the term "positioning" in a series of articles in the magazine "Advertising Age". Later, in 1981, they published the book "Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind", which became a fundamental reference in the field of marketing.

Evolution of Positioning

1970s - 1980s: The Product Era

  • Focus on the Product: During this period, positioning was mainly centered on the features and benefits of the product. Companies sought to highlight what made their product unique compared to the competition.
  • Example: The Avis "We Try Harder" campaign in 1962, which positioned the company as a more hardworking alternative to the market leader, Hertz.

1990s: The Consumer Era

  • Focus on the Consumer: Over time, companies began to understand that positioning should focus on the perceptions and needs of the consumer, not just the product features.
  • Market Segmentation: It became common to segment the market into different groups of consumers with specific needs and desires.
  • Example: Nike's "Just Do It" campaign that sold not only shoes but also a lifestyle and attitude.

2000s: The Digital Era

  • Internet and Social Media: The advent of the Internet and social media transformed positioning, allowing brands to interact directly with consumers and adjust their strategies in real-time.
  • SEO and SEM: Search engine optimization (SEO) and search engine marketing (SEM) became crucial components of positioning strategies.
  • Example: Red Bull's content strategy, which positions itself as a brand associated with extreme sports and adventures.

2010s Onwards: The Experience Era

  • Customer Experience: The focus has shifted towards creating memorable experiences for customers. Brands aim not only to sell a product but also to offer a complete experience.
  • Personalization: Advanced technologies allow companies to personalize their offerings and communications, creating a stronger connection with consumers.
  • Example: Amazon, which uses advanced algorithms to personalize product recommendations and improve the shopping experience.

Comparison of Positioning Strategies

| Decade | Main Focus | Notable Example | |------------------|-------------------------|--------------------------------| | 1960-1970 | Product Features | Avis "We Try Harder" | | 1990 | Consumer Needs | Nike "Just Do It" | | 2000 | Digital Interaction | Red Bull and extreme sports | | 2010 onwards | Customer Experience | Amazon and personalization |

Conclusion

Positioning has evolved from a focus on product features to centering on the customer experience. This evolution reflects changes in the market and available technologies and underscores the importance of continuously adapting to stay relevant. Understanding this history and evolution is crucial for developing effective positioning strategies in today's environment.
