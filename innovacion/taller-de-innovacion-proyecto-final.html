Introduction

In this section of the course, students will apply the concepts and techniques learned in previous sessions to develop a final innovation project. This project will allow participants to experience the complete innovation process, from ideation to implementation and presentation.

Final Project Objectives

  • Apply innovation methodologies to solve a real problem or improve an existing process.
  • Develop teamwork and collaboration skills.
  • Present and defend an innovative solution before a panel of experts.

Project Phases

1. Problem Identification

  • Problem Definition: Identify a real problem that needs an innovative solution.
  • Research: Conduct preliminary research to understand the context and needs of the problem.

2. Ideation

  • Brainstorming: Generate a wide range of possible ideas to solve the identified problem.
  • Idea Selection: Evaluate and select the most viable and promising ideas.

3. Prototyping

  • Prototype Development: Create a functional prototype of the selected solution.
  • Initial Testing: Conduct preliminary tests to evaluate the effectiveness of the prototype.

4. Implementation

  • Implementation Plan: Develop a detailed plan for the implementation of the solution.
  • Development: Build the final solution based on the prototype and initial tests.

5. Presentation

  • Presentation Preparation: Create a presentation that explains the problem, the proposed solution, and the process followed.
  • Project Defense: Present and defend the solution before a panel of experts and receive feedback.

Project Example

Problem: Improving Internal Communication in a Company

Problem Identification

  • Definition: Internal communication in the company is inefficient, leading to misunderstandings and project delays.
  • Research: Surveys and interviews with employees to identify the main problematic areas.

Ideation

  • Brainstorming: Ideas such as an internal messaging platform, weekly team meetings, and a digital newsletter.
  • Idea Selection: Selection of the internal messaging platform as the most viable solution.

Prototyping

  • Prototype Development: Create a prototype of the platform using tools like Slack or Microsoft Teams.
  • Initial Testing: Implement the prototype in a small department for testing and feedback.

Implementation

  • Implementation Plan: Detailed plan for company-wide implementation, including training and technical support.
  • Development: Configuration and customization of the platform to meet the specific needs of the company.

Presentation

  • Presentation Preparation: Create a presentation that includes the identified problem, the research conducted, the ideation process, the developed prototype, and the implementation plan.
  • Project Defense: Present the solution before a panel of experts and receive feedback.

Conclusion

The final project is an opportunity to apply the knowledge acquired in the Innovation course. Through this project, students will experience the complete innovation cycle, from problem identification to the implementation and presentation of a solution. This process not only reinforces theoretical concepts but also develops essential practical skills for any IT professional.
