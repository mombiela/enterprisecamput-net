In this section of the Keyword Research course, we will learn to identify the relevant topics that will guide our content strategy and search engine optimization (SEO). Identifying relevant topics is crucial to attracting the right audience and improving visibility in search engines.

What are Relevant Topics?

Relevant topics are the concepts and areas of interest that are important to your target audience. Identifying these topics will help you create content that resonates with your users and aligns with their needs and searches.

Importance of Relevant Topics

  • Attract the right audience: By focusing on topics that interest your audience, you increase the likelihood of attracting visitors who are genuinely interested in your content.
  • Improve SEO: Search engines value content that is relevant and useful to users. By identifying and creating content on relevant topics, you improve your ranking in search results.
  • Guide content creation: Having clear relevant topics helps you plan and create content more efficiently and effectively.

How to Identify Relevant Topics

To identify relevant topics, you can follow various methods and tools. Below are some key steps:

1. Know Your Audience

  • Demographics: Age, gender, geographic location, etc.
  • Interests and needs: What problems are they looking to solve? What topics interest them?
  • Online behavior: What type of content do they consume? On which platforms do they spend the most time?

2. Competitor Analysis

  • Identify competitors: Who are your main online competitors?
  • Content analysis: What type of content are they creating? What topics are they covering?
  • Analysis tools: Use tools like SEMrush, Ahrefs, or Moz to analyze your competitors' keywords and content.

3. Use Keyword Research Tools

  • Google Keyword Planner: Free Google tool that allows you to find related keywords and see their search volume.
  • Answer the Public: Generates questions and phrases related to your keywords.
  • Ubersuggest: Provides keyword suggestions and competitor analysis.

4. Trend Analysis

  • Google Trends: Analyzes the popularity of searches over time.
  • Social Media: Observe what topics are being discussed on platforms like Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn.

5. Surveys and Feedback

  • Online surveys: Conduct surveys with your audience to understand their interests and needs.
  • Comments and feedback: Review comments on your blog, social media, and other channels to identify topics of interest.

Practical Example

Suppose you have a blog about "Fitness and Health." Here’s how you could identify relevant topics:

  1. Know your audience:

    • Demographics: Young adults (18-35 years), both men and women, interested in fitness and health.
    • Interests: Exercise routines, nutrition, mental well-being.
    • Behavior: Consume content on Instagram and YouTube.

  2. Competitor analysis:

    • Competitors: Blogs and YouTube channels like "FitnessBlender," "Blogilates," etc.
    • Content: Exercise routines, healthy recipes, wellness tips.
    • Tools: Use Ahrefs to analyze the keywords they are using.

  3. Use keyword research tools:

    • Google Keyword Planner: Search for keywords like "home workout routines," "healthy recipes."
    • Answer the Public: Generate questions like "How to start an exercise routine?"
    • Ubersuggest: Get additional suggestions and analyze the competition.

  4. Trend analysis:

    • Google Trends: See the popularity of terms like "home workout" during the pandemic.
    • Social Media: Observe popular hashtags like #HomeWorkout on Instagram.

  5. Surveys and feedback:

    • Surveys: Ask your followers on Instagram what type of content they would like to see.
    • Comments: Review comments on your posts to identify recurring topics.

Conclusion

Identifying relevant topics is a fundamental step in keyword research. Knowing your audience, analyzing competitors, using research tools, analyzing trends, and gathering feedback will help you identify the topics that truly matter to your users. This will not only improve your content strategy but also increase your visibility in search engines and attract the right audience.
