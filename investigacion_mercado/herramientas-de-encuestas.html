Introduction

Survey tools are essential in market research as they allow data collection directly from consumers. This topic will cover the main survey tools available, their features, advantages and disadvantages, as well as practical examples of how to use them.

Main Survey Tools

1. Google Forms

Google Forms is a free and easy-to-use tool that allows you to create surveys and collect responses in real-time.

  • Features:

    • Integration with Google Sheets.
    • Variety of question types (multiple choice, text, linear scale, etc.).
    • Theme and color customization.
    • Ability to add images and videos.

  • Advantages:

    • Free and accessible.
    • Easy to share and distribute.
    • Integration with other Google tools.

  • Disadvantages:

    • Limited functionalities compared to paid tools.
    • Fewer advanced analysis options.

  • Example of use:

    ### Example of Survey in Google Forms
1. Open Google Forms and select "Start a new form".
2. Add a multiple-choice question:
   - Question: "What is your favorite product?"
   - Options: "Product A", "Product B", "Product C"
3. Customize the theme and share the link with participants.

2. SurveyMonkey

SurveyMonkey is a popular tool for creating surveys with advanced functionalities.

  • Features:

    • Predefined survey templates.
    • Advanced analysis and reporting.
    • Integration with multiple applications (Salesforce, Mailchimp, etc.).
    • Customization options and question logic.

  • Advantages:

    • Advanced functionalities for data analysis.
    • Wide range of integrations.
    • Support and educational resources.

  • Disadvantages:

    • Costs associated with advanced functionalities.
    • Steeper learning curve.

  • Example of use:

    ### Example of Survey in SurveyMonkey
1. Log in to SurveyMonkey and select "Create Survey".
2. Choose a template or start from scratch.
3. Add a Likert scale question:
   - Question: "How satisfied are you with our service?"
   - Options: "Very dissatisfied", "Dissatisfied", "Neutral", "Satisfied", "Very satisfied"
4. Set up question logic to customize the respondent's experience.
5. Distribute the survey via email or social media.

3. Typeform

Typeform is known for its interactive and visually appealing surveys.

  • Features:

    • Interactive and user-friendly design.
    • Variety of question types.
    • Integration with tools like Zapier, Slack, and Google Sheets.
    • Real-time data analysis.

  • Advantages:

    • Attractive user experience.
    • Easy to use and set up.
    • Good customization options.

  • Disadvantages:

    • Advanced functionalities require a subscription.
    • Fewer analysis options compared to SurveyMonkey.

  • Example of use:

    ### Example of Survey in Typeform
1. Sign up for Typeform and select "Create new Typeform".
2. Choose a template or start from scratch.
3. Add a text question:
   - Question: "What improvements would you like to see in our product?"
4. Customize the design and appearance of the survey.
5. Share the survey link with your audience.

Conclusion

Survey tools are fundamental for market research, allowing valuable data collection efficiently. Google Forms, SurveyMonkey, and Typeform are some of the most popular options, each with its own advantages and disadvantages. The choice of the right tool will depend on the specific needs of your research and the resources available.
