Introduction

In this section, you will learn how to configure permissions and access in Google Data Studio. Proper permission configuration is crucial to ensure that only the right people have access to your reports and data. This not only protects sensitive information but also facilitates efficient collaboration.

Types of Permissions in Google Data Studio

Google Data Studio offers several levels of permissions that you can assign to users:

  • Owner: Has full control over the report or data source. Can share, edit, and delete the resource.
  • Editor: Can modify the report or data source but cannot delete it or change ownership.
  • Viewer: Can only view the report or data source, without the ability to make modifications.

Configuring Permissions for Reports

Assigning Permissions to a Report

To assign permissions to a report in Google Data Studio, follow these steps:

  1. Open the report in Google Data Studio.
  2. Click the "Share" button in the upper right corner.
  3. In the sharing dialog, enter the email addresses of the people you want to grant access to.
  4. Select the appropriate permission level (Owner, Editor, Viewer).
  5. Click "Send".

Example of Permission Configuration

1. Open the report "Annual Sales 2023".
2. Click "Share".
3. Enter the email "jane.doe@example.com".
4. Select "Editor".
5. Click "Send".

Configuring Permissions for Data Sources

Assigning Permissions to a Data Source

To assign permissions to a data source, follow these steps:

  1. Open the data source in Google Data Studio.
  2. Click the "Share" button in the upper right corner.
  3. In the sharing dialog, enter the email addresses of the people you want to grant access to.
  4. Select the appropriate permission level (Owner, Editor, Viewer).
  5. Click "Send".

Example of Permission Configuration for a Data Source

1. Open the data source "Customer Database".
2. Click "Share".
3. Enter the email "john.smith@example.com".
4. Select "Viewer".
5. Click "Send".

Best Practices for Permission Configuration

  • Principle of Least Privilege: Grant only the permissions necessary to perform assigned tasks.
  • Periodic Review: Regularly review and update permissions to ensure that only the right people have access.
  • Documentation: Keep a record of who has access to which resources and why.

Conclusion

Proper configuration of permissions and access in Google Data Studio is essential to protect your data and facilitate collaboration. Make sure to understand the different levels of permissions and follow best practices to keep your reports and data sources secure and well-managed.
