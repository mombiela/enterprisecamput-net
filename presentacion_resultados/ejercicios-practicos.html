Introduction

In this section, a series of practical exercises will be presented designed to strengthen your skills in presenting results. These exercises will help you apply the concepts learned in real situations and improve your ability to communicate information effectively and persuasively.

Exercise 1: Preparation of the Presentation

Objective

Develop a clear and logical structure for a results presentation.

Instructions

  1. Select a topic: Choose a recent project or report in your work.
  2. Define the objective: What do you want to achieve with this presentation? Inform, persuade, motivate?
  3. Identify your audience: Who are they? What level of knowledge do they have about the topic?
  4. Structure your presentation:
    • Introduction: Context and objective.
    • Development: Data and analysis.
    • Conclusion: Results and recommendations.

Example

  • Topic: Results of the last sales quarter.
  • Objective: Inform the board of directors about performance and propose improvement strategies.
  • Audience: Board of directors with advanced knowledge in finance.

Exercise 2: Slide Design

Objective

Create visually attractive and effective slides.

Instructions

  1. Select a tool: PowerPoint, Google Slides, etc.
  2. Design the slides:
    • Use professional templates.
    • Include charts and tables to visualize data.
    • Limit the amount of text per slide.
    • Use consistent colors and fonts.

Example

  • Slide 1: Title and objective of the presentation.
  • Slide 2: Bar chart showing quarterly sales.
  • Slide 3: Comparative table of sales by region.

Exercise 3: Presentation Rehearsal

Objective

Practice delivering the presentation to improve fluency and confidence.

Instructions

  1. Rehearse out loud: Practice your presentation several times.
  2. Time your presentation: Ensure you stick to the allotted time.
  3. Record your rehearsal: Review the recording to identify areas for improvement.
  4. Request feedback: Present to a colleague and ask for feedback.

Example

  • First practice: 20 minutes, exceeds the allotted time.
  • Adjustments: Simplify some slides and reduce unnecessary details.
  • Second practice: 15 minutes, within the allotted time.

Exercise 4: Handling Questions and Answers

Objective

Prepare to respond to audience questions effectively.

Instructions

  1. Anticipate questions: Think of possible questions the audience might ask.
  2. Prepare answers: Develop clear and concise answers.
  3. Practice with a colleague: Simulate a Q&A session.

Example

  • Anticipated question: Why did sales in the northern region decrease?
  • Prepared answer: "Sales decreased due to aggressive competition and lack of promotion. We propose increasing the marketing budget in that region."

Conclusion

These practical exercises provide you with a guide to improve your skills in presenting results. By following these steps, you will be able to structure, design, and deliver more effective and professional presentations. Remember that constant practice and feedback are key to developing your competencies in this area.
