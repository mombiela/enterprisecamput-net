Introduction

The creation of support materials is a fundamental part of presenting results. These materials not only complement the information presented but also help clarify and emphasize key points. In this section, you will learn to develop effective support materials that enhance your audience's understanding and retention of information.

Types of Support Materials

  • Visual Presentations (PowerPoint, Keynote, etc.)
  • Written Documents (Reports, Executive Summaries)
  • Graphs and Tables
  • Videos and Animations
  • Prototypes and Physical Models

Key Elements in Creating Support Materials

Visual Presentations

  • Clarity and Simplicity
    • Use a clean design and avoid excessive text.
    • Use images and graphics to illustrate key points.
  • Consistency
    • Maintain a coherent visual style (colors, fonts, formats).
  • Interactivity
    • Incorporate interactive elements if possible (hyperlinks, live polls).

Written Documents

  • Clear Structure
    • Well-defined introduction, development, and conclusion.
  • Precise and Concise Language
    • Avoid jargon and unnecessary technical terms.
  • References and Sources
    • Cite all sources of information used.

Graphs and Tables

  • Relevance
    • Ensure that each graph or table adds value to the presentation.
  • Clarity
    • Clear and legible labels and legends.
  • Comparison and Contrast
    • Use tables to effectively compare data.

| Type of Graph | Main Use | Example | |---------------|----------|---------| | Bar Graph | Compare quantities | Monthly sales by region | | Line Graph | Show trends over time | Annual revenue evolution | | Pie Chart | Represent proportions | Budget distribution |

Videos and Animations

  • Appropriate Duration
    • Keep videos short and to the point.
  • Production Quality
    • Ensure that audio and video are of high quality.
  • Content Relevance
    • The content should be directly relevant to the points you are presenting.

Prototypes and Physical Models

  • Functionality
    • Prototypes should be functional and clearly demonstrate the concept.
  • Accessibility
    • Ensure that all audience members can see and understand the prototype.

Practical Exercises

  1. Develop a Visual Presentation

    • Create a 5-slide presentation on a topic relevant to your work.
    • Incorporate at least one graph and one image.

  2. Write an Executive Summary

    • Draft a one-page executive summary of a recent project.
    • Include a comparative table of the results obtained.

  3. Create a Relevant Graph

    • Choose a data set from your work and create a graph that represents it.
    • Ensure that the graph is clear and easy to understand.

Conclusion

The creation of effective support materials is essential for a successful presentation of results. By using a variety of materials and ensuring that each one is well-designed and relevant, you can significantly enhance your audience's understanding and retention of information. Regularly practice creating these materials to perfect your skills and increase the impact of your presentations.
