Introduction

In this section of the Google Ads course, you will learn how to set up a display campaign. Display campaigns allow you to show visual ads on a wide network of websites and apps, which can help you reach a broader and more diverse audience. We will cover the essential steps to effectively set up your display campaign.

Steps to Set Up a Display Campaign

1. Create a New Campaign

To start a display campaign in Google Ads, follow these steps:

  1. Access your Google Ads account.
  2. Click the "+" button to create a new campaign.
  3. Select "New campaign".

2. Choose the Campaign Objective

Google Ads allows you to select a specific objective for your campaign. Common objectives include:

  • Sales: Generate online, in-app, phone, or in-store sales.
  • Leads: Attract potential customers and other conversions.
  • Website traffic: Increase visits to your website.
  • Product and brand consideration: Encourage people to explore your products or services.
  • Brand awareness and reach: Reach a broad audience and increase brand awareness.

3. Select the Campaign Type

After choosing the objective, select the "Display" campaign type.

4. Configure Campaign Details

Complete the following fields:

  • Campaign name: Assign a descriptive name to your campaign.
  • Locations: Select the geographic locations where you want your ads to be shown.
  • Languages: Choose the languages spoken by your target audience.
  • Bidding strategy: Select a bidding strategy that aligns with your objectives, such as "Maximize clicks" or "Target CPA".
  • Budget: Set the daily or total budget for the campaign.

5. Create Ad Groups

Within your display campaign, you can create multiple ad groups. Each ad group can have its own set of ads and targeting settings.

  • Ad group name: Assign a name to your ad group.
  • Targeting: Define the audience you want to reach. You can target by:
    • Demographics: Age, gender, parental status, etc.
    • Interests and habits: Interests, affinities, etc.
    • Custom audiences: Audiences based on your own data or remarketing lists.
    • Placements: Specify websites, apps, or specific categories.

6. Create Display Ads

Create visual ads that will be shown on the Google Display Network. You can use different formats, such as:

  • Image ads: Upload static images.
  • Responsive display ads: Google automatically adjusts the size, appearance, and format of your ads to fit available ad spaces.

Example Code for a Responsive Display Ad:

<div class="ad-container">
  <img src="https://example.com/image.jpg" alt="Example Ad">
  <div class="ad-text">
    <h2>Discover Our Products!</h2>
    <p>Visit our online store and find the best deals.</p>
    <a href="https://example.com" class="cta-button">Shop Now</a>
  </div>
</div>

7. Review and Launch the Campaign

Before launching your campaign, review all the details to ensure everything is set up correctly. Once you are satisfied, click "Publish" to launch your display campaign.

Conclusion

Setting up a display campaign in Google Ads may seem complex at first, but by following these steps, you can create effective campaigns that reach your target audience. Always remember to review and adjust your campaigns based on performance to maximize your results. Good luck with your display campaigns!
