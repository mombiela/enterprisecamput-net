Introduction

Google Analytics and Google Ads are two powerful tools that, when used together, can provide a comprehensive view of your advertising campaign performance. This topic will teach you how to link both platforms and leverage their functionalities to optimize your digital marketing strategies.

Linking Google Analytics with Google Ads

To start using Google Analytics with Google Ads, you first need to link both accounts. This process is essential for sharing data between the two platforms.

Steps to link the accounts

  1. Access Google Analytics:

    • Log in to your Google Analytics account.
    • Go to the "Admin" section (gear icon at the bottom left).

  2. Select the property:

    • In the "Property" column, select the Google Analytics property you want to link.

  3. Google Ads setup:

    • In the "Property" column, click on "Google Ads Linking".
    • Click on "New link group".

  4. Select the Google Ads account:

    • Select the Google Ads account you want to link and click "Continue".

  5. Configure the linking:

    • Enable the "Linking" option and click "Next".
    • Review the settings and click "Submit".

Importing goals and metrics

Once the accounts are linked, you can import goals and metrics from Google Analytics to Google Ads to measure your campaign performance.

Import goals

  1. Access Google Ads:

    • Log in to your Google Ads account.
    • Go to the "Tools & settings" section and select "Conversions".

  2. Import from Google Analytics:

    • Click on the "+" button and select "Import".
    • Select "Google Analytics" and click "Continue".

  3. Select the goals:

    • Select the goals you want to import and click "Import and continue".

Import metrics

  • Key metrics:
    • Sessions
    • Bounce rate
    • Average session duration
    • Pages per session

These metrics will help you understand how users interact with your website after clicking on your ads.

Creating custom reports

Google Analytics allows you to create custom reports that can be very useful for analyzing the performance of your Google Ads campaigns.

Steps to create a custom report

  1. Access the "Customization" section:

    • In Google Analytics, go to the "Customization" section in the left menu.

  2. Create a new report:

    • Click on "Custom Reports" and then on "New Custom Report".

  3. Configure the report:

    • Assign a name to your report.
    • Add the metrics and dimensions you want to analyze (e.g., "Clicks", "Cost", "Conversions").

  4. Save and view the report:

    • Click "Save" to create the report.
    • Access your custom report from the "Custom Reports" section.

Practical examples

Example code for conversion tracking

To track conversions on your website, you can use the following Google Ads tracking code:

<!-- Google Ads conversion tracking code -->
<script>
  gtag('event', 'conversion', {
      'send_to': 'AW-CONVERSION_ID/label',
      'value': 1.0,
      'currency': 'USD'
  });
</script>

Example of a custom report

Suppose you want to create a custom report to analyze the performance of your Google Ads campaigns in terms of conversions and cost per conversion. Configure your report as follows:

  • Metrics:

    • Conversions
    • Cost per conversion

  • Dimensions:

    • Campaign
    • Ad group

Conclusion

Integrating Google Analytics with Google Ads is a powerful strategy to get a complete view of your advertising campaign performance. By linking both platforms, importing goals and metrics, and creating custom reports, you can optimize your digital marketing strategies and make informed decisions based on accurate data.
