Introduction

Properly setting up your Google Ads account is crucial for the success of your advertising campaigns. In this section, you will learn how to create and set up your Google Ads account from scratch, ensuring that all initial settings are optimized for your marketing goals.

Steps for Initial Setup

1. Account Creation

To start using Google Ads, you first need to create an account. Follow these steps:

  1. Access Google Ads: Go to ads.google.com and click on "Get started now".
  2. Sign in with your Google account: If you don't have a Google account, you will need to create one.
  3. Set up your account: Enter basic information such as your business name, website, and time zone.

2. Billing Setup

Properly setting up billing is essential to avoid interruptions in your campaigns. Here are the steps:

  1. Select the country and time zone: Make sure these details are correct, as they cannot be changed later.
  2. Choose the payment method: You can opt for automatic, manual, or monthly payments.
  3. Enter payment information: Provide details of your credit card, bank account, or any other payment method available in your region.

3. Defining Campaign Objectives

Before creating your first campaign, it is important to define your objectives. Google Ads allows you to select from several objectives, such as:

  • Sales: To increase online, app, phone, or in-store sales.
  • Leads: To obtain potential customers and other conversions.
  • Website traffic: To attract people to your website.
  • Product and brand consideration: To encourage people to explore your products or services.
  • Brand awareness and reach: To increase brand awareness.

4. Initial Campaign Setup

Once you have defined your objectives, you can proceed to set up your first campaign:

  1. Select the campaign type: Google Ads offers several types of campaigns, such as Search, Display, Video, Shopping, and App.
  2. Define the daily budget: Set how much you are willing to spend per day.
  3. Set up targeting: Define the geographic location, language, and audience you want to reach.
  4. Set up bidding: Choose a bidding strategy that aligns with your objectives, such as CPC (cost per click), CPA (cost per acquisition), or ROAS (return on ad spend).

Example of Setting Up a Search Campaign

Below is an example of how to set up a basic search campaign:

1. Access your Google Ads account.
2. Click on "New campaign".
3. Select "Sales" as the campaign objective.
4. Choose "Search Network" as the campaign type.
5. Define the daily budget: $50.
6. Set up targeting:
   - Location: United States
   - Language: English
7. Set up bidding:
   - Bidding strategy: Maximize clicks
   - Bid limit: $2 per click
8. Create ad groups and add relevant keywords.
9. Write the ads and provide the destination URLs.
10. Review and launch the campaign.

Conclusion

The initial setup of your Google Ads account is a fundamental step for the success of your campaigns. Make sure to follow each step carefully, from account creation to billing setup and objective definition. With a solid foundation, you will be better prepared to optimize your campaigns and achieve your marketing goals.
