Introduction

In video game development, goal-based behaviors are a fundamental technique for creating non-playable characters (NPCs) that act intelligently and coherently. This approach allows NPCs to make decisions and perform actions that bring them closer to achieving certain goals, thereby enhancing the player's experience.

Key Concepts

  • Goal: A specific target that an NPC tries to achieve.
  • Action: A task or behavior that an NPC can perform to get closer to its goal.
  • Planning: The process of determining which actions should be taken to achieve a goal.
  • Goal Hierarchy: Structure that organizes goals into levels of priority.

Practical Example

Suppose we have an NPC in an adventure game whose goal is to collect resources and take them to a storage.

  1. Define Goals:

    • Collect resource.
    • Take resource to storage.

  2. Define Actions:

    • Move to the resource location.
    • Pick up the resource.
    • Move to storage.
    • Deposit the resource.

  3. Planning:

    • Create a plan that sequences the necessary actions to achieve the goals.
# Defining goals
collect_resource = Objective("Collect Resource")
deliver_resource = Objective("Deliver Resource")

# Defining actions
move_to_resource = Action("Move to Resource", cost=1)
pick_up_resource = Action("Pick Up Resource", cost=1)
move_to_storage = Action("Move to Storage", cost=1)
deposit_resource = Action("Deposit Resource", cost=1)

# Planning
planner = Planner()
planner.add_action(move_to_resource)
planner.add_action(pick_up_resource)
planner.add_action(move_to_storage)
planner.add_action(deposit_resource)

# Create a plan to achieve the goals
plan = planner.create_plan(collect_resource)

Conclusion

Goal-based behaviors are a powerful technique for developing intelligent NPCs in video games. By defining clear goals, specific actions, and a planning process, developers can create characters that act coherently and realistically, enhancing immersion and gameplay.
