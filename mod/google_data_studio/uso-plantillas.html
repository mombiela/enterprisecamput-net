Introduction

In this section, you will learn how to use templates in Google Data Studio to create reports and dashboards efficiently. Templates allow you to reuse predefined designs and structures, saving time and ensuring consistency in your reports.

What are templates in Google Data Studio?

Templates in Google Data Studio are predefined designs that you can use as a starting point for your reports. These templates can include charts, tables, filters, and other visual elements that facilitate the creation of customized reports.

Benefits of using templates

  • Time-saving: You don't need to start from scratch every time you create a report.
  • Consistency: Maintain a uniform design across all your reports.
  • Ease of use: Templates are easy to customize according to your specific needs.
  • Best practices: Templates are often designed following best data visualization practices.

How to access templates in Google Data Studio

To access templates in Google Data Studio, follow these steps:

  1. Open Google Data Studio.
  2. Click on the "Create" button in the upper left corner.
  3. Select "Report" from the dropdown menu.
  4. In the pop-up window, select "Templates" to see the available options.

Example of using a template

Let's create a report using a predefined template.

  1. Select a template: Choose a template that fits your needs. For example, select the "Marketing Performance Report" template.
  2. Connect your data: Connect the template to your data source, such as Google Analytics or Google Sheets.
  3. Customize the report: Modify the charts, tables, and other visual elements to reflect your specific data.
### Example code to connect a data source

// Connect Google Analytics var dataSource = DataStudioApp.createCommunityConnector() .newConfig() .setAuthType(DataStudioApp.AuthType.USER_PASS) .setDataSourceUrl('https://analytics.google.com/analytics/web/') .build();

## Customizing templates
Once you have selected and connected your template, you can customize it to fit your specific needs.

- **Modify charts**: Change the chart type, colors, and labels.
- **Add filters**: Add filters to allow users to interact with the data.
- **Update data**: Ensure that the data is updated automatically or manually as needed.

## Comparison of predefined and customized templates

| Feature                       | Predefined Templates     | Customized Templates       |
|-------------------------------|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| Setup time                    | Fast                    | Moderate                  |
| Flexibility                   | Limited                 | High                      |
| Consistency                   | High                    | Variable                  |
| Knowledge requirement         | Basic                   | Intermediate/Advanced     |

## Conclusion
Using templates in Google Data Studio is an excellent way to save time and maintain consistency in your reports. Whether you use predefined or customized templates, these tools will allow you to create visually appealing and functional reports more efficiently. Always remember to customize the templates to fit your specific needs and ensure that the data is updated correctly.

You are now ready to start using templates in Google Data Studio and improve the efficiency of your reports!
