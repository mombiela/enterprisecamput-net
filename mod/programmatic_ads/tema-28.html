Introduction

Programmatic advertising is a dynamic and constantly evolving field that involves the automated buying and selling of online advertising spaces. This process is subject to various regulations and norms aimed at protecting user privacy, ensuring transparency, and maintaining the integrity of the advertising ecosystem. In this section, we will explore the main regulations and norms that affect programmatic advertising.

Main Regulations and Norms

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

The GDPR is a European Union regulation that establishes strict guidelines on the collection, storage, and use of personal data.

  • Scope: Affects all companies that process personal data of EU residents, regardless of the company's location.
  • Key Principles:
    • Consent: Users must give explicit consent for the collection of their data.
    • Right to be Forgotten: Users can request the deletion of their personal data.
    • Transparency: Companies must clearly inform how data is used.
  • Implementation Example: 
    // Example code to obtain cookie consent
document.getElementById('consentButton').addEventListener('click', function() {
  document.cookie = "user_consent=true; path=/; max-age=" + 60*60*24*365;
});

CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)

The CCPA is a data privacy law that grants California residents specific rights over their personal data.

  • Scope: Companies doing business in California and meeting certain income or data volume criteria.
  • Consumer Rights:
    • Right to Know: Consumers can request information about the data collected.
    • Right to Delete: Consumers can request the deletion of their data.
    • Right to Opt-Out: Consumers can opt-out of the sale of their data.
  • Implementation Example: 
    // Example code to handle data deletion requests
function handleDeleteRequest(userId) {
  // Logic to delete user data
  deleteUserFromDatabase(userId);
  alert('Your data has been deleted.');
}

COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act)

COPPA regulates the collection of personal data from children under 13 years old in the United States.

  • Scope: Websites and online services directed at children under 13 years old.
  • Key Requirements:
    • Parental Consent: Verifiable parental consent must be obtained before collecting data.
    • Privacy Policy: There must be a clear and accessible privacy policy.
  • Implementation Example: 
    // Example code to verify parental consent
function verifyParentalConsent(parentEmail) {
  // Send a verification email to parents
  sendVerificationEmail(parentEmail);
}

Comparison of Regulations

| Regulation | Scope | Key Rights | Implementation Example | |------------|-------|------------|------------------------| | GDPR | European Union | Consent, Right to be Forgotten, Transparency | Code to obtain cookie consent | | CCPA | California, USA | Right to Know, Right to Delete, Right to Opt-Out | Code to handle data deletion requests | | COPPA | USA (under 13 years old) | Parental Consent, Privacy Policy | Code to verify parental consent |

Conclusion

Regulations and norms in programmatic advertising are essential to protect user privacy and ensure transparency in data handling. It is crucial for professionals in this field to understand and comply with these regulations to avoid penalties and maintain user trust. By implementing appropriate practices and staying informed about changes in laws, companies can operate ethically and effectively in the programmatic advertising ecosystem.
