Introduction

In this section, we will explore the next steps you can take to continue improving your skills in Google Ads and provide additional resources to deepen your knowledge. This course has provided you with a solid foundation, but continuous learning is key to staying updated and maximizing the performance of your campaigns.

Next Steps

1. Experiment with Different Types of Campaigns

  • Search Campaigns: Ideal for capturing users who are already searching for specific products or services.
  • Display Campaigns: Useful for increasing your brand's visibility through graphic ads on associated websites.
  • Video Campaigns: Perfect for storytelling and capturing attention on platforms like YouTube.
  • Shopping Campaigns: Specific for e-commerce, showing products directly in search results.

2. Optimize Your Existing Campaigns

  • Keyword Review: Use tools like the Keyword Planner to find new opportunities.
  • A/B Testing: Test different versions of ads to see which performs better.
  • Bid Adjustments: Modify bids based on the performance of different devices, locations, and times.

3. Learn to Use Google Analytics

  • Integration with Google Ads: Link your accounts to get more detailed data on your campaign performance.
  • Conversion Analysis: Track the actions users take after interacting with your ads.
  • Audience Segmentation: Create custom audiences based on user behavior.

Example Code for Conversion Tracking

Below is an example of how to implement conversion tracking on your website using Google Ads code:

<!-- Global site tag (gtag.js) - Google Ads: YOUR_CONVERSION_ID -->
<script async src="https://www.googletagmanager.com/gtag/js?id=AW-YOUR_CONVERSION_ID"></script>
<script>
  window.dataLayer = window.dataLayer || [];
  function gtag(){dataLayer.push(arguments);}
  gtag('js', new Date());

  gtag('config', 'AW-YOUR_CONVERSION_ID');
</script>

<!-- Event snippet for Conversion Page -->
<script>
  gtag('event', 'conversion', {'send_to': 'AW-YOUR_CONVERSION_ID/YOUR_LABEL'});
</script>

Code Explanation

  • Global site tag: This script should be on all pages of your website.
  • Event snippet: This script should be on the page where the conversion takes place (e.g., the thank you page after a purchase).
