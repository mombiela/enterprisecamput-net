Introduction

Social media monitoring is an essential tool in crisis management. It allows organizations to detect emerging problems, measure public sentiment, and respond quickly to adverse situations. In this module, you will learn to use various tools and techniques to effectively monitor social media.

Importance of Social Media Monitoring

  • Early Crisis Detection: Identify problems before they become major crises.
  • Measurement of Public Sentiment: Evaluate how the public feels about your brand or situation.
  • Quick Response: Act quickly to mitigate damage and control the narrative.
  • Competitor Analysis: Observe how other organizations handle similar situations.

Monitoring Tools

Free Tools

  • Google Alerts: Set up alerts to receive notifications about specific mentions.
  • TweetDeck: Monitor Twitter in real-time with customizable columns.
  • Social Mention: Track mentions across various platforms and measure sentiment.

Paid Tools

  • Hootsuite: Comprehensive platform for managing and monitoring multiple social networks.
  • Brandwatch: Offers advanced analytics and real-time monitoring.
  • Sprout Social: Combines monitoring, analytics, and social media management.

Monitoring Techniques

  • Keywords: Identify and track relevant keywords for your brand or situation.
  • Hashtags: Monitor popular hashtags related to your industry or crisis.
  • Direct Mentions: Observe direct mentions of your account or brand.
  • Sentiment: Use tools that analyze the sentiment of mentions (positive, negative, neutral).

Practical Example

Setting Up Google Alerts

  1. Go to Google Alerts.
  2. Enter the keywords you want to monitor.
  3. Set the frequency and source options.
  4. Receive alerts in your email.
Example keywords:
- "Company Name"
- "Product X problem"
- "Competitor Y"

Using TweetDeck

  1. Access TweetDeck.
  2. Log in with your Twitter account.
  3. Add columns for keywords, hashtags, and direct mentions.
  4. Monitor in real-time and respond quickly.
Example columns in TweetDeck:
- Column 1: "Company Name"
- Column 2: "#CrisisHashtag"
- Column 3: "Direct Mentions"

Conclusion

Social media monitoring is a crucial practice in crisis management. By using the right tools and techniques, you can detect problems early, measure public sentiment, and respond effectively. Implementing a robust monitoring system will allow you to handle adverse situations with greater control and efficiency.
