Resources for Content Distribution
Next ►

In this section, we will explore various resources that can help you effectively distribute your content to reach your target audience. Content distribution is a critical component of a successful content marketing strategy, as it ensures that your valuable content is seen by the right people at the right time.

Key Concepts

  1. Content Distribution Channels: The platforms and methods used to share your content with your audience.
  2. Paid, Owned, and Earned Media: The three main types of media used in content distribution.
  3. Content Amplification: Techniques to increase the reach and impact of your content.

Types of Content Distribution Channels

  1. Owned Media

Owned media refers to the channels that you control. These include:

  • Website/Blog: Your primary platform for publishing content.
  • Email Newsletters: Regular updates sent directly to subscribers.
  • Social Media Profiles: Your business's accounts on platforms like Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, etc.

  1. Earned Media

Earned media is the exposure you gain through word-of-mouth. This includes:

  • Social Shares: When users share your content on their own social media profiles.
  • Mentions: When other websites or influencers mention your content.
  • Reviews and Testimonials: Positive feedback from customers.

  1. Paid Media

Paid media involves paying to promote your content. This includes:

  • Pay-Per-Click (PPC) Advertising: Ads on search engines or social media.
  • Sponsored Content: Paying influencers or other websites to feature your content.
  • Display Ads: Banner ads on various websites.

Tools for Content Distribution

  1. Social Media Management Tools

These tools help you schedule, publish, and analyze your social media posts.

  • Hootsuite: Allows you to manage multiple social media accounts from one dashboard.
  • Buffer: Helps you schedule posts across various social media platforms.
  • Sprout Social: Provides social media management, analytics, and engagement tools.

  1. Email Marketing Tools

These tools help you create and send email newsletters.

  • Mailchimp: A popular email marketing service with a variety of templates and automation features.
  • Constant Contact: Offers email marketing, social media marketing, and other online marketing tools.
  • SendinBlue: Provides email marketing, SMS marketing, and chat functionalities.

  1. Content Discovery and Sharing Tools

These tools help you find and share relevant content.

  • BuzzSumo: Allows you to discover popular content and influencers in your industry.
  • Pocket: Helps you save articles and videos for later viewing and sharing.
  • Feedly: An RSS feed reader that helps you stay updated with the latest content from your favorite sources.

  1. Analytics Tools

These tools help you measure the performance of your content distribution efforts.

  • Google Analytics: Tracks website traffic and user behavior.
  • HubSpot: Provides comprehensive marketing analytics and reporting.
  • Kissmetrics: Offers detailed insights into user behavior and conversion tracking.

Practical Exercise

Exercise: Creating a Content Distribution Plan

  1. Identify Your Channels: List the owned, earned, and paid media channels you will use.
  2. Set Goals: Define what you want to achieve with your content distribution (e.g., increase website traffic, generate leads).
  3. Create a Schedule: Plan when and where you will distribute your content.
  4. Measure and Adjust: Use analytics tools to track the performance of your content and make necessary adjustments.

Example Solution

  1. Channels:

    • Owned: Company blog, email newsletter, LinkedIn profile.
    • Earned: Social shares, mentions by industry influencers.
    • Paid: Facebook Ads, Google AdWords.

  2. Goals:

    • Increase website traffic by 20% in the next three months.
    • Generate 50 new leads per month.

  3. Schedule:

    • Blog posts: Publish every Monday and Thursday.
    • Email newsletter: Send every Wednesday.
    • Social media posts: Share new content on LinkedIn and Twitter daily.
    • Paid ads: Run Facebook Ads campaign for one month.

  4. Measure and Adjust:

    • Use Google Analytics to track website traffic.
    • Monitor social media engagement with Hootsuite.
    • Analyze email open rates and click-through rates with Mailchimp.

Conclusion

Effective content distribution is essential for maximizing the reach and impact of your content marketing efforts. By leveraging a mix of owned, earned, and paid media channels, and utilizing various tools for management and analytics, you can ensure that your content reaches your target audience and drives meaningful results. Remember to continuously measure and adjust your strategy based on performance data to optimize your content distribution efforts.
