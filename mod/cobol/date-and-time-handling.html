Date and Time Handling in COBOL
Advanced File Handling ►

Handling dates and times is a crucial aspect of many programming tasks, especially in business applications where COBOL is widely used. This section will cover the basics of date and time handling in COBOL, progressing to more advanced topics.

Introduction to Date and Time in COBOL

  • COBOL does not have built-in date and time data types like some modern programming languages.
  • Dates and times are typically handled using numeric or alphanumeric data types.
  • The ACCEPT statement is often used to get the current date and time from the system.

Basic Date Handling

Getting the Current Date

To get the current date, you can use the ACCEPT statement with the DATE or DATE YYYYMMDD format.

IDENTIFICATION DIVISION.
PROGRAM-ID. GetCurrentDate.

DATA DIVISION.
WORKING-STORAGE SECTION.
01 CURRENT-DATE.
   05 YEAR  PIC 9(4).
   05 MONTH PIC 9(2).
   05 DAY   PIC 9(2).

PROCEDURE DIVISION.
    ACCEPT CURRENT-DATE FROM DATE YYYYMMDD.
    DISPLAY 'Current Date: ' YEAR '-' MONTH '-' DAY.
    STOP RUN.

Formatting Dates

Dates can be formatted by manipulating the individual components (year, month, day).

IDENTIFICATION DIVISION.
PROGRAM-ID. FormatDate.

DATA DIVISION.
WORKING-STORAGE SECTION.
01 CURRENT-DATE.
   05 YEAR  PIC 9(4).
   05 MONTH PIC 9(2).
   05 DAY   PIC 9(2).
01 FORMATTED-DATE PIC X(10).

PROCEDURE DIVISION.
    ACCEPT CURRENT-DATE FROM DATE YYYYMMDD.
    MOVE YEAR TO FORMATTED-DATE (1:4).
    MOVE '-' TO FORMATTED-DATE (5:5).
    MOVE MONTH TO FORMATTED-DATE (6:7).
    MOVE '-' TO FORMATTED-DATE (8:8).
    MOVE DAY TO FORMATTED-DATE (9:10).
    DISPLAY 'Formatted Date: ' FORMATTED-DATE.
    STOP RUN.

Basic Time Handling

Getting the Current Time

To get the current time, use the ACCEPT statement with the TIME format.

IDENTIFICATION DIVISION.
PROGRAM-ID. GetCurrentTime.

DATA DIVISION.
WORKING-STORAGE SECTION.
01 CURRENT-TIME.
   05 HOURS   PIC 9(2).
   05 MINUTES PIC 9(2).
   05 SECONDS PIC 9(2).

PROCEDURE DIVISION.
    ACCEPT CURRENT-TIME FROM TIME.
    DISPLAY 'Current Time: ' HOURS ':' MINUTES ':' SECONDS.
    STOP RUN.

Formatting Time

Similar to dates, times can be formatted by manipulating the individual components (hours, minutes, seconds).

IDENTIFICATION DIVISION.
PROGRAM-ID. FormatTime.

DATA DIVISION.
WORKING-STORAGE SECTION.
01 CURRENT-TIME.
   05 HOURS   PIC 9(2).
   05 MINUTES PIC 9(2).
   05 SECONDS PIC 9(2).
01 FORMATTED-TIME PIC X(8).

PROCEDURE DIVISION.
    ACCEPT CURRENT-TIME FROM TIME.
    MOVE HOURS TO FORMATTED-TIME (1:2).
    MOVE ':' TO FORMATTED-TIME (3:3).
    MOVE MINUTES TO FORMATTED-TIME (4:5).
    MOVE ':' TO FORMATTED-TIME (6:6).
    MOVE SECONDS TO FORMATTED-TIME (7:8).
    DISPLAY 'Formatted Time: ' FORMATTED-TIME.
    STOP RUN.

Advanced Date and Time Handling

Date Arithmetic

COBOL allows for date arithmetic using integer operations. For example, adding days to a date can be done by converting the date to an integer, performing the addition, and then converting back.

IDENTIFICATION DIVISION.
PROGRAM-ID. DateArithmetic.

DATA DIVISION.
WORKING-STORAGE SECTION.
01 CURRENT-DATE.
   05 YEAR  PIC 9(4).
   05 MONTH PIC 9(2).
   05 DAY   PIC 9(2).
01 INTEGER-DATE PIC 9(8).
01 NEW-DATE.
   05 NEW-YEAR  PIC 9(4).
   05 NEW-MONTH PIC 9(2).
   05 NEW-DAY   PIC 9(2).

PROCEDURE DIVISION.
    ACCEPT CURRENT-DATE FROM DATE YYYYMMDD.
    MOVE FUNCTION NUMVAL(YEAR & MONTH & DAY) TO INTEGER-DATE.
    ADD 1 TO INTEGER-DATE.
    MOVE FUNCTION NUMVAL-COALESCE(INTEGER-DATE(1:4)) TO NEW-YEAR.
    MOVE FUNCTION NUMVAL-COALESCE(INTEGER-DATE(5:6)) TO NEW-MONTH.
    MOVE FUNCTION NUMVAL-COALESCE(INTEGER-DATE(7:8)) TO NEW-DAY.
    DISPLAY 'New Date: ' NEW-YEAR '-' NEW-MONTH '-' NEW-DAY.
    STOP RUN.

Handling Leap Years

Leap years can be handled by checking if the year is divisible by 4, but not by 100, unless it is also divisible by 400.

IDENTIFICATION DIVISION.
PROGRAM-ID. LeapYearCheck.

DATA DIVISION.
WORKING-STORAGE SECTION.
01 YEAR PIC 9(4).
01 IS-LEAP-YEAR PIC X VALUE 'N'.

PROCEDURE DIVISION.
    DISPLAY 'Enter Year: '.
    ACCEPT YEAR.
    IF (YEAR MOD 4 = 0 AND YEAR MOD 100 NOT = 0) OR (YEAR MOD 400 = 0)
        MOVE 'Y' TO IS-LEAP-YEAR
    END-IF.
    DISPLAY 'Is Leap Year: ' IS-LEAP-YEAR.
    STOP RUN.

Summary

  • COBOL handles dates and times using numeric or alphanumeric data types.
  • The ACCEPT statement is used to get the current date and time.
  • Dates and times can be formatted by manipulating their components.
  • Advanced date handling includes date arithmetic and leap year calculations.

Understanding how to handle dates and times in COBOL is essential for developing robust business applications. By mastering these concepts, you can ensure your programs handle temporal data accurately and efficiently.
