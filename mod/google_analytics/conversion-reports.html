Introduction to Conversion Reports

Conversion reports in Google Analytics are essential for understanding how well your website or app is achieving its goals. These reports provide insights into user behavior, helping you optimize your marketing strategies and improve user experience.

  • Goals: Specific actions you want users to take on your site (e.g., making a purchase, signing up for a newsletter).
  • E-commerce Tracking: Detailed tracking of transactions and revenue.
  • Multi-Channel Funnels: Analysis of how different marketing channels contribute to conversions.
  • Attribution Models: Methods to assign credit for conversions to different touchpoints in the user journey.

Setting Up Goals

Goals are fundamental to conversion tracking. They help you measure how well your site or app fulfills your target objectives.

Types of Goals

  • Destination: A specific location loads (e.g., a thank you page).
  • Duration: Sessions that last a specific amount of time or longer.
  • Pages/Screens per Session: A user views a specific number of pages or screens.
  • Event: An action defined as an event is triggered (e.g., video play, ad click).

Creating a Goal

  1. Navigate to your Google Analytics account.
  2. Go to the Admin panel.
  3. Under the View column, click on Goals.
  4. Click the + NEW GOAL button.
  5. Choose a template or create a custom goal.
  6. Define the goal details and save.
Example: Setting up a Destination Goal

1. Goal Setup: Choose "Custom" and click "Continue".
2. Goal Description: Name your goal (e.g., "Thank You Page") and choose "Destination".
3. Goal Details: Enter the destination URL (e.g., `/thank-you.html`).
4. Verify the goal and save.

E-commerce Tracking

E-commerce tracking provides detailed insights into product performance, sales, and revenue.

Enabling E-commerce Tracking

  1. Navigate to the Admin panel.
  2. Under the View column, click on E-commerce Settings.
  3. Toggle the Enable E-commerce switch to ON.
  4. Optionally, enable Enhanced E-commerce Reporting for more detailed data.

E-commerce Reports

  • Overview: Summary of revenue, transactions, and e-commerce conversion rate.
  • Product Performance: Data on product sales, quantity, and average price.
  • Sales Performance: Revenue and transaction data.
  • Transactions: Detailed information on individual transactions.
  • Time to Purchase: Time lag between user sessions and transactions.

Multi-Channel Funnels

Multi-Channel Funnels (MCF) help you understand the role of various marketing channels in driving conversions.

Key Concepts

  • Assisted Conversions: Conversions that were assisted by one or more channels.
  • Top Conversion Paths: The most common sequences of channel interactions leading to conversions.
  • Time Lag: The amount of time it takes for users to convert after their first interaction.
  • Path Length: The number of interactions it takes for users to convert.

Accessing MCF Reports

  1. Navigate to the Conversions section in the left-hand menu.
  2. Click on Multi-Channel Funnels.
  3. Explore the different reports such as Overview, Assisted Conversions, and Top Conversion Paths.

Attribution Models

Attribution models determine how credit for conversions is assigned to different touchpoints in the user journey.

Types of Attribution Models

  • Last Interaction: All credit goes to the last touchpoint.
  • First Interaction: All credit goes to the first touchpoint.
  • Linear: Credit is evenly distributed across all touchpoints.
  • Time Decay: More credit is given to touchpoints closer to the conversion.
  • Position-Based: 40% credit to the first and last interactions, 20% distributed among the middle interactions.

Using the Model Comparison Tool

  1. Navigate to the Conversions section.
  2. Click on Attribution.
  3. Select Model Comparison Tool.
  4. Choose different models to compare how credit is assigned.

Conclusion

Conversion reports in Google Analytics are powerful tools for measuring and optimizing your website's performance. By setting up goals, enabling e-commerce tracking, analyzing multi-channel funnels, and understanding attribution models, you can gain valuable insights into user behavior and improve your marketing strategies. Regularly reviewing these reports will help you make data-driven decisions to enhance your site's effectiveness and achieve your business objectives.
