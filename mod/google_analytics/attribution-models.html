Attribution Models
Advanced Segments ►

Attribution models are essential in understanding how different marketing channels contribute to conversions in Google Analytics. This topic will guide you from the basics to advanced concepts, ensuring you have a comprehensive understanding of attribution models.

What is an Attribution Model?

An attribution model is a rule or set of rules that determines how credit for sales and conversions is assigned to touchpoints in conversion paths. In simpler terms, it helps you understand which marketing channels are most effective in driving conversions.

Key Concepts

  • Touchpoints: Interactions that a customer has with your brand before converting.
  • Conversion Path: The sequence of touchpoints that lead to a conversion.
  • Credit Assignment: How value is distributed among different touchpoints.

Types of Attribution Models

Google Analytics offers several predefined attribution models. Each model distributes credit differently across the conversion path.

Last Interaction

  • Definition: Assigns 100% of the credit to the final touchpoint before the conversion.
  • Use Case: Useful for understanding which touchpoint directly led to the conversion.
  • Example:
    • Conversion Path: Email > Social Media > Direct
    • Credit Assignment: Direct gets 100% of the credit.

Last Non-Direct Click

  • Definition: Ignores direct traffic and assigns 100% of the credit to the last channel that the customer clicked through before converting.
  • Use Case: Helps to understand the last marketing effort before the conversion, excluding direct visits.
  • Example:
    • Conversion Path: Email > Social Media > Direct
    • Credit Assignment: Social Media gets 100% of the credit.

Last Google Ads Click

  • Definition: Assigns 100% of the credit to the last Google Ads click before the conversion.
  • Use Case: Useful for evaluating the performance of Google Ads campaigns.
  • Example:
    • Conversion Path: Google Ads > Email > Direct
    • Credit Assignment: Google Ads gets 100% of the credit.

First Interaction

  • Definition: Assigns 100% of the credit to the first touchpoint in the conversion path.
  • Use Case: Useful for understanding which initial touchpoint creates awareness.
  • Example:
    • Conversion Path: Email > Social Media > Direct
    • Credit Assignment: Email gets 100% of the credit.

Linear

  • Definition: Distributes the credit equally across all touchpoints in the conversion path.
  • Use Case: Useful for understanding the contribution of all touchpoints equally.
  • Example:
    • Conversion Path: Email > Social Media > Direct
    • Credit Assignment: Email, Social Media, and Direct each get 33.33% of the credit.

Time Decay

  • Definition: Gives more credit to touchpoints that are closer in time to the conversion.
  • Use Case: Useful for understanding the impact of recent touchpoints.
  • Example:
    • Conversion Path: Email (7 days ago) > Social Media (3 days ago) > Direct (1 day ago)
    • Credit Assignment: Direct gets the most credit, followed by Social Media, and then Email.

Position-Based

  • Definition: Assigns 40% of the credit to the first and last touchpoints, and the remaining 20% is distributed evenly among the middle touchpoints.
  • Use Case: Useful for understanding the importance of both the first and last interactions.
  • Example:
    • Conversion Path: Email > Social Media > Direct
    • Credit Assignment: Email and Direct each get 40%, Social Media gets 20%.

Custom Attribution Models

For advanced users, Google Analytics allows the creation of custom attribution models. This enables you to tailor the credit assignment based on your specific business needs.

Creating a Custom Attribution Model

  1. Navigate to the Model Comparison Tool: Found under the Conversions > Attribution section in Google Analytics.
  2. Select 'Create new custom model': This option allows you to define your own rules for credit assignment.
  3. Define the Rules: You can set custom weights for different touchpoints, apply lookback windows, and more.

Example of a Custom Attribution Model

# Pseudo-code for a custom attribution model
def custom_attribution_model(touchpoints):
    credit = {}
    total_touchpoints = len(touchpoints)
    
    for i, touchpoint in enumerate(touchpoints):
        if i == 0 or i == total_touchpoints - 1:
            credit[touchpoint] = 0.4  # 40% credit to first and last touchpoints
        else:
            credit[touchpoint] = 0.2 / (total_touchpoints - 2)  # Remaining 20% distributed equally
    
    return credit

# Example usage
touchpoints = ['Email', 'Social Media', 'Direct']
credit_distribution = custom_attribution_model(touchpoints)
print(credit_distribution)

Comparing Attribution Models

Understanding the differences between attribution models can help you choose the right one for your analysis.

Model Credit Assignment Use Case
Last Interaction 100% to the final touchpoint Direct impact analysis
Last Non-Direct Click 100% to the last non-direct touchpoint Excluding direct visits
Last Google Ads Click 100% to the last Google Ads click Evaluating Google Ads performance
First Interaction 100% to the first touchpoint Initial touchpoint analysis
Linear Equal credit to all touchpoints Equal contribution analysis
Time Decay More credit to recent touchpoints Recent touchpoint impact
Position-Based 40% to first and last, 20% to middle touchpoints First and last interaction importance

Conclusion

Attribution models are crucial for understanding the effectiveness of different marketing channels in driving conversions. By using predefined models or creating custom ones, you can gain valuable insights into your marketing efforts. Remember to choose the model that best aligns with your business goals and the specific questions you are trying to answer.
