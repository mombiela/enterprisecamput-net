Introduction to Multi-Channel Funnels

Multi-Channel Funnels (MCF) in Google Analytics help you understand the customer journey across different marketing channels. They provide insights into how various channels work together to drive conversions.

Key Concepts

  • Conversion Path: The sequence of interactions (clicks/referrals) that lead to a conversion.
  • Assisted Conversions: Conversions where a channel contributed to the conversion but was not the final interaction.
  • Top Conversion Paths: The most common sequences of interactions leading to conversions.
  • Time Lag: The time it takes for a user to convert after their first interaction.
  • Path Length: The number of interactions it takes for a user to convert.

Setting Up Multi-Channel Funnels

To start using Multi-Channel Funnels, you need to have goals or e-commerce tracking set up in Google Analytics.

Steps to Set Up Goals

  1. Navigate to the Admin panel.
  2. Under the 'View' column, click on 'Goals'.
  3. Click on '+ New Goal'.
  4. Follow the setup wizard to define your goal.

Enabling E-commerce Tracking

  1. Navigate to the Admin panel.
  2. Under the 'View' column, click on 'E-commerce Settings'.
  3. Toggle the 'Enable E-commerce' switch to 'ON'.
  4. Optionally, enable 'Enhanced E-commerce Reporting'.

Analyzing Multi-Channel Funnels

Once set up, you can access Multi-Channel Funnels reports under the 'Conversions' section in Google Analytics.

Types of Reports

  • Overview: Provides a summary of the assisted conversions, top conversion paths, and other key metrics.
  • Assisted Conversions: Shows how many conversions each channel assisted.
  • Top Conversion Paths: Displays the most common paths users take to convert.
  • Time Lag: Shows the time between the first interaction and conversion.
  • Path Length: Displays the number of interactions before a conversion.

Example Analysis

Consider a scenario where you want to analyze the assisted conversions for your website.

  1. Navigate to 'Conversions' > 'Multi-Channel Funnels' > 'Assisted Conversions'.
  2. Review the table that shows each channel and the number of assisted conversions.
| Channel       | Assisted Conversions | Last Click or Direct Conversions | Assisted/Last Click or Direct Conversions |
|---------------|----------------------|----------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Organic Search| 50                   | 100                              | 0.5                                      |
| Paid Search   | 30                   | 70                               | 0.43                                     |
| Social Network| 20                   | 40                               | 0.5                                      |

In this example, Organic Search has assisted in 50 conversions and has a ratio of 0.5, indicating it plays a significant role in the conversion path.

Advanced Techniques

For advanced users, Multi-Channel Funnels can be customized and integrated with other tools for deeper analysis.

Custom Channel Groupings

Custom Channel Groupings allow you to create and analyze your own set of channels.

  1. Navigate to 'Conversions' > 'Multi-Channel Funnels'.
  2. Click on 'Channel Groupings' > 'Create a Custom Channel Grouping'.
  3. Define your custom channels based on your marketing strategy.

Integration with Google Data Studio

Google Data Studio can be used to visualize Multi-Channel Funnels data.

  1. Connect Google Analytics to Google Data Studio.
  2. Create a new report and add a data source.
  3. Use the 'Multi-Channel Funnels' data to create custom visualizations.

Conclusion

Multi-Channel Funnels in Google Analytics provide valuable insights into the customer journey and the role of different marketing channels in driving conversions. By understanding and utilizing these reports, you can optimize your marketing strategies and improve your conversion rates. Whether you are a beginner setting up goals or an advanced user creating custom channel groupings, Multi-Channel Funnels offer powerful tools to enhance your analytics capabilities.
