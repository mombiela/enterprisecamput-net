Introduction

The definition of the project is a crucial stage in any market research. In this phase, the objectives, scope, and necessary resources to carry out the study are established. A clear and precise definition of the project ensures that all involved understand the goals and methods to be used, facilitating coordination and effective execution of the project.

Project Objectives

The project objectives should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART). Clearly defining the objectives allows for efficient focus of efforts and resources.

  • Specific: Objectives should be clear and detailed.
  • Measurable: They should be quantifiable to assess progress.
  • Achievable: They should be realistic and possible to achieve with available resources.
  • Relevant: They should align with the overall goals of the organization.
  • Time-bound: They should have a defined timeframe for completion.

Example of SMART Objectives

Objective: Increase market share by 10% in the next year by identifying new market opportunities and optimizing current marketing strategies.

Project Scope

The project scope defines the boundaries and limits of the study. It includes what will be investigated, what will not be investigated, and the inclusion and exclusion criteria.

  • What will be investigated: Specific market areas, consumer segments, products, or services.
  • What will not be investigated: Areas outside the main focus, irrelevant segments.
  • Inclusion criteria: Characteristics that elements must have to be considered in the study.
  • Exclusion criteria: Characteristics that disqualify elements from the study.

Example of Scope

The study will focus on the mobile device market in the northern region of the country, excluding low-end devices and markets outside this region.

Necessary Resources

Identifying and assigning the necessary resources is fundamental for the successful execution of the project. Resources may include:

  • Human: Necessary personnel, such as analysts, surveyors, and project managers.
  • Financial: Available budget for the project.
  • Technological: Tools and software needed for data collection and analysis.
  • Materials: Physical materials needed, such as printed questionnaires or data collection devices.

Example of Resources

Human Resources: 2 data analysts, 5 surveyors, 1 project manager.
Budget: $50,000.
Technology: Data analysis software (SPSS), online survey tools (SurveyMonkey).
Materials: Printed questionnaires, tablets for field surveys.

Methodology

The methodology describes the methods and techniques that will be used to carry out the research. It includes:

  • Research design: Exploratory, descriptive, causal.
  • Data collection methods: Surveys, interviews, focus groups, observation.
  • Data analysis: Statistical techniques, qualitative analysis.

Example of Methodology

Research design: Descriptive.
Data collection methods: Online surveys and telephone interviews.
Data analysis: Statistical analysis using SPSS, qualitative analysis of open responses.

Schedule

The schedule establishes the stages of the project and the deadlines for each. A well-defined schedule helps ensure that the project stays on track and is completed on time.

Conclusion

The definition of the project is an essential stage in market research. Establishing clear objectives, defining the scope, assigning resources, choosing the appropriate methodology, and creating a detailed schedule are fundamental steps to ensure the success of the project. Careful planning in this initial phase facilitates execution and increases the likelihood of obtaining valuable and actionable results.
