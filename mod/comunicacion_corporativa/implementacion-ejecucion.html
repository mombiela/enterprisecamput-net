Introduction

The implementation and execution of the corporate communication plan is a crucial phase that determines the success of the designed strategies. In this section, we will learn how to carry out the communication plan, ensuring that all actions align with organizational objectives.

Steps for Implementing the Plan

  1. Assignment of Responsibilities

    • Identify those responsible for each task.
    • Define clear roles and responsibilities.
    • Example: The marketing team will handle social media campaigns, while the public relations department will manage media communications.

  2. Development of a Schedule

    • Create a detailed calendar with specific dates and deadlines.
    • Ensure that all activities are well distributed over time.
    • Example: Launch of the social media campaign on March 1, followed by a press conference on March 15.

  3. Resource Allocation

    • Determine the necessary resources (human, financial, technological).
    • Ensure the availability of these resources.
    • Example: Budget allocated for social media advertising, hiring a graphic designer.

  4. Training and Preparation

    • Train the staff involved in the execution of the plan.
    • Conduct drills or tests if necessary.
    • Example: Training workshops for the customer service team on how to handle inquiries related to the new campaign.

Execution of the Plan

  1. Campaign Launch

    • Start activities according to the schedule.
    • Monitor progress and make adjustments if necessary.
    • Example: Publish the first ad on social media and monitor public reactions.

  2. Monitoring and Control

    • Supervise the performance of activities.
    • Use tracking and analysis tools.
    • Example: Use Google Analytics to measure web traffic generated by the campaign.

  3. Internal Communication

    • Keep all team members informed about progress.
    • Facilitate communication between departments.
    • Example: Weekly meetings to discuss progress and resolve issues.

  4. Crisis Management

    • Be prepared to handle any unforeseen events.
    • Have a contingency plan in case of a crisis.
    • Example: Rapid response strategy in case of negative comments on social media.

Conclusion

The implementation and execution of the corporate communication plan require meticulous planning and effective coordination among all involved. Assigning clear responsibilities, developing a detailed schedule, and continuously monitoring progress are essential steps to ensure the success of the plan. Preparation and the ability to respond to unforeseen events also play a crucial role in the effective execution of communication strategies.
