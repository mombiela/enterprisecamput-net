Introduction

In this section of the Corporate Communication course, we will address the importance of evaluation and learning after a crisis. This process is crucial to identify what worked, what didn't, and how communication strategies can be improved for future situations.

Importance of Post-crisis Evaluation

  • Identification of Failures and Successes: Evaluate which aspects of communication were effective and which were not.
  • Continuous Improvement: Implement improvements based on lessons learned.
  • Transparency and Accountability: Maintain stakeholder trust by showing a commitment to continuous improvement.
  • Preparation for the Future: Develop more robust contingency plans.

Evaluation Methods

Data Analysis

  • Quantitative:
    • Social media metrics (reach, engagement).
    • Web traffic analysis.
    • Surveys and questionnaires.
  • Qualitative:
    • Interviews with stakeholders.
    • Media content analysis.
    • Review of internal feedback.

Feedback Meetings

  • Internal:
    • Meetings with the communication team.
    • Brainstorming sessions to identify areas for improvement.
  • External:
    • Meetings with clients and partners.
    • Discussion forums with the community.

Tools and Techniques

Analysis Tools

  • Google Analytics: To measure the impact on web traffic.
  • Hootsuite/Buffer: To analyze social media performance.
  • SurveyMonkey: To conduct surveys and collect feedback.

Evaluation Techniques

  • SWOT Analysis: Identify Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.
  • Ishikawa Diagram: To identify root causes of problems.
  • Benchmarking: Compare performance with industry standards.

Example of Post-crisis Evaluation

Suppose a company faced a crisis due to a defective product. Here is an example of how a post-crisis evaluation could be conducted:

### Step 1: Data Collection

- **Social Media Metrics**:
  - Reach: 500,000 impressions.
  - Engagement: 10,000 interactions.
- **Web Traffic**:
  - 20% increase in visits to the support page.
- **Surveys**:
  - 70% of customers felt that communication was clear.

### Step 2: Data Analysis

- **Strengths**:
  - High visibility on social media.
  - Quick response from the support team.
- **Weaknesses**:
  - Lack of detailed information in the first communication.
  - Delays in updating the website.

### Step 3: Feedback Meetings

- **Internal**:
  - Meeting with the communication team to discuss findings.
  - Brainstorming session to improve the speed of website updates.
- **External**:
  - Meeting with key customers to get direct feedback.
  - Discussion forum with the community to address concerns.

### Step 4: Implementation of Improvements

- **Protocol Updates**:
  - Create communication templates for different types of crises.
  - Establish a dedicated team for website updates.
- **Training**:
  - Additional training for the support team in crisis management.
  - Effective communication workshops for the marketing team.

Conclusion

Post-crisis evaluation is an essential component of crisis management in corporate communication. Through detailed analysis and the implementation of improvements based on lessons learned, organizations can strengthen their ability to handle future crises more effectively. This process not only improves crisis response but also contributes to transparency and trust with stakeholders.
