Definition of External Communication

External communication refers to all interactions and messages that an organization issues outward, that is, to its external audience. This audience may include customers, suppliers, investors, media, government authorities, and the community at large.

Key Concepts

  • External Audience: Groups or individuals outside the organization who have an interest in or are affected by the company's activities.
  • Media: Channels through which information is transmitted, such as press, social media, emails, etc.
  • Corporate Messages: Information that the organization wishes to communicate, which may include news, announcements, financial reports, among others.

Objectives of External Communication

External communication has several fundamental objectives that help the organization maintain a positive image and build strong relationships with its external audience.

Main Objectives

  1. Build and Maintain Corporate Image:

    • Create a positive perception of the organization.
    • Manage the reputation and identity of the brand.

  2. Foster Relationships with the External Audience:

    • Establish and maintain relationships with customers, suppliers, and other stakeholders.
    • Facilitate collaboration and mutual understanding.

  3. Inform and Educate:

    • Provide relevant and accurate information about the company's products, services, and policies.
    • Educate the public on topics of interest related to the organization.

  4. Crisis Management:

    • Respond effectively and quickly to crisis situations.
    • Minimize the negative impact of adverse events.

  5. Promote Products and Services:

    • Communicate the features and benefits of products and services.
    • Attract and retain customers.

Practical Examples

  • Press Release: An official document sent to the media to announce important events, such as the launch of a new product.
  • Newsletter: Periodic publications sent to customers and partners to keep them informed about company news.
  • Social Media: Use of platforms like Twitter, LinkedIn, and Facebook to interact with the public and share relevant content.

Conclusion

External communication is an essential tool for any organization seeking to maintain a positive and effective relationship with its external audience. Through a well-defined strategy, companies can build and maintain a strong corporate image, foster valuable relationships, inform and educate their audience, manage crises, and promote their products and services. Effective external communication not only improves the public perception of the organization but also contributes to its long-term success.
