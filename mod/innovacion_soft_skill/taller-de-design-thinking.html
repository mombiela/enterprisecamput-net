Introduction to Design Thinking

Design Thinking is a user-centered methodology that seeks to solve complex problems through creativity and innovation. This workshop is designed to provide professionals with the tools and techniques necessary to apply Design Thinking in their work environments.

Workshop Objectives

  • Understand the fundamental principles of Design Thinking.
  • Learn to identify and define problems from the user's perspective.
  • Develop skills to generate innovative ideas.
  • Prototype and test solutions effectively.
  • Foster a culture of innovation and collaboration within the team.

Workshop Structure

1. Empathize

Objective:

Understand the needs and problems of users.

Activities:

  • Interviews: Conduct interviews with users to obtain detailed information about their experiences and challenges.
  • Observation: Observe users in their natural environment to identify behaviors and patterns.
  • Empathy maps: Create empathy maps to visualize users' emotions and needs.

2. Define

Objective:

Synthesize the collected information to define the central problem.

Activities:

  • Data analysis: Review and analyze the information obtained during the empathy phase.
  • Problem statement: Formulate a clear and concise problem statement from the user's perspective.
  • Personas: Create user profiles (personas) to represent the different types of users affected by the problem.

3. Ideate

Objective:

Generate a wide range of ideas and possible solutions.

Activities:

  • Brainstorming: Conduct brainstorming sessions to generate as many solutions as possible.
  • SCAMPER: Use the SCAMPER technique (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Rearrange) to explore new ideas.
  • Idea selection: Evaluate and select the most promising ideas to develop.

4. Prototype

Objective:

Create quick and tangible prototypes of the selected ideas.

Activities:

  • Low-fidelity prototypes: Build simple and economical prototypes (such as sketches, paper models, etc.) to visualize the ideas.
  • Iteration: Refine the prototypes through multiple iterations based on the feedback received.

5. Test

Objective:

Test the prototypes with real users to obtain feedback.

Activities:

  • User testing: Conduct tests with users to evaluate the effectiveness and usability of the prototypes.
  • Feedback collection: Gather and analyze user feedback to identify areas for improvement.
  • Final iteration: Adjust and improve the prototypes based on the feedback obtained.

Conclusion

Design Thinking is a powerful methodology that allows professionals to tackle complex problems in an innovative and user-centered way. By following the stages of empathizing, defining, ideating, prototyping, and testing, teams can develop effective and creative solutions that meet the real needs of users. This workshop provides the tools and techniques necessary to implement Design Thinking in any work environment, fostering a culture of continuous innovation and collaboration.
