Introduction

In any communication strategy, defining clear and achievable objectives is fundamental to success. Communication objectives act as a guide that directs all actions and decisions within the strategy. This topic will address how to define these objectives effectively.

What are Communication Objectives?

Communication objectives are specific goals that an organization or individual wishes to achieve through their communication efforts. These objectives must be clear, measurable, and aligned with the overall goals of the organization.

Characteristics of Communication Objectives

  • Specific: They must be clear and detailed.
  • Measurable: They must be quantifiable.
  • Achievable: They must be realistic and possible to achieve.
  • Relevant: They must be aligned with the overall goals of the organization.
  • Time-bound: They must have a defined timeframe for their achievement.

Types of Communication Objectives

There are several types of communication objectives, each with a specific purpose:

  • Informative: Provide clear and precise information to the audience.
  • Persuasive: Influence the attitudes or behaviors of the audience.
  • Relational: Strengthen relationships with the audience.
  • Reputational: Improve or maintain the organization's image.

Examples of Communication Objectives

Below are some examples of communication objectives for different contexts:

  • Informative: "Increase awareness of the new product by 30% in the next six months."
  • Persuasive: "Increase product sales by 20% during the next quarter."
  • Relational: "Improve customer satisfaction by 15% in the next year."
  • Reputational: "Position the company as a leader in sustainability in the sector in the next two years."

How to Define Communication Objectives

To define effective communication objectives, follow these steps:

  1. Situation Analysis: Evaluate the current context and communication needs.
  2. Audience Identification: Clearly define who you are targeting.
  3. Setting General Goals: Define the broad goals you wish to achieve.
  4. Formulating Specific Objectives: Use the SMART methodology (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound).
  5. Review and Adjustment: Review the objectives to ensure they are realistic and aligned with the general goals.

Practical Exercise

Below is an exercise to practice defining communication objectives:

Exercise

  1. Context: Imagine you work in the communication department of a tech company that is launching new project management software.
  2. Task: Define three communication objectives using the SMART methodology.

Example Response

  • Informative Objective: "Increase awareness of the new project management software by 40% among project managers in the next six months."
  • Persuasive Objective: "Get 25% of project managers who attend the software demo to sign up for a free trial in the next three months."
  • Relational Objective: "Increase social media engagement by 50% by posting relevant content about the software in the next quarter."

Conclusion

Defining clear and achievable communication objectives is essential for the success of any communication strategy. These objectives must be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound. By following a structured methodology, such as SMART, you can ensure that your objectives are well-defined and aligned with the organization's overall goals.
