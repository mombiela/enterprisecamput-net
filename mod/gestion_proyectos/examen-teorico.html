Introduction

The theoretical exam is a fundamental part of the Project Management course. Its objective is to evaluate the knowledge acquired by students throughout the course, ensuring they understand key concepts and are able to apply them in practical situations.

Exam Structure

The theoretical exam is divided into several sections, each focusing on different aspects of project management:

  • Basic Project Management Concepts
  • Project Management Methodologies
  • Planning and Scheduling
  • Risk Management
  • Resource Management
  • Monitoring and Control

Basic Project Management Concepts

In this section, knowledge about the fundamentals of project management will be evaluated.

  • Project Definition: A project is a temporary effort to create a unique product, service, or result.
  • Project Life Cycle: Includes the phases of initiation, planning, execution, monitoring, and closing.
  • Triple Constraint: Time, cost, and scope are the three elements that must be balanced in project management.

Project Management Methodologies

Knowledge about the different methodologies used in project management will be evaluated.

  • Agile: Iterative and flexible methodology that adapts to rapid changes.
  • Waterfall: Sequential methodology where each phase must be completed before moving on to the next.
  • Scrum: Framework within Agile that focuses on incremental deliveries and daily meetings.

| Methodology | Characteristics | Advantages | Disadvantages | |-------------|-----------------|------------|---------------| | Agile | Iterative, flexible | Adaptability, quick response to changes | Can be chaotic without good management | | Waterfall | Sequential, structured | Clarity in each phase, easy to manage | Rigidity, difficult to adapt to changes | | Scrum | Incremental, daily meetings | Transparency, continuous improvement | Requires commitment and discipline |

Planning and Scheduling

This section covers the techniques and tools used to plan and schedule projects.

  • WBS (Work Breakdown Structure): Hierarchical decomposition of the work to be done.
  • Gantt Chart: Visual tool for scheduling tasks over time.
  • CPM (Critical Path Method): Technique to identify critical tasks that determine the project's duration.
Example of WBS:
1. Start Project
   1.1 Define objectives
   1.2 Identify stakeholders
2. Plan Project
   2.1 Create WBS
   2.2 Develop schedule

Risk Management

Evaluation of the ability to identify, analyze, and mitigate risks in a project.

  • Risk Identification: Process of determining which risks may affect the project.
  • Qualitative and Quantitative Analysis: Evaluation of the probability and impact of risks.
  • Risk Response Planning: Strategies to mitigate, transfer, accept, or avoid risks.

Resource Management

Understanding of the allocation and management of human, material, and financial resources will be evaluated.

  • Resource Allocation: Process of assigning resources to project tasks.
  • Team Management: Techniques to lead and motivate the project team.
  • Project Budget: Planning and control of project costs.

Monitoring and Control

This section covers techniques for monitoring project progress and taking corrective actions when necessary.

  • KPIs (Key Performance Indicators): Key indicators to measure project performance.
  • Earned Value Management (EVM): Technique to measure project performance in terms of cost and time.
  • Progress Reports: Documents that summarize the current status of the project.

Conclusion

The theoretical exam is a crucial tool for evaluating students' knowledge and understanding of project management. Through questions that cover basic concepts to advanced techniques, it ensures that students are prepared to apply this knowledge in real situations. Proper preparation for this exam includes a thorough review of all topics covered in the course and practice with examples and exercises.
