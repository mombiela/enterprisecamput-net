Introduction

Zoom has become an essential tool for online collaboration, especially in the professional field. However, with its popularity, concerns about security and privacy have also arisen. This topic will address best practices and settings to ensure that your Zoom meetings are secure and private.

Basic Security Settings

To ensure the security of your Zoom meetings, it is crucial to properly configure the available security options. Below are some essential settings:

  • Meeting Passwords: Always enable passwords for your meetings to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Waiting Room: Activate the waiting room to control who can join the meeting.
  • Lock the Meeting: Once all expected participants have joined, lock the meeting to prevent others from entering.
  • User Authentication: Set up the meeting so that only authenticated users can join.

Privacy Settings

Privacy is as important as security. Here are some settings to protect the privacy of participants:

  • Disable Automatic Recording: Ensure that automatic recording is disabled unless absolutely necessary.
  • Recording Notification: If you decide to record the meeting, Zoom will notify all participants.
  • Hide Profile Photos: To prevent inappropriate profile photos from being displayed, you can disable this option in the account settings.
  • Screen Sharing Control: Limit the ability to share the screen only to the host or specific users.

Configuration Examples

Enable Meeting Passwords

To enable passwords for your meetings, follow these steps:

  1. Log in to your Zoom account.
  2. Go to "Settings" in the navigation panel.
  3. In the "Meeting" tab, find the "Meeting (Basic)" section.
  4. Enable the "Require password for scheduled meetings" option.
Settings > Meeting > Meeting (Basic) > Require password for scheduled meetings

Activate Waiting Room

To activate the waiting room:

  1. Log in to your Zoom account.
  2. Go to "Settings" in the navigation panel.
  3. In the "Meeting" tab, find the "Meeting (Basic)" section.
  4. Enable the "Waiting room" option.
Settings > Meeting > Meeting (Basic) > Waiting room

Conclusion

Security and privacy in Zoom are critical aspects that should not be ignored. By following the best practices and settings mentioned, you can ensure that your meetings are secure and that the privacy of participants is protected. Remember to regularly review and update security settings to adapt to new threats and maintain a secure collaboration environment.
