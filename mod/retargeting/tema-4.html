In this section of the course on Retargeting, we will explore the different types of retargeting that can be used in digital marketing campaigns. Retargeting is a technique that allows advertisers to reconnect with users who have previously interacted with their website or application but have not completed a desired action, such as a purchase or a subscription.

Types of Retargeting

There are several types of retargeting that can be implemented depending on the campaign objectives and user behavior. Below are the most common ones:

1. Website Retargeting

This type of retargeting is based on website visits. It is used to show ads to users who have visited specific pages of a website but have not completed a conversion.

  • How it works: A tracking pixel is placed on the website that collects data about user visits.
  • Example: A user visits a product page on an online store but does not make a purchase. Later, they are shown ads for that product on other websites.
<!-- Example of tracking pixel code -->
<script>
  (function() {
    var pixel = document.createElement('img');
    pixel.src = 'https://example.com/track?event=pageview&product_id=12345';
    document.body.appendChild(pixel);
  })();
</script>

2. Search Retargeting

This type of retargeting is based on the searches users perform on search engines like Google. It is used to show ads to users who have searched for terms related to the company's products or services.

  • How it works: Cookies are used to track users' searches and then show them relevant ads.
  • Example: A user searches for "best running shoes" on Google. Later, they are shown ads for running shoes on other websites.

3. Email Retargeting

This type of retargeting is based on users' interactions with emails. It is used to show ads to users who have opened or clicked on an email from the company.

  • How it works: A tracking pixel is integrated into the emails sent to users.
  • Example: A user opens a promotional email but does not take any action. Later, they are shown ads related to the promotion on other websites.

4. Social Media Retargeting

This type of retargeting is based on users' interactions on social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter. It is used to show ads to users who have interacted with the company's page or posts.

  • How it works: Tracking pixels provided by social media platforms are used.
  • Example: A user likes a brand's post on Facebook. Later, they are shown ads from that brand on Facebook and Instagram.

5. Dynamic Retargeting

This type of retargeting is based on showing personalized ads that include specific products or services that users have viewed on the website.

  • How it works: User behavior data is used to generate dynamic ads.
  • Example: A user visits a product page on an online store. Later, they are shown ads with images and details of that specific product.
<!-- Example of dynamic retargeting code -->
<script>
  (function() {
    var productData = {
      id: '12345',
      name: 'Running Shoes',
      price: '79.99'
    };
    var pixel = document.createElement('img');
    pixel.src = 'https://example.com/track?event=view&product=' + JSON.stringify(productData);
    document.body.appendChild(pixel);
  })();
</script>

Comparison of Types of Retargeting

| Type of Retargeting | Based on | Example of Use | |---------------------------|-----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------| | Website Retargeting | Website visits | Show ads for products viewed but not purchased | | Search Retargeting | Search engine searches | Show ads based on search terms | | Email Retargeting | Email interactions | Show ads to users who opened emails | | Social Media Retargeting | Social media interactions | Show ads to users who interacted with posts | | Dynamic Retargeting | User behavior | Show personalized ads with specific products |

Conclusion

Retargeting is a powerful tool in the digital marketing arsenal, allowing companies to reconnect with users who have shown interest in their products or services. By understanding the different types of retargeting and how to implement them, marketing professionals can create more effective and personalized campaigns that drive conversions and improve return on investment (ROI).
