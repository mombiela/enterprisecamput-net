Introduction

Brand architecture is an essential component in the marketing strategy of any company. However, it is not a static concept; it requires continuous adjustments and improvements to remain relevant and effective. In this section, we will explore how to identify areas for improvement, implement changes, and measure the impact of these adjustments on brand architecture.

Identification of Areas for Improvement

To make effective adjustments, it is first necessary to identify the areas that require attention. Here are some methods to do so:

  • Data Analysis: Use analysis tools to review the performance of different brands and sub-brands.
  • Customer Feedback: Collect and analyze customer feedback to identify problems or areas for improvement.
  • Brand Audits: Conduct periodic audits to evaluate the consistency and effectiveness of brand architecture.

Implementation of Changes

Once the areas for improvement have been identified, the next step is to implement the necessary changes. Here are some steps to follow:

  • Define Clear Objectives: Before making any changes, it is crucial to have clear and measurable objectives.
  • Develop an Action Plan: Create a detailed plan that includes specific steps to follow, necessary resources, and timelines.
  • Internal Communication: Ensure that all team members are informed and aligned with the proposed changes.

Measuring the Impact

To ensure that the adjustments made are having the desired effect, it is necessary to measure their impact. Here are some ways to do so:

  • KPIs (Key Performance Indicators): Define and monitor specific KPIs related to brand architecture.
  • Customer Satisfaction Surveys: Conduct surveys to measure customer perception before and after the changes.
  • Comparative Analysis: Compare brand performance before and after the adjustments to evaluate their effectiveness.

Example of KPI

KPI Description Measurement Method
Brand Recognition Level of brand awareness among the target audience Surveys, market studies
Customer Loyalty Percentage of customers who make repeat purchases Sales data analysis
Brand Consistency Degree of consistency in brand communication Brand audits, internal reviews

Practical Example

Suppose a company has identified that one of its sub-brands is not performing as expected. Here is an example of how they might address this issue:

  1. Identification of Problems:

    • They conduct a brand audit and discover that the sub-brand is not aligned with the main brand.
    • They collect customer feedback and find that there is confusion about the sub-brand's purpose.

  2. Implementation of Changes:

    • They define the objective of improving the sub-brand's consistency with the main brand.
    • They develop an action plan that includes reviewing the sub-brand's logo, message, and marketing strategy.
    • They communicate these changes to the entire team to ensure coherent implementation.

  3. Measuring the Impact:

    • They define KPIs such as brand recognition and customer loyalty.
    • They conduct customer satisfaction surveys before and after the changes.
    • They compare sales data and customer feedback to evaluate the effectiveness of the adjustments.

Conclusion

Brand architecture is a dynamic process that requires continuous adjustments and improvements to remain effective. Identifying areas for improvement, implementing changes in a structured manner, and measuring the impact of these adjustments are crucial steps to ensure long-term success. By following these steps, companies can maintain a coherent and effective brand architecture that resonates with their target audience.
