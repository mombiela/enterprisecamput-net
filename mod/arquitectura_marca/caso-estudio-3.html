Introduction

In this case study, we will explore the concept of endorsed brands within brand architecture. Endorsed brands are those presented to the market with the backing of a parent or main brand, which gives them credibility and recognition. This approach can be very effective for companies looking to launch new products or services without starting from scratch in terms of brand recognition.

Key Concepts

  • Endorsed Brand: A brand that relies on the reputation and recognition of a parent brand.
  • Parent Brand: The main brand that supports one or more endorsed brands.
  • Brand Synergy: The competitive advantage gained by combining the reputation of the parent brand with the new brand.

Examples of Endorsed Brands

Example 1: Nestlé and KitKat

Nestlé is a parent brand that endorses KitKat. In this case, KitKat benefits from Nestlé's recognition and reputation, making its market acceptance easier.

  • Parent Brand: Nestlé
  • Endorsed Brand: KitKat
  • Synergy: The quality and trust associated with Nestlé are transferred to KitKat.

Example 2: Marriott and Courtyard by Marriott

Marriott International uses its parent brand to endorse Courtyard by Marriott, a mid-range hotel chain.

  • Parent Brand: Marriott
  • Endorsed Brand: Courtyard by Marriott
  • Synergy: Marriott's reputation for luxury and quality helps position Courtyard by Marriott in the market.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Endorsed Brands

| Advantages | Disadvantages | |--------------------------------------|----------------------------------------| | Greater credibility and recognition | Risk of brand dilution | | Lower launch cost | Dependence on the parent brand's reputation | | Marketing synergy | Confusion in positioning |

Strategies for Implementing Endorsed Brands

  • Evaluation of the Parent Brand: Ensure that the parent brand has a solid and positive reputation.
  • Clear Definition of the Endorsement: Establish how the relationship between the parent brand and the endorsed brand will be presented.
  • Consistency in Communication: Maintain coherent communication that reinforces the relationship between both brands.

Practical Exercise

  1. Identification of Endorsed Brands: Research and list three examples of endorsed brands in your industry or sector.
  2. Synergy Analysis: For each example, describe how the endorsed brand benefits from the parent brand.
  3. Endorsed Brand Proposal: Imagine you work for a company that wants to launch a new product. Propose an endorsed brand strategy, including the choice of the parent brand and how the endorsement would be communicated.

Conclusion

Endorsed brands are an effective strategy to leverage the reputation and recognition of a parent brand. Although they present certain risks, such as the potential dilution of the parent brand, the benefits in terms of credibility and lower launch costs can be significant. When implementing an endorsed brand strategy, it is crucial to carefully evaluate the parent brand, clearly define the endorsement, and maintain consistent communication.

This case study has allowed us to better understand how endorsed brands work and how they can be effectively implemented in different business contexts.
