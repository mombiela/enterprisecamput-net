Introduction

The endorsed brands strategy is one of the multiple ways to structure a brand architecture. In this strategy, a main brand (or "parent brand") supports one or more secondary brands, providing credibility and recognition to the latter. This technique is commonly used by companies that want to launch new products or services without losing the trust and loyalty of their customers.

Key Concepts

  • Parent Brand: The main brand that endorses or supports the secondary brands.
  • Secondary Brand: The brands that are supported by the parent brand.
  • Endorsement: The act of the parent brand providing its support to the secondary brands.
  • Credibility and Recognition: Key benefits that secondary brands obtain by being endorsed by the parent brand.

Benefits of the Endorsed Brands Strategy

  • Transfer of Trust: The trust and loyalty that consumers have towards the parent brand are transferred to the secondary brands.
  • Ease of Introducing New Products: New products can be introduced to the market with greater ease and acceptance.
  • Brand Consistency: Maintains a coherent and consistent brand image across different products and services.

Examples of Endorsed Brands Strategy

Example 1: Nestlé and its Products

Nestlé is a parent brand that endorses a variety of products such as Nescafé, KitKat, and Maggi. The Nestlé brand provides credibility and recognition to these products, facilitating their acceptance in the market.

Example 2: Marriott and its Hotels

Marriott is another parent brand that endorses several hotel brands such as Courtyard by Marriott, Fairfield by Marriott, and Residence Inn by Marriott. Marriott's endorsement helps these brands maintain an image of quality and trust.

Comparison with Other Brand Architecture Strategies

Conclusion

The endorsed brands strategy is a powerful tool in brand architecture, allowing companies to leverage the credibility and recognition of a parent brand to launch and support additional products and services. This strategy not only facilitates the introduction of new products to the market but also helps maintain a coherent and reliable brand image. By understanding and applying this strategy, companies can significantly improve their positioning and success in the market.
