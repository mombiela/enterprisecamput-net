Introduction

The study of real cases is an effective methodology for developing problem-solving skills. Analyzing authentic situations allows professionals to understand how to apply theories and techniques in practical contexts. This approach not only improves the ability to identify and solve problems but also fosters critical thinking and informed decision-making.

Objectives of the Study of Real Cases

  • Understand the practical application of problem-solving theories and techniques.
  • Develop analytical and critical thinking skills.
  • Improve the ability to make informed decisions.
  • Foster collaboration and teamwork.

Methodology

Selection of Cases

  • Relevance: The cases must be pertinent to the participants' work environment.
  • Diversity: Include a variety of cases to address different types of problems.
  • Complexity: Choose cases with different levels of difficulty to challenge participants.

Case Analysis

  1. Case Description:

    • Context and background.
    • Main problem.
    • Involved actors.

  2. Problem Identification:

    • Explicit and implicit problems.
    • Causes and effects.

  3. Solution Development:

    • Generation of possible solutions.
    • Evaluation of advantages and disadvantages.
    • Selection of the best solution.

  4. Implementation and Evaluation:

    • Action plan.
    • Monitoring and adjustment.
    • Evaluation of results.

Practical Examples

Case 1: Resolving a Conflict in the Work Team

  • Case Description:

    • A software development team faces internal conflicts affecting productivity.
    • Main problem: Lack of communication and collaboration.
    • Involved actors: Team members, project leader.

  • Problem Identification:

    • Explicit problems: Disputes among members, project delays.
    • Implicit problems: Lack of trust, differences in work styles.

  • Solution Development:

    • Solution 1: Effective communication workshop.
    • Solution 2: Team restructuring.
    • Solution 3: Mediation by the project leader.

  • Implementation and Evaluation:

    • Action plan: Conduct an effective communication workshop.
    • Monitoring: Evaluate improvement in communication and collaboration.
    • Evaluation of results: Increase in productivity and reduction of conflicts.

Case 2: Process Optimization in a Manufacturing Plant

  • Case Description:

    • A manufacturing plant faces efficiency problems and high operational costs.
    • Main problem: Inefficient processes.
    • Involved actors: Plant manager, operators, engineering team.

  • Problem Identification:

    • Explicit problems: Production delays, material waste.
    • Implicit problems: Lack of training, obsolete machinery.

  • Solution Development:

    • Solution 1: Implementation of a training program.
    • Solution 2: Machinery update.
    • Solution 3: Process redesign.

  • Implementation and Evaluation:

    • Action plan: Redesign production processes.
    • Monitoring: Measure efficiency and operational costs.
    • Evaluation of results: Cost reduction and increase in efficiency.

Conclusion

The study of real cases is a powerful tool for developing problem-solving skills. Through detailed analysis and practical application, professionals can improve their ability to face and resolve challenges in the work environment. This approach not only strengthens technical competencies but also fosters critical thinking, decision-making, and teamwork.
