Introduction

The Problem Analysis and Resolution Method (PAR) is a structured technique that helps identify, analyze, and solve problems efficiently. This method is essential for improving decision-making and optimizing processes in any work environment.

Phases of the PAR Method

1. Problem Identification

  • Clear definition of the problem: It is crucial to understand and define the problem precisely.
  • Data collection: Gather all relevant information that can help understand the problem.
  • Symptom detection: Identify the symptoms that indicate the existence of the problem.

2. Problem Analysis

  • Root causes: Use tools like the Ishikawa diagram (cause-effect) or the 5 Whys to identify the fundamental causes of the problem.
  • Problem impact: Assess how the problem affects the organization or team.
  • Contributing factors: Identify other factors that may be contributing to the problem.

3. Solution Generation

  • Brainstorming: Encourage creativity and the generation of multiple possible solutions.
  • Alternative evaluation: Analyze the advantages and disadvantages of each proposed solution.
  • Selection of the best solution: Choose the most viable and effective solution.

4. Solution Implementation

  • Action plan: Develop a detailed plan to implement the solution.
  • Assignment of responsibilities: Define who will be responsible for each task.
  • Necessary resources: Identify and secure the resources needed for implementation.

5. Results Evaluation

  • Monitoring and follow-up: Evaluate the progress of the implementation and make adjustments if necessary.
  • Measurement of results: Compare the results obtained with the initial objectives.
  • Feedback: Collect feedback to improve future problem-solving processes.

Support Tools and Techniques

  • Ishikawa Diagram: Helps identify the root causes of the problem.
  • SWOT Analysis: Evaluates the strengths, opportunities, weaknesses, and threats related to the problem.
  • Decision Matrix: Facilitates the comparison of different solutions based on specific criteria.
Tool Purpose When to use it
Ishikawa Diagram Identify root causes Problem analysis
SWOT Analysis Evaluate the internal and external environment Solution generation
Decision Matrix Compare and select the best alternative Evaluation of alternatives

Practical Exercise

  1. Problem Identification: Think of a recent problem in your workplace and describe it briefly.
  2. Problem Analysis: Use the Ishikawa diagram to identify the root causes of the problem.
  3. Solution Generation: Conduct a brainstorming session and list at least three possible solutions.
  4. Solution Implementation: Develop an action plan for one of the proposed solutions.
  5. Results Evaluation: Define how you will measure the success of the implementation and what indicators you will use.

Conclusion

The Problem Analysis and Resolution Method (PAR) provides a systematic approach to effectively address and solve problems. By following these phases and using the appropriate tools, professionals can significantly improve their ability to make informed decisions and optimize processes in their work environment.
