Introduction

The analysis of behavior and needs of the community is a fundamental part of Community Management. This process allows for a better understanding of community members, identifying their interests and needs, and adapting communication and content strategies to meet them.

Objectives of the Analysis

  • Identify behavior patterns: Understand how community members interact.
  • Detect needs and expectations: Know what community members expect from the group or platform.
  • Optimize content strategy: Create relevant and attractive content based on community interests.
  • Improve interaction and engagement: Foster active and positive participation.

Analysis Tools and Techniques

Analysis Tools

  • Google Analytics: To analyze web traffic and user behavior.
  • Hootsuite Insights: To monitor and analyze social media activity.
  • SurveyMonkey: To create and distribute surveys to community members.
  • Facebook Insights: To obtain detailed data on interaction on Facebook pages.

Analysis Techniques

  • Quantitative data analysis: Use metrics and statistics to understand community behavior.
  • Qualitative data analysis: Review comments, surveys, and feedback to understand needs and expectations.
  • Empathy mapping: Technique to visualize what community members think, feel, say, and do.

Steps for Conducting an Effective Analysis

  1. Define clear objectives: Establish what you want to achieve with the analysis.
  2. Collect data: Use appropriate tools and techniques to gather relevant information.
  3. Analyze the data: Interpret the collected information to identify patterns and trends.
  4. Identify needs and behaviors: Determine what the community needs and how it behaves.
  5. Implement changes: Adapt content and communication strategy based on analysis findings.
  6. Monitor and adjust: Continue monitoring the community and adjust strategies as necessary.

Practical Example

Case Study: Software Developers Community

Definition of Objectives

  • Objective: Improve participation in discussion forums and increase member satisfaction.

Data Collection

  • Google Analytics: Analyze traffic and most visited pages.
  • Surveys: Send surveys to members to get feedback on their experience.

Data Analysis

  • Quantitative: Identify that tutorial pages have higher traffic but lower time spent.
  • Qualitative: Survey feedback indicates that tutorials are useful but too long.

Identification of Needs and Behaviors

  • Need: More concise and direct tutorials.
  • Behavior: Members prefer content they can consume quickly.

Implementation of Changes

  • Strategy: Create a series of short and direct tutorials.
  • Communication: Inform the community about the new tutorials through newsletters and social media.

Monitoring and Adjustment

  • Monitoring: Use Google Analytics to see if time spent on tutorials improves.
  • Adjustment: Collect continuous feedback and adjust the length and format of tutorials as necessary.

Conclusion

The analysis of behavior and needs of the community is essential for any Community Manager. Using the appropriate tools and techniques, a deep understanding of the community can be obtained, allowing for the adaptation of content and communication strategies to better meet the needs of members and foster active and positive participation.
