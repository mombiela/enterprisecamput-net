Introduction

In this section of the course, we will explore several success cases in Community Management. We will analyze how different companies and organizations have used effective strategies to build and maintain strong and engaged online communities. We will learn from their successes and failures to apply these lessons to our own Community Management strategies.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the key strategies used in successful Community Management cases.
  • Identify best practices and effective tactics.
  • Apply learned lessons to real situations in your own community.

Success Case 1: Nike

Strategy

Nike has been a pioneer in creating online communities through its Nike+ platform and social networks. Nike's strategy focuses on:

  • User-Generated Content (UGC): Encouraging users to share their sports experiences and achievements.
  • Events and Challenges: Organizing events and challenges that motivate the community to actively participate.
  • Constant Interaction: Responding to user comments and messages to maintain two-way communication.

Results

  • Increase in Brand Loyalty: Users feel more connected to the brand.
  • Higher Participation: Significant increase in participation in events and challenges.
  • Valuable Content: Generation of authentic and valuable content that can be reused in marketing campaigns.

Example of Social Media Interaction

User: "I just completed my first marathon with my new Nike Air! #JustDoIt"
Nike: "Congratulations! We love seeing your achievements. What's your next challenge? #JustDoIt"

Success Case 2: Starbucks

Strategy

Starbucks has used its loyalty program and social networks to build a strong and engaged community. Their strategies include:

  • Loyalty Program: Offering exclusive rewards and benefits to members of their loyalty program.
  • Personalized Content: Creating content that resonates with the interests and preferences of their audience.
  • Seasonal Campaigns: Launching themed campaigns during festive seasons to maintain interest and participation.

Results

  • Customer Loyalty: Increase in customer retention thanks to the loyalty program.
  • Social Media Engagement: High level of interaction and participation in their posts.
  • Increased Sales: Increase in sales during seasonal campaigns.

Example of Seasonal Campaign

Starbucks: "The Pumpkin Spice Latte season has arrived! Have you tried yours yet? Share your experience with us using #PSLSeason"

Success Case 3: Lego

Strategy

Lego has created a vibrant community through its Lego Ideas platform and social networks. Their key strategies include:

  • Co-Creation Platform: Allowing users to propose and vote for new Lego sets.
  • User Stories: Sharing user stories and creations on their official channels.
  • Events and Contests: Organizing contests and events that encourage creativity and participation.

Results

  • Collaborative Innovation: Development of products based on community ideas and votes.
  • Engagement and Loyalty: Users more engaged and loyal to the brand.
  • Attractive Content: Generation of attractive and relevant content for their audience.

Example of Proposal on Lego Ideas

User: "I propose a new Lego set based on Hogwarts Castle. Vote for my idea to make it a reality!"
Lego: "Great idea! Don't forget to share your proposal with your friends to get more votes. #LegoIdeas"

Conclusion

The study of success cases in Community Management provides us with valuable lessons on how to build and maintain effective online communities. The strategies used by Nike, Starbucks, and Lego demonstrate the importance of constant interaction, user-generated content, and personalization. By applying these tactics to our own communities, we can improve user loyalty, increase participation, and generate valuable content.

Always remember to adapt the strategies to the specific needs and characteristics of your community to achieve the best results.
