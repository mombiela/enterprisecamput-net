Introduction

In this section of the course, we will focus on practical exercises that will help you apply the positioning strategies you have learned. These exercises are designed to reinforce your understanding and skills in positioning products, brands, or services in the market.

Exercise 1: Competitor Analysis

Objective

Identify and analyze the main competitors in your market to understand their positioning strategies.

Instructions

  1. Select a market: Choose a specific market you are interested in.
  2. Identify competitors: Make a list of at least 5 main competitors.
  3. Analyze their strategies: Research and document the positioning strategies of each competitor. Consider aspects such as:
    • Value proposition
    • Target audience
    • Distribution channels
    • Marketing strategies

Example

| Competitor | Value Proposition | Target Audience | Distribution Channels | Marketing Strategies | |------------------|-------------------------------------|------------------------|------------------------|-----------------------------------| | Competitor A | High quality and durability | Professionals | Specialized stores | Advertising in technical magazines| | Competitor B | Affordable price | Students | Online stores | Social media campaigns | | Competitor C | Technological innovation | Technology enthusiasts | Department stores | Technology events and fairs |

Exercise 2: Developing a Value Proposition

Objective

Create a clear and compelling value proposition for your product or service.

Instructions

  1. Define your product or service: Briefly describe what you offer.
  2. Identify the problem: What problem does your product or service solve?
  3. Key benefits: List the most important benefits it offers.
  4. Differentiators: What makes your product or service unique?

Example

  • Product: Project management application
  • Problem: The difficulty of coordinating remote teams and maintaining productivity.
  • Key benefits:
    • Facilitates real-time communication.
    • Allows task and deadline management.
    • Offers integrations with other work tools.
  • Differentiators:
    • Intuitive and easy-to-use interface.
    • Advanced analysis and reporting features.
    • 24/7 technical support.

Exercise 3: Creating a Positioning Map

Objective

Visualize how different competitors are positioned in the market in relation to your product or service.

Instructions

  1. Select two criteria: Choose two relevant criteria for your market (e.g., price and quality).
  2. Draw the map: Create a chart with the two axes representing the selected criteria.
  3. Position the competitors: Place each competitor on the chart according to their relative position in the chosen criteria.
  4. Position your product: Add your product to the chart and analyze its position compared to the competitors.

Conclusion

These practical exercises allow you to apply positioning strategies in real situations. By analyzing the competition, developing a value proposition, and creating a positioning map, you will be better prepared to effectively position your product or service in the market. Remember that positioning is a continuous process that requires constant adaptation and adjustments to stay relevant and competitive.
