Introduction

In this section of the course on Positioning Strategies, we will address how to present and evaluate a positioning project. Presentation and evaluation are crucial steps to ensure that the proposed strategies are effective and aligned with business objectives.

Presentation Objectives

  • Clearly Communicate the Strategy: Ensure that all stakeholders understand the positioning strategy.
  • Obtain Feedback: Gather opinions and suggestions to improve the strategy.
  • Align Expectations: Confirm that all involved agree with the proposed objectives and methods.

Presentation Structure

  1. Introduction

    • Brief project summary.
    • Main objectives.

  2. Market Analysis

    • Market research.
    • Competitor analysis.
    • Identification of opportunities and threats.

  3. Positioning Strategy

    • Value proposition.
    • Market segmentation.
    • Market positioning.

  4. Implementation Plan

    • Specific tactics and actions.
    • Implementation timeline.
    • Necessary resources.

  5. Metrics and KPIs

    • Key performance indicators.
    • Monitoring and evaluation methods.

  6. Conclusion

    • Summary of key points.
    • Next steps.

Presentation Example

# Positioning Strategy for XYZ Corp

## Introduction
The objective of this presentation is to detail the positioning strategy for XYZ Corp, focused on increasing market share in the educational technology sector.

## Market Analysis
### Market Research
- Market size: $10 billion.
- Annual growth rate: 15%.

### Competitor Analysis
- Competitor A: 25% market share.
- Competitor B: 20% market share.

### Opportunities and Threats
- Opportunities: Growing demand for digital educational solutions.
- Threats: Entry of new competitors.

## Positioning Strategy
### Value Proposition
- Innovation in educational technology.
- Personalized customer service.

### Market Segmentation
- Segment 1: Educational institutions.
- Segment 2: Individual students.

### Market Positioning
- Positioning as a leader in innovation and customer service.

## Implementation Plan
### Tactics and Actions
- Digital marketing campaigns.
- Strategic partnerships with educational institutions.

### Implementation Timeline
| Phase        | Action                       | Start Date      | End Date     |
|--------------|------------------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Phase 1      | Market research              | 01/01/2023      | 31/01/2023   |
| Phase 2      | Campaign development         | 01/02/2023      | 28/02/2023   |
| Phase 3      | Implementation of tactics    | 01/03/2023      | 31/03/2023   |

### Necessary Resources
- Marketing team.
- Budget of $50,000.

## Metrics and KPIs
- Market share increase: 5% in the first year.
- Campaign conversion rate: 10%.

## Conclusion
In summary, the positioning strategy for XYZ Corp focuses on innovation and customer service, with a clear implementation plan and defined metrics to evaluate success.

Project Evaluation

Evaluation Criteria

  • Clarity and Coherence: The presentation should be clear and coherent, facilitating the understanding of the strategy.
  • Relevance of Information: The information presented should be relevant and supported by data.
  • Feasibility: The strategy should be viable and realistic, considering the available resources.
  • Potential Impact: Evaluate the potential impact of the strategy on business objectives.

Evaluation Methods

  • Stakeholder Feedback: Gather opinions and suggestions from stakeholders.
  • KPI Analysis: Monitor key performance indicators to evaluate the success of the strategy.
  • Periodic Review: Conduct periodic reviews to adjust the strategy as necessary.

Conclusion

Presentation and evaluation of the project are essential steps to ensure that positioning strategies are effective and aligned with business objectives. A well-structured presentation and rigorous evaluation can make the difference between the success and failure of a positioning strategy.
