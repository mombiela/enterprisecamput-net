Introduction

Positioning based on use or application focuses on how products or services are used by consumers. This strategy emphasizes highlighting specific benefits and practical applications that meet the needs of the target market. It is an effective technique to differentiate in saturated markets and to capture the attention of specific consumer segments.

Key Concepts

  • Specific Benefits: Highlight the concrete advantages offered by the product or service.
  • Practical Applications: Show how the product or service can be used in real situations.
  • Market Segmentation: Identify and target specific groups of consumers who will benefit most from the use of the product or service.
  • Differentiation: Create a unique value proposition that distinguishes the product or service from the competition.

Examples of Positioning Based on Use or Application

Example 1: Project Management Software

Project management software can be positioned in different ways according to its use or application:

  • For Small Teams: "Our software facilitates collaboration in small teams, allowing efficient task management and smooth communication."
  • For Large Corporations: "Optimize large-scale project management with our advanced tracking and analysis tools."

Example 2: Web Development Tools

Web development tools can be positioned according to the needs of different types of developers:

  • For Beginners: "Our platform offers an intuitive and easy-to-use environment, perfect for those starting in web development."
  • For Advanced Developers: "Leverage our advanced and customizable features to create robust and scalable web applications."

Implementation Strategies

  1. Identification of Key Benefits:

    • Conduct market studies to understand which benefits consumers value most.
    • Create marketing messages that highlight these benefits.

  2. Development of Use Cases:

    • Create detailed scenarios showing how the product or service can be used in specific situations.
    • Use customer testimonials and case studies to validate these scenarios.

  3. Market Segmentation:

    • Divide the market into segments based on consumer needs and behaviors.
    • Target specific marketing campaigns to each segment.

  4. Product Differentiation:

    • Identify unique features of the product or service that differentiate it from the competition.
    • Communicate these features clearly and consistently across all marketing channels.

Practical Exercise

Exercise 1: Identification of Benefits and Applications

  1. Choose a product or service you are familiar with.
  2. List at least three specific benefits offered by this product or service.
  3. Describe two practical applications for each listed benefit.
  4. Create a marketing message for each practical application.

Example of Solved Exercise

Product: Fitness Application

Specific Benefits:

  • Improves cardiovascular health.
  • Facilitates weight loss.
  • Increases motivation through challenges and rewards.

Practical Applications:

  • Improves cardiovascular health: "Perform personalized workouts that adapt to your fitness level and improve your cardiovascular health."
  • Facilitates weight loss: "Follow diet and exercise plans designed to help you lose weight effectively."
  • Increases motivation: "Participate in weekly challenges and earn rewards for reaching your fitness goals."

Marketing Messages:

  • "Transform your cardiovascular health with personalized workouts."
  • "Lose weight effectively with our diet and exercise plans."
  • "Keep your motivation high with weekly challenges and rewards."

Conclusion

Positioning based on use or application is a powerful strategy to highlight the specific benefits and practical applications of a product or service. By identifying and clearly communicating these aspects, companies can differentiate themselves in the market and attract specific consumer segments. Implementing this strategy requires a deep understanding of market needs and effective communication of the product or service's benefits and applications.
