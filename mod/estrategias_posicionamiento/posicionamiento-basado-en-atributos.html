Attribute-based positioning is a marketing strategy that focuses on highlighting specific characteristics of a product or service to differentiate it from the competition. This technique is especially useful when consumers value certain attributes over others when making purchasing decisions.

Key Concepts

  • Product Attributes: Specific characteristics that define a product, such as quality, price, design, functionality, etc.
  • Differentiation: The process of distinguishing a product from others in the market by promoting unique attributes.
  • Market Segmentation: Identifying specific groups of consumers who value certain attributes.
  • Value Proposition: A statement that explains how a product solves a customer's problem or improves their situation, highlighting key attributes.

Attribute-Based Positioning Strategies

  1. Focus on a Main Attribute:
    • Example: Volvo positions itself in the automotive market as the safest brand.
  2. Combine Multiple Attributes:
    • Example: Apple highlights both the sleek design and advanced functionality of its products.
  3. Comparison with Competitors:
    • Example: Avis positions itself as the car rental company that "tries harder" compared to Hertz.

Practical Examples

Example 1: Positioning a Smartphone

Suppose a company wants to position its new smartphone in a saturated market. The key attributes could be:

  • Battery Life: 48 hours of continuous use.
  • Camera Quality: 108 MP camera with artificial intelligence technology.
  • Price: More affordable than high-end competitors.
| Attribute           | Competitor A  | Competitor B  | Our Product      |
|---------------------|---------------|---------------|------------------|
| Battery Life        | 24 hours      | 36 hours      | 48 hours         |
| Camera Quality      | 64 MP         | 108 MP        | 108 MP + AI      |
| Price               | $999          | $1099         | $899             |

Example 2: Positioning a Project Management Software

For a project management software, the key attributes could be:

  • Ease of Use: Intuitive and easy-to-learn interface.
  • Integrations: Compatible with other popular tools like Slack and Trello.
  • Customer Support: 24/7 support with fast response times.
| Attribute           | Competitor A  | Competitor B  | Our Product      |
|---------------------|---------------|---------------|------------------|
| Ease of Use         | Medium        | High          | Very High        |
| Integrations        | Limited       | Extensive     | Extensive        |
| Customer Support    | 9-5, M-F      | 24/7          | 24/7             |

Exercises

  1. Identification of Attributes:

    • Choose a product or service you are familiar with and identify at least three key attributes that could be used for its positioning.

  2. Competitor Comparison:

    • Create a comparative table of the key attributes of your chosen product against two main competitors.

  3. Value Proposition:

    • Write a value proposition for the product or service based on the identified attributes.

Conclusion

Attribute-based positioning is an effective strategy to differentiate a product in a competitive market. By focusing on specific characteristics that are valued by consumers, companies can create a unique and attractive value proposition. The key is to identify the most relevant attributes and clearly communicate how these attributes benefit the consumer.
