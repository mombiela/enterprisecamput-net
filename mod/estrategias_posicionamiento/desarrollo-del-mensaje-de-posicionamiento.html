The development of the positioning message is a crucial part of any positioning strategy. This message must clearly communicate the brand's value proposition and differentiate it from the competition. In this section, you will learn how to create an effective positioning message that resonates with your target audience.

Key Concepts of the Positioning Message

  • Value Proposition: The main reason a customer should choose your product or service over the competition.
  • Target Audience: The specific group of consumers you are targeting.
  • Key Differentiators: Unique features of your product or service that set you apart from the competition.
  • Key Benefits: The main advantages your product or service offers to customers.

Components of the Positioning Message

To develop an effective positioning message, you should consider the following components:

  1. Who: Clearly define who your target audience is.
  2. What: Describe what your product or service is.
  3. How: Explain how your product or service meets the needs of your audience.
  4. Why: Justify why your audience should choose your product or service over others.

Example of a Positioning Message

Below is an example of a positioning message for a fictitious project management software company:

For software development teams that need an efficient solution to manage projects, our software ProjectMaster offers an intuitive and collaborative platform that improves productivity and communication. Unlike other project management tools, ProjectMaster integrates time tracking and performance analysis functionalities, allowing teams to deliver projects on time and within budget.

Breakdown of the Example

  • Who: Software development teams.
  • What: Project management software.
  • How: Intuitive and collaborative platform that improves productivity and communication.
  • Why: Integrates time tracking and performance analysis functionalities, allowing teams to deliver projects on time and within budget.

Conclusion

The development of a clear and effective positioning message is essential to differentiate your brand in the market. By clearly defining who your audience is, what you offer, how you do it, and why they should choose you, you can create a message that resonates with your customers and sets you apart from the competition. Practice these concepts and apply the exercises to perfect your skill in creating effective positioning messages.
