Competence-based positioning is a marketing strategy that focuses on differentiating your product or service in relation to direct competitors. This strategy involves thoroughly analyzing the market and competitors' offerings to identify differentiation opportunities and stand out in areas where competitors are weaker.

Key Concepts

  • Competitor Analysis: Assessing the strengths and weaknesses of direct competitors.
  • Differentiation: Creating a unique value proposition that stands out against the competition.
  • Market Segmentation: Identifying and focusing on market segments where competition is less intense.
  • Benchmarking: Comparing your products, services, and processes with those of competitors to identify areas for improvement.
  • Value Proposition: Developing an offer that resonates more with customer needs and desires compared to the competition.

Steps to Implement Competence-Based Positioning

  1. Identification of Competitors:

    • Direct: Those offering similar products or services.
    • Indirect: Those satisfying the same customer need differently.

  2. Competitor Analysis:

    • Products and Services: Compare features, prices, quality, and benefits.
    • Marketing Strategies: Evaluate advertising campaigns, social media presence, and sales tactics.
    • Strengths and Weaknesses: Identify areas where competitors are strong and where they are vulnerable.

  3. Development of the Value Proposition:

    • Differentiation: Identify unique features of your product or service.
    • Key Benefits: Focus on benefits that are most important to customers and that competitors do not offer.

  4. Market Segmentation:

    • Identification of Niches: Find market segments that are underserved by competitors.
    • Offer Customization: Adapt your product or service to meet the specific needs of these niches.

  5. Implementation and Monitoring:

    • Marketing Strategies: Develop campaigns that clearly communicate your differentiated value proposition.
    • Continuous Monitoring: Regularly evaluate the performance of your strategy and make adjustments as necessary.

Practical Example

Suppose you have a company that manufactures bicycles and you want to position yourself against your competitors.

Step 1: Identification of Competitors

  • Direct Competitors: Other high-end bicycle brands.
  • Indirect Competitors: Companies selling home exercise equipment.

Step 2: Competitor Analysis

  • Products and Services: | Competitor | Features | Price | Quality | Benefits | |------------|----------|-------|--------|----------| | Brand A | Lightweight bikes, aerodynamic design | $1500 | High | Speed, efficiency | | Brand B | Robust bikes, advanced suspension | $1200 | Medium | Durability, comfort |

  • Marketing Strategies:

    • Brand A: Focus on performance and speed.
    • Brand B: Focus on durability and comfort.

Step 3: Development of the Value Proposition

  • Differentiation: Bikes with electric assistance technology to facilitate pedaling on difficult terrains.
  • Key Benefits: Greater accessibility for cyclists of all ages and fitness levels.

Step 4: Market Segmentation

  • Identification of Niches: Urban cyclists looking for an eco-friendly and easy-to-use option for city commuting.
  • Offer Customization: Electric bikes with compact design and long-lasting batteries.

Step 5: Implementation and Monitoring

  • Marketing Strategies: Social media campaigns highlighting ease of use and ecological benefits.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Customer surveys and sales analysis to adjust the strategy as necessary.

Conclusion

Competence-based positioning is an effective strategy to differentiate your product or service in a saturated market. By identifying and analyzing your competitors, developing a unique value proposition, and adequately segmenting the market, you can stand out in areas where competitors are weaker and attract customers looking for something different. Continuously monitoring and adjusting your strategy will allow you to maintain a competitive advantage in the long term.
