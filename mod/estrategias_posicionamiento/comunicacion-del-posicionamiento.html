Introduction

Positioning communication is a crucial stage in the implementation of a positioning strategy. It is about how to effectively convey the positioning of a brand, product, or service to the target market. Clear and coherent communication ensures that the message reaches consumers correctly and that they perceive the differential value that is intended to be highlighted.

Key Concepts

  • Central Message: The core of what you want to communicate. It must be clear, concise, and aligned with the desired positioning.
  • Target Audience: The specific group of consumers to whom the message is directed.
  • Communication Channels: The means through which the message will be transmitted (advertising, social media, public relations, etc.).
  • Consistency: Maintaining a coherent message across all consumer touchpoints.
  • Feedback: Evaluating how the message is received and adjusting communication as necessary.

Development of the Central Message

The central message should encapsulate the positioning in a way that is easily understandable and memorable for the target audience.

Example of a Central Message

For a premium coffee brand:

"Our premium coffee offers a unique experience with beans selected from the best farms in the world, guaranteeing exceptional flavor in every cup."

Identification of the Target Audience

Knowing the target audience is essential to adapt the message and choose the appropriate communication channels. The audience can be segmented by:

  • Demographics: Age, gender, income, education.
  • Geography: Geographic location.
  • Psychographics: Lifestyle, values, interests.
  • Behavior: Buying habits, brand loyalty.

Selection of Communication Channels

Choosing the right channels is essential to effectively reach the target audience. Here is a comparative table of some common channels:

Channel Advantages Disadvantages
TV Advertising Wide coverage, high visual impact Expensive, difficult segmentation
Social Media Precise segmentation, direct interaction Requires constant management, can be overwhelming
Email Marketing Personalization, precise measurement Can be considered spam
Public Relations Credibility, organic reach Difficult to control, long-term results
Online Advertising Advanced segmentation, real-time measurement High competition, ad blockers

Consistency in Communication

Consistency is key to building a solid and reliable brand image. This involves:

  • Visual Uniformity: Consistent use of logos, colors, and typography.
  • Verbal Uniformity: Consistent use of slogans, tone, and communication style.
  • Experience Uniformity: Ensuring that the customer experience is consistent across all touchpoints.

Feedback and Adjustments

It is important to monitor how the message is received and be willing to make adjustments. This may include:

  • Surveys and Direct Feedback: Collecting opinions directly from consumers.
  • Data Analysis: Evaluating performance metrics across different channels.
  • A/B Testing: Testing different versions of the message to see which resonates best.

Conclusion

Positioning communication is a dynamic process that requires a deep understanding of the target audience, a clear and consistent central message, and the selection of appropriate communication channels. Constant feedback and the willingness to adjust the strategy are essential to ensure that the positioning is communicated effectively and resonates with consumers.

By mastering these elements, brands can establish a strong and differentiated presence in the market, which is crucial for long-term success.
