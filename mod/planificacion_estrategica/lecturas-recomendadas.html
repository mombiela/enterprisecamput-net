Introduction

In this section, we will provide a list of recommended readings that will complement the course on Strategic Planning. These readings are selected to offer a deeper understanding of the concepts and practices discussed in the course. They include books, academic articles, and online resources that cover a variety of approaches and perspectives on strategic planning.

Books

  1. "The Art of Strategy" by Avinash K. Dixit and Barry J. Nalebuff

    • This book offers an accessible introduction to game theory and its application in business strategy. It is ideal for understanding how strategic decisions can influence the behavior of competitors and other market actors.

  2. "Good Strategy Bad Strategy" by Richard Rumelt

    • Rumelt distinguishes between good and bad strategy, providing clear and practical examples. This book is essential for learning how to identify and develop effective strategies.

  3. "Blue Ocean Strategy" by W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne

    • This book introduces the concept of "blue oceans," untapped markets where competition is irrelevant. It is a fundamental read for those interested in strategic innovation.

  4. "Competitive Strategy" by Michael E. Porter

    • A classic in the field of business strategy, this book presents Porter's five forces analysis and other essential tools for strategic planning.

Academic Articles

  1. "What is Strategy?" by Michael E. Porter (Harvard Business Review)

    • This article defines what strategy is and how it differs from operations. It is a key read for understanding the fundamentals of strategic planning.

  2. "The Core Competence of the Corporation" by C.K. Prahalad and Gary Hamel (Harvard Business Review)

    • The authors explore the concept of core competencies and how they can be used to build a sustainable competitive advantage.

  3. "Building Your Company’s Vision" by James C. Collins and Jerry I. Porras (Harvard Business Review)

    • This article discusses the importance of a clear vision and how it can guide long-term strategic planning.

Online Resources

  1. Coursera: "Foundations of Business Strategy"

    • An online course that covers the fundamentals of business strategy, including industry analysis and competitive strategy development.

  2. MIT OpenCourseWare: "Strategic Management"

    • Free course materials that include readings, assignments, and exams on strategic management.

  3. Harvard Business Review Online

    • A continuous source of articles and case studies on strategic planning and other related topics.

Comparison of Strategic Approaches

Strategic Approach Description Practical Example
Competitive Strategy Focuses on outperforming competitors in the market. Using Porter's five forces to analyze competition in an industry.
Blue Ocean Strategy Seeks to create new markets without competition. Developing an innovative product with no direct competitors.
Resource-Based Strategy Focuses on utilizing the company's internal competencies and resources. Identifying and exploiting the company's core competencies.

Conclusion

The recommended readings provide a solid foundation for understanding and applying the strategic planning concepts discussed in the course. By exploring these resources, students will be able to delve into the different approaches and tools they can use to develop effective strategies in their organizations. It is recommended to complement these readings with practice and application of the concepts in real situations to maximize learning and effectiveness.
