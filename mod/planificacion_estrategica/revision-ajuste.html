Introduction

The review and adjustment of strategies is a crucial phase in strategic planning. This process allows organizations to evaluate progress towards their goals and make necessary changes to stay on the right track. In this section, we will explore key concepts, methods, and tools to effectively carry out a review and adjustment of strategies.

Key Concepts

  • Performance Evaluation: Measurement of progress towards strategic goals.
  • Deviation Analysis: Identification of differences between expected and actual results.
  • Strategy Adjustment: Modification of strategies based on performance and deviation analysis.
  • Key Performance Indicators (KPI): Metrics used to evaluate the success of a strategy.
  • Continuous Feedback: Process of continuously receiving and acting on performance information.

Review Methods

Performance Evaluation

Performance evaluation involves measuring progress towards strategic goals using key performance indicators (KPIs). These indicators should be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART).

Example of KPI:

Strategic Objective KPI Goal
Increase sales Quarterly sales percentage increase 10% quarterly

Deviation Analysis

Deviation analysis focuses on identifying and understanding the differences between expected and actual results. This analysis helps determine the underlying causes of deviations and make informed decisions about necessary adjustments.

Steps for Deviation Analysis:

  1. Identify Deviations: Compare actual results with established objectives.
  2. Analyze Causes: Investigate the reasons behind the deviations.
  3. Develop Solutions: Propose corrective actions to address the identified causes.

Strategy Adjustment

Once deviations and their causes have been identified, it is necessary to adjust strategies to realign the course towards the objectives. This may involve changes in resources, processes, or even the objectives themselves.

Example of Strategy Adjustment:

If deviation analysis reveals that sales have not increased due to a lack of training in the sales team, a possible adjustment strategy would be to implement an intensive training program.

Tools for Review and Adjustment

  • Balanced Scorecard: A tool that allows organizations to measure and manage performance across multiple perspectives (financial, customer, internal processes, learning and growth).
  • SWOT Analysis: Evaluation of strengths, opportunities, weaknesses, and threats to adjust strategies based on the internal and external environment.
  • Performance Management Software: Platforms that facilitate the collection and analysis of performance data, allowing continuous review and quick adjustments.

Practical Example

Suppose a technology company has set the goal of increasing its market share by 15% in the next year. Using a Balanced Scorecard, the company can measure performance in several key areas:

| Perspective          | Objective                          | KPI                          | Goal               |
|----------------------|-----------------------------------|------------------------------|--------------------|
| Financial            | Increase revenue                  | Annual percentage increase   | 20% annually       |
| Customer             | Improve customer satisfaction     | Satisfaction index           | 90%                |
| Internal Processes   | Optimize development processes    | Development time             | Reduce by 10%      |
| Learning and Growth  | Train staff                       | Annual training hours        | 40 hours per employee |

At the end of the first quarter, the company reviews the KPIs and finds that the revenue increase is only 5%. A deviation analysis reveals that the main cause is the lack of trained sales personnel. As an adjustment, the company decides to increase training hours and hire an external consultant to improve the sales team's skills.

Conclusion

The review and adjustment of strategies is a dynamic and continuous process that allows organizations to stay aligned with their strategic objectives. Using methods such as performance evaluation, deviation analysis, and tools like the Balanced Scorecard, organizations can identify areas for improvement and make necessary adjustments to ensure long-term success.
