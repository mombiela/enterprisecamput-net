Creating Custom Territories
Introduction to Tableau Server ►

Creating custom territories in Tableau allows you to group geographic regions into meaningful areas for analysis. This can be particularly useful for sales territories, regional performance analysis, or any scenario where predefined geographic boundaries do not meet your needs.

Introduction to Custom Territories

Custom territories in Tableau are user-defined geographic areas that you create by grouping existing geographic regions. This feature is useful for:

  • Sales and marketing analysis.
  • Regional performance tracking.
  • Custom reporting requirements.

Steps to Create Custom Territories

1. Preparing Your Data

Before creating custom territories, ensure your data is clean and includes geographic fields such as state, city, or zip code.

  • Example Data Structure:

| State | City | Sales | |-------|-----------|-------| | CA | Los Angeles | 1000 | | CA | San Francisco | 1500 | | NY | New York | 2000 | | TX | Houston | 1200 |

2. Connecting to Your Data Source

  1. Open Tableau and connect to your data source.
  2. Drag your geographic field (e.g., State) to the "Rows" or "Columns" shelf.

3. Creating a Map View

  1. Drag the geographic field to the "Detail" shelf in the Marks card.
  2. Tableau will automatically generate a map view.

4. Grouping Geographic Regions

  1. In the map view, select the geographic regions you want to group.
  2. Right-click and select "Group" from the context menu.
  • Example:
Select California (CA) and Texas (TX) on the map.
Right-click and choose "Group".
  1. Rename the group to a meaningful name, such as "West Coast".

5. Using Custom Territories in Analysis

  1. Drag the newly created group field to the "Color" shelf to visualize the custom territories.
  2. Use the custom territories in your analysis by dragging them into the "Rows" or "Columns" shelf.
  • Example:
Drag "West Coast" to the "Color" shelf to see the grouped regions highlighted.

Advanced Custom Territory Techniques

Using Calculated Fields

You can create more complex custom territories using calculated fields.

  • Example:
IF [State] = 'CA' OR [State] = 'TX' THEN 'West Coast'
ELSE 'Other'
END
  1. Create a calculated field with the above formula.
  2. Use this calculated field to group your data.

Combining Multiple Geographic Fields

You can create custom territories by combining multiple geographic fields, such as State and City.

  • Example:
IF [State] = 'CA' AND [City] = 'Los Angeles' THEN 'LA Territory'
ELSEIF [State] = 'NY' AND [City] = 'New York' THEN 'NY Territory'
ELSE 'Other'
END
  1. Create a calculated field with the above formula.
  2. Use this calculated field to define more granular custom territories.

Conclusion

Creating custom territories in Tableau is a powerful way to tailor geographic analysis to your specific needs. By grouping geographic regions, using calculated fields, and combining multiple geographic fields, you can create meaningful and actionable insights. Practice these techniques with your data to become proficient in custom territory creation.

Remember:

  • Always ensure your data is clean and well-structured.
  • Use grouping and calculated fields to define custom territories.
  • Leverage custom territories in your analysis to gain deeper insights.

By mastering these techniques, you can enhance your geographic analysis and make more informed decisions based on your custom-defined regions.
