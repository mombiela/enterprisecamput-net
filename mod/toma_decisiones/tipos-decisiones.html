In the decision-making process, it is essential to understand the different types of decisions that can be faced in a work environment. Each type of decision has its own characteristics and requires a specific approach to be effectively addressed. Below, we will explore the main types of decisions that professionals may encounter in their daily activities.

Programmed Decisions

Programmed decisions are those that are made routinely and repetitively. These decisions are usually supported by previously established policies, procedures, or rules.

  • Characteristics:

    • They occur frequently.
    • They have a standard procedure for resolution.
    • They require less time and effort to be made.

  • Examples:

    • Approval of vacation requests.
    • Restocking inventory when it reaches a minimum level.
    • Processing recurring invoices.

Non-Programmed Decisions

Non-programmed decisions are those that occur sporadically and do not have an established procedure for resolution. These decisions are usually more complex and require deeper analysis.

  • Characteristics:

    • They occur unexpectedly.
    • They do not have a standard procedure.
    • They require detailed analysis and creativity for resolution.

  • Examples:

    • Responding to a business crisis.
    • Deciding on entering a new market.
    • Implementing new technology in the company.

Strategic Decisions

Strategic decisions are those that have a long-term impact on the organization. These decisions are usually made by the highest levels of the organizational hierarchy and are aimed at achieving the company's long-term objectives.

  • Characteristics:

    • They have a long-term impact.
    • They affect the entire organization.
    • They require a broad vision and thorough analysis.

  • Examples:

    • Defining the company's mission and vision.
    • Deciding on mergers and acquisitions.
    • Establishing strategic alliances.

Tactical Decisions

Tactical decisions are those made to implement the defined strategies. These decisions usually have a medium-term impact and are made by the intermediate levels of the organization.

  • Characteristics:

    • They have a medium-term impact.
    • They implement the defined strategies.
    • They require coordination and planning.

  • Examples:

    • Planning marketing campaigns.
    • Allocating resources to specific projects.
    • Developing training plans for employees.

Operational Decisions

Operational decisions are those made on a daily basis to ensure the efficient functioning of the organization. These decisions usually have a short-term impact and are made by the operational levels of the organization.

  • Characteristics:

    • They have a short-term impact.
    • They ensure daily functioning.
    • They require speed and efficiency.

  • Examples:

    • Scheduling work shifts.
    • Resolving immediate technical problems.
    • Managing daily workflow.

Conclusion

Understanding the different types of decisions is crucial for improving decision-making effectiveness within an organization. Each type of decision requires a specific approach and set of skills. By identifying the type of decision being faced, professionals can apply the appropriate strategies to make informed and effective decisions, thereby contributing to the success and efficiency of the organization.
