Introduction

The Decision Tree is a visual and analytical tool that helps make complex decisions in a structured manner. It is used to map possible outcomes, costs, utilities, and probabilities of different options. This technique is especially useful in work environments where decisions need to be justified and data-driven.

Key Concepts

  • Decision Node: Represents a point where a decision must be made. It is drawn as a square.
  • Chance Node: Represents a point where a random event occurs. It is drawn as a circle.
  • Branch: Represents a possible action or outcome following a node. It is drawn as a line.
  • Leaf: Represents the final outcome of a series of decisions and events. It is drawn as a triangle.

Steps to Create a Decision Tree

  1. Identify the decision to be made: Clearly define the problem or decision you need to analyze.
  2. Draw the decision node: Place a square at the starting point of your diagram.
  3. Add branches for each option: Draw lines coming out of the decision node, each representing a different option.
  4. Add chance nodes: At the ends of the branches, place circles to represent random events that may occur.
  5. Continue the process: Repeat the previous steps until all possible decisions and events are represented.
  6. Assign values and probabilities: Add costs, utilities, and probabilities to each branch.
  7. Calculate the expected value: For each decision node, calculate the expected value to determine the best option.

Practical Example

Suppose a company must decide whether to launch a new product. The options are: launch the product or not launch it. If launched, there are two possible outcomes: success or failure.

| Decision | Random Event | Probability | Utility | |-------------------|------------------|-------------|----------| | Launch Product | Success | 0.7 | $100,000 | | | Failure | 0.3 | -$50,000 | | Do Not Launch | N/A | 1.0 | $0 |

Simplified Decision Tree

  1. Decision Node: Launch or not launch the product.

  2. Branches:

    • Launch Product
      • Chance Node: Success (70%) or Failure (30%)
    • Do Not Launch

  3. Outcomes:

    • Success: $100,000
    • Failure: -$50,000
    • Do Not Launch: $0

Expected Value Calculation

  • Launch Product: (0.7 * $100,000) + (0.3 * -$50,000) = $70,000 - $15,000 = $55,000
  • Do Not Launch: $0

Since the expected value of launching the product ($55,000) is greater than not launching it ($0), the best decision would be to launch the product.

Conclusion

The Decision Tree is a powerful tool for visualizing and analyzing complex decisions. By breaking down a decision into its basic components and evaluating the probabilities and utilities of each outcome, a more informed and justified decision can be made. This technique not only facilitates decision-making but also provides a solid basis for justifying the decisions made to other stakeholders.
