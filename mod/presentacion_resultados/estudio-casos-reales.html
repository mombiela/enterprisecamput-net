Introduction

The study of real cases is a powerful tool for learning and improving results presentation. Analyzing how other organizations or individuals have presented their results can provide valuable lessons and strategies that can be applied in different work contexts.

Objectives of the Study of Real Cases

  • Identify good practices: Recognize effective techniques used in successful presentations.
  • Learn from mistakes: Analyze less successful presentations to avoid making the same mistakes.
  • Apply knowledge: Adapt the learned strategies to one's own presentations.

Case Selection

For this course, we have selected a variety of cases that cover different industries and types of presentations:

  1. Case 1: Presentation of Financial Results in a Multinational
  2. Case 2: Progress Report of a Technological Project
  3. Case 3: Presentation of Research Results at a Scientific Conference
  4. Case 4: Social Impact Report of an NGO

Case Analysis

Case 1: Presentation of Financial Results in a Multinational

  • Context: A multinational presents its quarterly results to shareholders.
  • Strategies Used:
    • Use of charts and tables to illustrate financial data.
    • Clear narrative that contextualizes the numbers.
    • Anticipation of questions and preparation of answers.
  • Lessons Learned:
    • Data visualization is crucial for understanding.
    • It is important to tell a coherent story that connects the data.
    • Preparing for difficult questions improves confidence and credibility.

Case 2: Progress Report of a Technological Project

  • Context: A software development team presents the progress of a project to stakeholders.
  • Strategies Used:
    • Use of prototypes and live demonstrations.
    • Comparison of achieved milestones with the initial plan.
    • Inclusion of user feedback to show impact.
  • Lessons Learned:
    • Practical demonstrations can be more effective than theoretical descriptions.
    • Comparing progress with the initial plan helps maintain transparency.
    • Including user feedback adds value and credibility.

Case 3: Presentation of Research Results at a Scientific Conference

  • Context: A researcher presents their findings at an international conference.
  • Strategies Used:
    • Use of slides with clear charts and diagrams.
    • Detailed explanation of the methodology used.
    • Summary of conclusions and suggestions for future research.
  • Lessons Learned:
    • Visual clarity is essential for understanding complex data.
    • Explaining the methodology strengthens the credibility of the results.
    • Summarizing conclusions and suggestions provides an effective closure.

Case 4: Social Impact Report of an NGO

  • Context: An NGO presents its annual social impact report to donors and partners.
  • Strategies Used:
    • Use of personal stories and testimonials.
    • Presentation of impact statistics and charts.
    • Proposal of future plans based on the obtained results.
  • Lessons Learned:
    • Personal stories can be very persuasive.
    • Combining statistics with testimonials offers a complete view.
    • Proposing future plans shows commitment and long-term vision.

Comparison of Strategies

Conclusion

The study of real cases in results presentation allows us to identify effective strategies and learn from the mistakes of others. By applying these lessons to our own presentations, we can significantly improve our ability to communicate results clearly, persuasively, and effectively.
