Introduction

Working under pressure is a crucial skill in many work environments. Self-assessment and feedback are essential tools for improving this competency. This topic will guide you through key concepts and provide practical exercises to strengthen your ability to work effectively under pressure.

Importance of Self-Assessment

Self-assessment allows professionals to identify their strengths and areas for improvement. It is an introspective process that helps to:

  • Recognize behavior patterns: Identify how you react under pressure.
  • Evaluate performance: Measure your performance in stressful situations.
  • Set goals: Define clear objectives to improve your ability to handle pressure.

Methods of Self-Assessment

There are various techniques for conducting an effective self-assessment:

  • Work diaries: Record your daily experiences and reflect on them.
  • Self-assessment questionnaires: Answer specific questions about your performance.
  • Personal SWOT analysis: Identify your Strengths, Opportunities, Weaknesses, and Threats.

Practical Exercise: Work Diary

  1. Daily Record: Note the pressure situations you face each day.
  2. Reflection: Reflect on how you handled each situation.
  3. Pattern Identification: Look for patterns in your behavior and performance.

Importance of Feedback

External feedback provides an objective perspective on your performance. It is essential for:

  • Validating self-assessment: Confirming or adjusting your personal perceptions.
  • Receiving practical advice: Getting specific suggestions for improvement.
  • Fostering professional growth: Developing skills through constructive criticism.

Sources of Feedback

Feedback can come from various sources:

  • Supervisors: Offer a global view of your performance.
  • Coworkers: Provide a lateral and collaborative perspective.
  • Comparative self-assessment: Compare your self-assessments with evaluations from others.

Practical Exercise: Requesting Feedback

  1. Identify sources: Select the people from whom you want to receive feedback.
  2. Formulate specific questions: Ask about specific aspects of your performance under pressure.
  3. Analyze the feedback: Reflect on the comments received and how you can apply them.

Comparison of Self-Assessment and Feedback

| Aspect | Self-Assessment | Feedback | |-----------------------|-------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------| | Perspective | Internal, subjective | External, objective | | Frequency | Continuous, at the individual's discretion| Periodic, according to others' availability | | Tools | Diaries, questionnaires, SWOT analysis | Meetings, surveys, formal evaluations | | Benefits | Self-awareness, self-improvement | Validation, practical advice |

Conclusion

Self-assessment and feedback are essential components for improving your ability to work under pressure. Self-assessment allows you to identify your own strengths and weaknesses, while feedback offers an external and objective perspective. By combining both tools, you can develop effective strategies to handle stressful situations and improve your professional performance.

Remember that constant practice and openness to receiving constructive criticism are keys to continuous growth. Start implementing these techniques today and see how your ability to work under pressure improves!
