Introduction

In this section, we will focus on practical exercises designed to help you apply the skills necessary to work effectively under pressure. These exercises will allow you to put into practice what you have learned and develop strategies to handle stressful situations in the workplace.

Exercise 1: Crisis Simulation

Objective

Develop the ability to make quick and effective decisions in high-pressure situations.

Instructions

  1. Form teams: Divide participants into teams of 4-5 people.
  2. Crisis scenario: Provide each team with a fictitious crisis scenario related to their work area. For example, a critical technical failure, an important dissatisfied customer, or an imminent deadline.
  3. Time limit: Set a time limit of 15 minutes for each team to discuss and propose a solution.
  4. Presentation: Each team must present their solution in 5 minutes, explaining the decisions made and the reasons behind them.

Evaluation

  • Effectiveness of the solution: Does the proposed solution effectively resolve the problem?
  • Teamwork: How were tasks distributed and communication managed within the team?
  • Time management: Was the time used efficiently?

Exercise 2: Prioritizing Tasks Under Pressure

Objective

Improve the ability to prioritize tasks when facing multiple simultaneous demands.

Instructions

  1. Task list: Provide a list of 10-15 tasks that need to be completed in a typical workday. Make sure to include tasks of different levels of urgency and importance.
  2. Classification: Ask participants to classify the tasks in order of priority, justifying their decisions.
  3. Group discussion: Gather participants to discuss their classifications and the reasons behind their decisions.

Evaluation

  • Justification of priorities: Are the priorities well justified based on the urgency and importance of the tasks?
  • Consistency: Is there consistency in the priorities set by different participants?

Exercise 3: Quick Relaxation Techniques

Objective

Learn and practice quick relaxation techniques to reduce stress in high-pressure moments.

Instructions

  1. Introduction to techniques: Briefly explain several quick relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing, positive visualization, and progressive muscle relaxation.
  2. Guided practice: Guide participants through a 5-minute practical session for each technique.
  3. Practical application: Ask participants to practice one of the techniques during a high-pressure moment during the week and record their experiences.

Evaluation

  • Perceived effectiveness: Which technique was most effective for each participant?
  • Frequency of use: How often did they use the techniques during the week?

Conclusion

These exercises are designed to help you apply and strengthen your skills to work under pressure. Through crisis simulation, task prioritization, and the practice of relaxation techniques, you will be able to develop effective strategies to manage stress and maintain high performance in challenging situations. Remember that constant practice and reflection on your experiences are key to improving these skills.
