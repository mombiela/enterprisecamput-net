Introduction

Review and feedback are essential components in the development of system architectures. This process not only ensures the quality of the design but also facilitates the early identification of problems and the implementation of continuous improvements. In this section, we will explore best practices for conducting effective reviews and providing constructive feedback.

Importance of Review and Feedback

  • Design Quality: Ensures that the architecture meets functional and non-functional requirements.
  • Early Error Detection: Identifies issues before they become costly errors in later stages.
  • Continuous Improvement: Facilitates the constant evolution and improvement of the architecture.
  • Team Collaboration: Promotes communication and collaboration among team members.

Types of Review

Formal Review

  • Description: Involves a structured process with defined roles and formal documentation.
  • Participants: Architects, developers, business analysts, and other stakeholders.
  • Example Process:
    1. Preparation: Distribute documents and set the agenda.
    2. Review: Conduct the review meeting following the agenda.
    3. Documentation: Record findings and decisions.
    4. Follow-up: Implement corrective actions and verify their effectiveness.

Informal Review

  • Description: Less structured, can be an ad-hoc discussion among team members.
  • Participants: Generally developers and architects.
  • Example Process:
    1. Discussion: Meet to discuss specific aspects of the architecture.
    2. Feedback: Provide immediate and direct feedback.
    3. Action: Implement changes based on the discussion.

Feedback Techniques

  • Constructive Feedback: Focus on specific aspects and provide clear suggestions for improvement.
  • Positive Feedback: Recognize and reinforce well-designed aspects.
  • Balanced Feedback: Combine positive observations and areas for improvement to maintain team motivation.

Example of Architecture Review

Context

Suppose we are reviewing a microservices architecture for an e-commerce application.

Architecture Document

services:
  - name: user-service
    endpoints:
      - /users
      - /users/{id}
  - name: product-service
    endpoints:
      - /products
      - /products/{id}
  - name: order-service
    endpoints:
      - /orders
      - /orders/{id}

Review Process

  1. Preparation: Distribute the YAML document and schedule a meeting.
  2. Review:
    • User Service: Verify if the endpoints cover all use cases.
    • Product Service: Ensure that the data structure is adequate.
    • Order Service: Review the consistency and integrity of transactions.
  3. Documentation: Record any findings, such as missing endpoints or consistency issues.
  4. Follow-up: Implement necessary corrections and conduct a follow-up review.

Conclusion

Review and feedback are critical processes in the development of system architectures. By implementing formal and informal reviews and providing constructive feedback, teams can ensure quality, detect problems early, and foster a culture of continuous improvement. By following these practices, a robust and efficient architecture that meets business and technical requirements can be achieved.
