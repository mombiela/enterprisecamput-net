Basic Transformations: Move, Rotate, Scale
Using Modifiers ►

Introduction

In Blender, mastering the basic transformations—Move, Rotate, and Scale—is essential for any 3D artist. These transformations allow you to manipulate objects in your scene, positioning them precisely where you want them. This section will cover these fundamental operations, providing clear explanations, examples, and exercises to help you understand and apply these transformations effectively.

Move (Translate)

Moving an object changes its position in the 3D space.

Key Concepts

  • Axes: X (red), Y (green), and Z (blue) axes represent the 3D space dimensions.
  • Global vs. Local: Global transformations affect the object in the world space, while local transformations affect the object relative to its own orientation.

How to Move Objects

  1. Using the Gizmo:

    • Select the object.
    • Click and drag the arrows of the gizmo to move the object along the X, Y, or Z axis.

  2. Using Keyboard Shortcuts:

    • Press G to grab (move) the object.
    • Press X, Y, or Z immediately after G to constrain the movement to the respective axis.
    • Hold Shift and press X, Y, or Z to exclude an axis from the movement.

Example

1. Select the cube.
2. Press `G` to move the cube.
3. Press `X` to constrain the movement to the X axis.
4. Move the mouse to position the cube and click to confirm.

Exercise

  1. Create a new cube.
  2. Move the cube 2 units along the X axis.
  3. Move the cube 3 units along the Y axis, but not along the Z axis.

Rotate

Rotating an object changes its orientation.

Key Concepts

  • Pivot Point: The point around which the object rotates.
  • Euler Angles: Common method to represent rotations using three angles (X, Y, Z).

How to Rotate Objects

  1. Using the Gizmo:

    • Select the object.
    • Click and drag the circular handles of the gizmo to rotate the object around the X, Y, or Z axis.

  2. Using Keyboard Shortcuts:

    • Press R to rotate the object.
    • Press X, Y, or Z immediately after R to constrain the rotation to the respective axis.
    • Press R twice to enter trackball rotation mode.

Example

1. Select the cube.
2. Press `R` to rotate the cube.
3. Press `Z` to constrain the rotation to the Z axis.
4. Move the mouse to rotate the cube and click to confirm.

Exercise

  1. Create a new cube.
  2. Rotate the cube 45 degrees around the Y axis.
  3. Rotate the cube 30 degrees around the X axis.

Scale

Scaling an object changes its size.

Key Concepts

  • Uniform Scaling: Scaling equally in all directions.
  • Non-Uniform Scaling: Scaling differently along different axes.

How to Scale Objects

  1. Using the Gizmo:

    • Select the object.
    • Click and drag the handles of the gizmo to scale the object along the X, Y, or Z axis.

  2. Using Keyboard Shortcuts:

    • Press S to scale the object.
    • Press X, Y, or Z immediately after S to constrain the scaling to the respective axis.
    • Hold Shift and press X, Y, or Z to exclude an axis from the scaling.

Example

1. Select the cube.
2. Press `S` to scale the cube.
3. Press `Y` to constrain the scaling to the Y axis.
4. Move the mouse to scale the cube and click to confirm.

Exercise

  1. Create a new cube.
  2. Scale the cube to twice its size along the Z axis.
  3. Scale the cube to half its size along the X axis.

Summary

Understanding and mastering the basic transformations—Move, Rotate, and Scale—are crucial for manipulating objects in Blender. These operations form the foundation of 3D modeling and animation. By practicing these transformations, you will gain better control over your 3D scenes and improve your overall efficiency in Blender. Remember to use the gizmo for visual guidance and keyboard shortcuts for quick transformations.
