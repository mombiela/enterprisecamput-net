Message and Moral
Next ►

Introduction

In storytelling, the message and moral are the core takeaways that the audience is meant to understand and internalize. These elements provide depth and purpose to the story, making it more than just an entertaining narrative. They help to convey values, lessons, and insights that resonate with the audience on a personal level.

Key Concepts

  1. Definition of Message and Moral

  • Message: The central idea or theme that the story communicates. It is the underlying point or argument that the storyteller wants to convey.
  • Moral: A specific lesson or principle that the story teaches. It often relates to ethical or moral conduct and is intended to guide the audience's behavior or beliefs.

  1. Importance of Message and Moral

  • Emotional Connection: Helps the audience connect emotionally with the story, making it more memorable and impactful.
  • Guidance and Reflection: Provides the audience with insights and lessons that can influence their thoughts and actions.
  • Brand Alignment: In marketing and branding, the message and moral should align with the brand's values and mission, reinforcing the brand identity.

Examples

Example 1: Classic Fable

  • Story: "The Tortoise and the Hare"
  • Message: Slow and steady wins the race.
  • Moral: Consistency and perseverance are more valuable than speed and arrogance.

Example 2: Marketing Campaign

  • Story: A commercial featuring a young athlete overcoming obstacles to achieve their dreams.
  • Message: Determination and hard work lead to success.
  • Moral: Never give up on your dreams, no matter the challenges.

Practical Application

Steps to Identify and Develop the Message and Moral

  1. Identify the Core Theme:

    • Determine the central idea you want to communicate.
    • Example: "Perseverance leads to success."

  2. Align with Audience Values:

    • Ensure the message resonates with the audience's beliefs and values.
    • Example: For a health-conscious audience, the message could be about the importance of maintaining a healthy lifestyle.

  3. Incorporate into the Narrative:

    • Weave the message and moral naturally into the story.
    • Example: Show characters facing challenges and learning the lesson through their experiences.

  4. Reinforce Through Resolution:

    • Ensure the story's resolution clearly reflects the message and moral.
    • Example: The protagonist achieves their goal through perseverance, reinforcing the message.

Practical Exercise

Exercise: Crafting a Message and Moral

Objective: Create a short story with a clear message and moral.

Instructions:

  1. Choose a Theme: Select a central theme for your story (e.g., honesty, kindness, perseverance).
  2. Develop Characters: Create characters that will help convey the message.
  3. Outline the Plot: Plan the beginning, middle, and end of your story.
  4. Identify the Message: Clearly define the message you want to communicate.
  5. Determine the Moral: Decide on the specific lesson you want the audience to learn.
  6. Write the Story: Craft a short story incorporating the elements above.

Example:

  • Theme: Kindness
  • Characters: A young girl, an elderly neighbor
  • Plot: The young girl helps her elderly neighbor with daily chores.
  • Message: Small acts of kindness can make a big difference.
  • Moral: Always be kind to others, as it can greatly impact their lives.

Solution Example

Story: Once upon a time, in a small village, there lived a young girl named Lily. Every day, she noticed her elderly neighbor, Mr. Thompson, struggling with his daily chores. One day, Lily decided to help him carry his groceries home. Mr. Thompson was very grateful and shared stories of his youth with Lily. Over time, Lily continued to help Mr. Thompson, and they formed a strong bond. The villagers noticed Lily's kindness and started helping each other more. The village became a happier place, all because of Lily's small acts of kindness.

Message: Small acts of kindness can make a big difference. Moral: Always be kind to others, as it can greatly impact their lives.

Conclusion

Understanding and effectively incorporating the message and moral into your storytelling can significantly enhance the impact of your narrative. Whether in marketing, branding, or personal communication, a well-crafted message and moral can leave a lasting impression on your audience, guiding their thoughts, emotions, and actions.
