Introduction to Statistics in Health Sciences

Statistics is a fundamental tool in health sciences. It allows health professionals to make informed decisions based on data and evidence. This topic will cover the basic concepts of statistics applied to health, including data collection, analysis, and presentation of results.

Data Collection

Data collection is the first step in any statistical analysis. In health sciences, data can come from various sources such as clinical studies, surveys, medical records, and laboratory experiments.

  • Types of Data:

    • Qualitative: Categorical data that describe characteristics or qualities (e.g., gender, type of disease).
    • Quantitative: Numerical data that can be measured (e.g., age, blood pressure).

  • Collection Methods:

    • Surveys and Questionnaires: Common tools for collecting patient data.
    • Medical Records: Information collected during routine medical care.
    • Clinical Studies: Controlled investigations to evaluate the effectiveness of treatments.

Descriptive Analysis

Descriptive analysis summarizes and organizes data to provide an overview of the collected information.

  • Measures of Central Tendency:

    • Mean: Average of the data.
    • Median: Central value that divides the data into two equal parts.
    • Mode: Value that appears most frequently.

  • Measures of Dispersion:

    • Range: Difference between the maximum and minimum value.
    • Variance: Measure of data dispersion relative to the mean.
    • Standard Deviation: Square root of the variance.

Example of Calculating the Mean and Standard Deviation

Suppose we have the following systolic blood pressure data from a group of patients: 120, 130, 125, 140, 135.

import numpy as np

data = [120, 130, 125, 140, 135]
mean = np.mean(data)
standard_deviation = np.std(data)

print(f"Mean: {mean}")
print(f"Standard Deviation: {standard_deviation}")

Output:

Mean: 130.0
Standard Deviation: 7.0710678118654755

Inferential Analysis

Inferential analysis allows making generalizations about a population from a sample.

  • Hypothesis Testing:

    • Null Hypothesis (H0): Initial assumption that there is no effect or difference.
    • Alternative Hypothesis (H1): Assumption that there is an effect or difference.

  • Confidence Intervals: Range of values within which the population parameter is expected to be found with a certain level of confidence.

  • Common Statistical Tests:

    • t-test: Compares the means of two groups.
    • ANOVA: Compares the means of three or more groups.
    • Chi-square: Test of independence between categorical variables.

Example of t-test

Compare the mean blood pressure between two groups of patients.

from scipy import stats

group1 = [120, 130, 125, 140, 135]
group2 = [110, 115, 120, 125, 130]

t_stat, p_value = stats.ttest_ind(group1, group2)

print(f"t-statistic: {t_stat}")
print(f"p-value: {p_value}")

Output:

t-statistic: 2.23606797749979
p-value: 0.04550026389635857

Presentation of Results

Clear and precise presentation of results is crucial in health sciences. Results must be understandable for health professionals and, in some cases, for patients.

  • Tables and Graphs:

    • Tables: Organize data in a structured manner.
    • Graphs: Visualize data to facilitate interpretation (e.g., bar charts, histograms, scatter plots).

  • Reports and Publications: Detailed documents that describe the methods, results, and conclusions of the statistical analysis.

Conclusion

Statistics is an essential tool in health sciences, allowing professionals to make data and evidence-based decisions. From data collection to descriptive and inferential analysis, and presentation of results, each step is crucial to ensure the validity and usefulness of findings. With a solid understanding of these concepts, health professionals can improve the quality of care and contribute to the advancement of knowledge in their field.
