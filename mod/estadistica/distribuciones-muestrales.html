Introduction to Sample Distributions

Sample distributions are fundamental in statistics, as they allow us to understand how the statistics of a sample behave in relation to the population from which they are drawn. This topic is crucial for making statistical inferences and data-driven decisions.

Key Concepts

  • Population: Complete set of elements or individuals being studied.
  • Sample: Subset of the population selected for the study.
  • Statistic: Measure calculated from a sample (e.g., sample mean).
  • Parameter: Measure calculated from a population (e.g., population mean).
  • Sample Distribution: Distribution of a statistic calculated from multiple samples of the same population.

Properties of Sample Distributions

  • Mean of the Sample Distribution: The mean of the sample distribution of a statistic is equal to the corresponding population parameter.
  • Variance of the Sample Distribution: The variance of the sample distribution of a statistic is less than the population variance and decreases as the sample size increases.

Example of Sample Distribution

Suppose we have a population with the following values: {2, 4, 6, 8, 10}. We want to study the sample distribution of the sample mean for samples of size 2.

  1. Possible Samples of Size 2:

    • (2, 4)
    • (2, 6)
    • (2, 8)
    • (2, 10)
    • (4, 6)
    • (4, 8)
    • (4, 10)
    • (6, 8)
    • (6, 10)
    • (8, 10)

  2. Sample Means:

    • Mean of (2, 4) = (2 + 4) / 2 = 3
    • Mean of (2, 6) = (2 + 6) / 2 = 4
    • Mean of (2, 8) = (2 + 8) / 2 = 5
    • Mean of (2, 10) = (2 + 10) / 2 = 6
    • Mean of (4, 6) = (4 + 6) / 2 = 5
    • Mean of (4, 8) = (4 + 8) / 2 = 6
    • Mean of (4, 10) = (4 + 10) / 2 = 7
    • Mean of (6, 8) = (6 + 8) / 2 = 7
    • Mean of (6, 10) = (6 + 10) / 2 = 8
    • Mean of (8, 10) = (8 + 10) / 2 = 9

  3. Sample Distribution of the Mean:

    • Possible values: {3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9}
    • Frequencies: {1, 1, 2, 2, 2, 1, 1}

| Sample Mean | Frequency | |-------------|-----------| | 3 | 1 | | 4 | 1 | | 5 | 2 | | 6 | 2 | | 7 | 2 | | 8 | 1 | | 9 | 1 |

Example Code in Python

Below is an example of how to calculate the sample distribution of the mean using Python:

import itertools
import numpy as np

# Population
population = [2, 4, 6, 8, 10]

# Generate all possible combinations of samples of size 2
samples = list(itertools.combinations(population, 2))

# Calculate the sample means
sample_means = [np.mean(sample) for sample in samples]

# Calculate the frequency of each sample mean
frequencies = {mean: sample_means.count(mean) for mean in set(sample_means)}

# Display results
print("Sample Means and their Frequencies:")
for mean, frequency in frequencies.items():
    print(f"Mean: {mean}, Frequency: {frequency}")

Conclusion

Sample distributions are a powerful tool in statistics, allowing analysts and data scientists to make inferences about populations from samples. Understanding how sample statistics behave and how they are distributed is essential for performing accurate analyses and making informed data-driven decisions.
