Introduction

The normal distribution, also known as the Gaussian distribution, is one of the most important probability distributions in statistics. It is characterized by its bell shape and symmetry around the mean. This distribution is fundamental in many areas of statistics and is used to model natural and social phenomena.

Characteristics of the Normal Distribution

  • Symmetry: The normal distribution is symmetric about its mean.
  • Mean, Median, and Mode: In a normal distribution, the mean, median, and mode are equal.
  • Gaussian Bell Curve: The shape of the distribution is a bell, with most of the data concentrated around the mean.
  • Standard Deviation: The standard deviation determines the width of the bell. A larger standard deviation results in a wider and lower bell, while a smaller standard deviation results in a narrower and higher bell.

Probability Density Function

The probability density function (PDF) of a normal distribution is defined as:

\[ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} \]

where:

  • \( \mu \) is the mean of the distribution.
  • \( \sigma \) is the standard deviation.
  • \( e \) is the base of the natural logarithm.

Properties of the Normal Distribution

  • Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7): Approximately 68% of the data is within one standard deviation of the mean, 95% within two standard deviations, and 99.7% within three standard deviations.
  • Asymptotic: The curve of the normal distribution approaches but never touches the horizontal axis.

Practical Example

Suppose we have a dataset that follows a normal distribution with a mean (\( \mu \)) of 50 and a standard deviation (\( \sigma \)) of 10. We want to calculate the probability that a random value \( X \) falls between 40 and 60.

Manual Calculation

  1. Convert to the standard normal distribution (Z-score): \[ Z = \frac{X - \mu}{\sigma} \]

    For \( X = 40 \): \[ Z = \frac{40 - 50}{10} = -1 \]

    For \( X = 60 \): \[ Z = \frac{60 - 50}{10} = 1 \]

  2. Use the Z table: We consult the Z table to find the probabilities corresponding to \( Z = -1 \) and \( Z = 1 \).

    • \( P(Z < 1) = 0.8413 \)
    • \( P(Z < -1) = 0.1587 \)

  3. Calculate the probability: \[ P(40 < X < 60) = P(Z < 1) - P(Z < -1) \] \[ P(40 < X < 60) = 0.8413 - 0.1587 = 0.6826 \]

Calculation with Python

We can use Python and the scipy.stats library to calculate this probability more efficiently.

import scipy.stats as stats

# Distribution parameters
mu = 50
sigma = 10

# Calculate the probability
probability = stats.norm.cdf(60, mu, sigma) - stats.norm.cdf(40, mu, sigma)
print(f"The probability that X is between 40 and 60 is: {probability:.4f}")

Conclusion

The normal distribution is a powerful tool in statistics due to its properties and its ability to model a wide variety of phenomena. Understanding its characteristics and how to calculate associated probabilities is fundamental for any professional working with data. In this topic, we have covered the basic properties of the normal distribution, how to calculate probabilities manually and through Python code.
