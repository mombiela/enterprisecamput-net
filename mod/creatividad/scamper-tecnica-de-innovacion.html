Introduction to SCAMPER

SCAMPER is a creativity and problem-solving technique that helps generate new ideas and innovative solutions. The acronym SCAMPER represents seven different techniques that can be applied to modify an existing product, service, or process. These techniques are: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, and Rearrange.

The Seven SCAMPER Techniques

Substitute

  • Description: Consists of replacing a part of the product or process with another.
  • Example: Substitute the material of a product to make it lighter or more durable.

Combine

  • Description: Involves uniting two or more elements to create something new.
  • Example: Combine functions of different devices into one, like a smartphone that is also a camera and a music player.

Adapt

  • Description: Consists of adjusting a product or process to fit a new context or need.
  • Example: Adapt a product designed for adults to make it safe and attractive for children.

Modify

  • Description: Involves changing the size, shape, color, or other characteristics of the product or process.
  • Example: Modify the design of a package to make it more ergonomic and easy to use.

Put to other uses

  • Description: Consists of finding new applications for an existing product or process.
  • Example: Use old tires as material to build playgrounds.

Eliminate

  • Description: Involves removing unnecessary elements to simplify the product or process.
  • Example: Eliminate redundant functions from software to make it more intuitive and easy to use.

Rearrange

  • Description: Consists of changing the order or arrangement of elements to improve efficiency or effectiveness.
  • Example: Rearrange the layout of a store to improve customer flow and increase sales.

Practical Exercise

To apply the SCAMPER technique, follow these steps:

  1. Select a product or process: Choose something you want to improve or innovate.
  2. Apply each SCAMPER technique: Go through each of the seven techniques and note all the ideas that come to mind.
  3. Evaluate the ideas: Review the generated ideas and select the most promising ones.
  4. Develop a plan: Create a plan to implement the selected ideas.

Example of Application

Let's imagine we want to innovate the design of a water bottle:

  • Substitute: Change the plastic to a biodegradable material.
  • Combine: Add an incorporated water filter.
  • Adapt: Design the bottle to be foldable to save space.
  • Modify: Change the shape of the bottle to make it easier to hold.
  • Put to other uses: Use the bottle as a weight for exercising.
  • Eliminate: Remove the paper label and engrave the information directly on the bottle.
  • Rearrange: Change the location of the cap to facilitate pouring.

Conclusion

The SCAMPER technique is a powerful tool to foster creativity and innovation in any work environment. By systematically applying the seven techniques, it is possible to generate a wide variety of ideas and solutions that can improve products, services, and processes. Practicing SCAMPER regularly can help develop a more creative mindset open to new possibilities.
