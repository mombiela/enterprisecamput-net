External Analysis: Market and Competition
Next ►

Understanding the external environment is crucial for developing a comprehensive communication strategy. This module will guide you through the process of analyzing the market and competition, which will help you identify opportunities and threats that could impact your communication efforts.

Key Concepts

Market Analysis

Market analysis involves examining the broader market environment in which your company operates. This includes understanding market trends, customer needs, and the overall economic landscape.

Competitive Analysis

Competitive analysis focuses on identifying and evaluating your competitors. This helps you understand their strengths and weaknesses, as well as their communication strategies.

SWOT Analysis

SWOT analysis is a strategic planning tool used to identify the Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats related to your business and its external environment.

Steps for Conducting External Analysis

1. Market Analysis

  1. Identify Market Trends:

    • Research current trends in your industry.
    • Use reports, market research studies, and industry publications.
    • Example: If you are in the tech industry, look for trends in AI, IoT, and cybersecurity.

  2. Understand Customer Needs:

    • Conduct surveys and focus groups.
    • Analyze customer feedback and reviews.
    • Example: Use tools like SurveyMonkey or Google Forms to gather customer insights.

  3. Economic Factors:

    • Consider the economic environment, including inflation rates, employment levels, and economic growth.
    • Example: Use data from government publications and economic reports.

2. Competitive Analysis

  1. Identify Key Competitors:

    • List your main competitors.
    • Example: If you are a coffee shop, your competitors might include Starbucks and local cafes.

  2. Evaluate Competitor Strategies:

    • Analyze their marketing and communication strategies.
    • Example: Review their social media presence, advertising campaigns, and public relations efforts.

  3. SWOT Analysis of Competitors:

    • Identify their strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
    • Example: A competitor might have a strong brand (strength) but poor customer service (weakness).

3. SWOT Analysis for Your Company

  1. Strengths:

    • Identify internal strengths that give you an advantage.
    • Example: Strong brand reputation, innovative products.

  2. Weaknesses:

    • Identify internal weaknesses that need improvement.
    • Example: Limited market reach, outdated technology.

  3. Opportunities:

    • Identify external opportunities that you can capitalize on.
    • Example: Emerging markets, technological advancements.

  4. Threats:

    • Identify external threats that could impact your business.
    • Example: New competitors, regulatory changes.

Practical Example

Case Study: Tech Startup

Imagine you are working for a tech startup that develops mobile applications. Here’s how you might conduct an external analysis:

  1. Market Analysis:

    • Trends: Increasing use of mobile apps for health and fitness.
    • Customer Needs: Users want apps that are easy to use and provide personalized experiences.
    • Economic Factors: Growing disposable income in target markets.

  2. Competitive Analysis:

    • Key Competitors: Established companies like MyFitnessPal and new startups.
    • Strategies: Competitors use influencer marketing and offer free trials.
    • SWOT Analysis:
      • Strengths: MyFitnessPal has a large user base.
      • Weaknesses: Some competitors have outdated user interfaces.

  3. SWOT Analysis for Your Company:

    • Strengths: Innovative app features, strong development team.
    • Weaknesses: Limited marketing budget.
    • Opportunities: Growing interest in health and fitness apps.
    • Threats: High competition in the app market.

Exercises

Exercise 1: Market Analysis

  1. Identify three current trends in your industry.
  2. Conduct a survey to understand customer needs.
  3. Research economic factors that could impact your business.

Exercise 2: Competitive Analysis

  1. List your top five competitors.
  2. Analyze their communication strategies.
  3. Conduct a SWOT analysis for one of your competitors.

Exercise 3: SWOT Analysis for Your Company

  1. Identify your company’s strengths and weaknesses.
  2. Identify external opportunities and threats.
  3. Create a SWOT matrix to visualize your findings.

Solutions

Exercise 1: Market Analysis

  1. Trends:
    • Example: Increasing demand for eco-friendly products.
  2. Survey:
    • Example: Use Google Forms to ask customers about their preferences for eco-friendly products.
  3. Economic Factors:
    • Example: Research reports on consumer spending trends in eco-friendly products.

Exercise 2: Competitive Analysis

  1. Competitors:
    • Example: List competitors such as EcoBrand, GreenGoods, and NatureFirst.
  2. Strategies:
    • Example: EcoBrand uses social media influencers, GreenGoods focuses on sustainability certifications.
  3. SWOT Analysis:
    • Example: EcoBrand has strong brand loyalty (strength) but limited product range (weakness).

Exercise 3: SWOT Analysis for Your Company

  1. Strengths:
    • Example: Strong R&D team, innovative products.
  2. Weaknesses:
    • Example: Limited distribution channels.
  3. Opportunities:
    • Example: Growing market for sustainable products.
  4. Threats:
    • Example: New regulations on product materials.

Conclusion

External analysis is a vital component of developing a communication strategy. By understanding the market and competition, you can identify opportunities and threats that will inform your communication efforts. This module has provided you with the tools and techniques to conduct a thorough external analysis, setting the foundation for a successful communication strategy.
