Objectives of the Communication Strategy
Next ►

In this section, we will explore the primary objectives of a communication strategy within a company. Understanding these objectives is crucial for developing a comprehensive communication plan that aligns with the company's strategic goals.

Key Objectives

  1. Enhance Brand Awareness

    • Definition: Increasing the visibility and recognition of the company's brand among target audiences.
    • Example: Launching a social media campaign to introduce a new product line.
    • Exercise: Identify three ways your company can enhance brand awareness through communication.

  2. Build and Maintain Relationships

    • Definition: Establishing and nurturing relationships with stakeholders, including customers, employees, investors, and partners.
    • Example: Regularly updating stakeholders through newsletters and meetings.
    • Exercise: List the key stakeholders for your company and describe one communication method to engage each group.

  3. Support Business Goals

    • Definition: Aligning communication efforts with the company's overall business objectives, such as increasing sales, entering new markets, or improving customer satisfaction.
    • Example: Creating targeted marketing campaigns to boost sales during a specific quarter.
    • Exercise: Choose one of your company's business goals and outline a communication strategy to support it.

  4. Manage Company Reputation

    • Definition: Protecting and enhancing the company's reputation through consistent and transparent communication.
    • Example: Addressing a public relations crisis with a well-prepared response plan.
    • Exercise: Develop a brief crisis communication plan for a potential issue your company might face.

  5. Facilitate Internal Communication

    • Definition: Ensuring effective communication within the organization to foster a positive work environment and improve employee engagement.
    • Example: Implementing an internal newsletter to keep employees informed about company news and updates.
    • Exercise: Propose two initiatives to improve internal communication in your company.

  6. Promote Innovation and Change

    • Definition: Communicating effectively to support innovation and manage change within the organization.
    • Example: Launching a campaign to introduce a new technology or process to employees.
    • Exercise: Describe a recent change in your company and how communication could have been improved to facilitate the transition.

Practical Exercise

Scenario:

Your company is planning to launch a new product in the market. As part of the communication strategy, you need to outline the objectives and how you will achieve them.

Task:

  1. Identify Objectives: List at least three objectives for the communication strategy related to the product launch.
  2. Develop Actions: For each objective, describe one action you will take to achieve it.
  3. Measure Success: Define one Key Performance Indicator (KPI) for each objective to measure the success of your communication efforts.

Solution Example:

  1. Objective: Enhance Brand Awareness

    • Action: Launch a social media campaign featuring teaser videos and behind-the-scenes content.
    • KPI: Increase in social media followers by 20% within three months.

  2. Objective: Build and Maintain Relationships

    • Action: Host a virtual launch event for key stakeholders and media.
    • KPI: Number of attendees and media mentions post-event.

  3. Objective: Support Business Goals

    • Action: Develop targeted email marketing campaigns to drive pre-orders.
    • KPI: Number of pre-orders received before the official launch date.

Summary

In this section, we covered the primary objectives of a communication strategy, including enhancing brand awareness, building relationships, supporting business goals, managing reputation, facilitating internal communication, and promoting innovation and change. By understanding and defining these objectives, you can create a communication plan that aligns with your company's strategic goals and effectively reaches your target audiences.
