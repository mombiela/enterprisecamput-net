Objective of the Exercise

The objective of this exercise is for students to apply the concepts learned about Communication Strategy to analyze the current situation of an organization or project. This analysis is crucial for developing an effective communication strategy aligned with the organization's objectives.

Instructions

  1. Select an Organization or Project: Choose a real or fictional organization or project to conduct the analysis.
  2. Gather Information: Collect relevant data about the organization or project, including its mission, vision, values, products/services, target market, and competition.
  3. SWOT Analysis: Conduct a SWOT analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) to identify the internal and external factors affecting the organization.
  4. Evaluate Current Communication: Evaluate the organization's current communication strategies, including the channels used, the main message, and the effectiveness of these strategies.
  5. Identify Problems and Opportunities: Based on the SWOT analysis and the evaluation of current communication, identify problems and opportunities to improve the communication strategy.

Components of the Analysis

1. Organization Information

  • Mission: What is the organization's reason for being?
  • Vision: What does the organization aspire to achieve in the future?
  • Values: What are the organization's fundamental principles and beliefs?
  • Products/Services: What does the organization offer to the market?
  • Target Market: Who are the main customers or users?
  • Competition: Who are the main competitors and what strategies do they use?

2. SWOT Analysis

| Internal Factors | External Factors | |-------------------|-------------------| | Strengths | Opportunities | | - Example: High product quality | - Example: Market growth | | Weaknesses | Threats | | - Example: Limited resources | - Example: Intense competition |

3. Evaluation of Current Communication

  • Channels Used: What media and platforms does the organization use to communicate with its audience (e.g., social media, email, press)?
  • Main Message: What is the central message the organization is trying to convey?
  • Effectiveness: How effective are the current communication strategies? How is this effectiveness measured (e.g., engagement metrics, sales, customer feedback)?

4. Identification of Problems and Opportunities

  • Problems: What aspects of the current communication are not working well? Why?
  • Opportunities: What new strategies or channels could improve communication? How can the strengths and opportunities identified in the SWOT analysis be leveraged?

Practical Example

Suppose we choose a tech startup that develops mobile apps for mental health.

Organization Information

  • Mission: Improve people's mental health through accessible technology.
  • Vision: To be the leading digital mental health platform worldwide.
  • Values: Innovation, accessibility, empathy.
  • Products/Services: Mobile apps for meditation, online therapy, mood tracking.
  • Target Market: Young adults aged 18 to 35.
  • Competition: Other mental health apps like Headspace and Calm.

SWOT Analysis

| Internal Factors | External Factors | |-------------------|-------------------| | Strengths | Opportunities | | - Technological innovation | - Growing interest in mental health | | - Highly skilled team | - Expansion to international markets | | Weaknesses | Threats | | - Limited financial resources | - Competition from large companies | | - Low brand visibility | - Changes in health regulations |

Evaluation of Current Communication

  • Channels Used: Social media (Instagram, Facebook), corporate blog, newsletters.
  • Main Message: "Improve your mental health with our innovative and accessible apps."
  • Effectiveness: Moderate engagement on social media, low conversion rate from newsletters.

Identification of Problems and Opportunities

  • Problems: Low brand visibility, low conversion rate from newsletters.
  • Opportunities: Collaborations with mental health influencers, more aggressive digital marketing campaigns, SEO content optimization.

Conclusion

The analysis of the current situation is a fundamental step in creating an effective communication strategy. By understanding the organization's mission, vision, values, products, target market, and competition, as well as conducting a SWOT analysis and evaluating current communication, key problems and opportunities can be identified. This exercise provides a solid foundation for developing communication strategies that are aligned with the organization's objectives and effective in reaching the desired audience.
