Introduction

In this exercise, you will learn to create effective key messages for a communication strategy. Key messages are concise statements that convey the essence of what you want to communicate to your audience. They are fundamental to ensuring that your message is clear, coherent, and memorable.

Exercise Objectives

  • Understand the importance of key messages in communication.
  • Learn to identify and formulate effective key messages.
  • Practice creating key messages for different audiences and contexts.

Key Concepts

  • Key Message: A brief and clear statement that conveys the essence of what you want to communicate.
  • Audience: The group of people to whom your message is directed.
  • Context: The circumstances or environment in which the message is communicated.

Steps to Create Key Messages

  1. Identify the communication objective:

    • What do you want to achieve with your message?
    • What action or reaction do you expect from your audience?

  2. Know your audience:

    • Who are they?
    • What are their interests, needs, and concerns?

  3. Define the main points:

    • What are the most important ideas you want to communicate?
    • What information is essential for your audience?

  4. Formulate the key messages:

    • They should be clear, concise, and easy to remember.
    • Use language that resonates with your audience.

Example of Creating Key Messages

Context: Launch of a New Technological Product

  1. Communication Objective:

    • Inform potential customers about the features and benefits of the new product.
    • Generate interest and motivate purchase.

  2. Audience:

    • Technology professionals.
    • Companies looking for innovative solutions.

  3. Main Points:

    • Technological innovation.
    • Benefits for productivity.
    • Ease of use.

  4. Key Messages:

    • "Our new product revolutionizes technology with innovations that increase productivity."
    • "Designed to be intuitive and easy to use, our product allows companies to focus on what really matters."
    • "With advanced features, our product offers innovative solutions for current technological challenges."

Practical Exercise

Instructions

  1. Select a context: Choose a relevant scenario for your professional field (e.g., service launch, awareness campaign, etc.).
  2. Define your audience: Describe who they are and their main characteristics.
  3. Identify the main points: List the key ideas you want to communicate.
  4. Create three key messages: Formulate three concise statements that convey your main points.

Example Exercise

Context: Cybersecurity Awareness Campaign

  1. Communication Objective:

    • Increase awareness about the importance of cybersecurity in the workplace.
    • Promote safe practices among employees.

  2. Audience:

    • Employees of a technology company.
    • Non-technical staff who use computer systems.

  3. Main Points:

    • Importance of cybersecurity.
    • Safe practices in password usage.
    • Recognition of common threats.

  4. Key Messages:

    • "Cybersecurity is everyone's responsibility; protect your information and your company's."
    • "Use secure and unique passwords for each account to avoid security breaches."
    • "Learn to recognize common threats like phishing to keep your data safe."

Conclusion

Creating key messages is an essential skill in communication strategy. By following a structured process, you can formulate messages that are clear, concise, and effective for your audience. Practice regularly to improve your skills and ensure you adapt your messages to different contexts and audiences.
