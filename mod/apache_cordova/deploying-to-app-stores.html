Deploying to App Stores
Handling Offline Scenarios ►

Deploying your Apache Cordova application to app stores is a crucial step in making your app available to users. This topic will guide you through the process of preparing, building, and submitting your app to both the Apple App Store and Google Play Store.

Preparing Your App for Deployment

Before deploying your app, ensure it meets the necessary requirements and guidelines set by the app stores.

  • App Store Guidelines: Familiarize yourself with the guidelines for both the Apple App Store and Google Play Store.

    • Apple App Store Guidelines
    • Google Play Store Guidelines

  • App Icons and Splash Screens: Ensure your app has the correct icons and splash screens for various devices.

    • Use the cordova-res tool to generate these assets.
    npm install -g cordova-res
cordova-res

  • App Metadata: Prepare your app's metadata, including the app name, description, keywords, and screenshots.

Building Your App

Building for iOS

  1. Set Up Your Environment: Ensure you have Xcode installed on your Mac.

    • Install Xcode from the Mac App Store.
    • Install the necessary command-line tools.
    xcode-select --install

  2. Add the iOS Platform: Add the iOS platform to your Cordova project.

    cordova platform add ios

  3. Build the App: Build your Cordova app for iOS.

    cordova build ios --release

  4. Open in Xcode: Open the generated Xcode project.

    open platforms/ios/YourApp.xcworkspace

  5. Configure Signing and Capabilities: In Xcode, configure your app's signing and capabilities.

    • Select your development team.
    • Ensure all necessary capabilities (e.g., push notifications, background modes) are enabled.

  6. Archive and Upload: Archive your app and upload it to the App Store.

    • In Xcode, go to Product > Archive.
    • Once archived, use the Organizer to upload your app to the App Store.

Building for Android

  1. Set Up Your Environment: Ensure you have Android Studio installed.

    • Install Android Studio from the official website.
    • Set up the necessary SDKs and tools.

  2. Add the Android Platform: Add the Android platform to your Cordova project.

    cordova platform add android

  3. Build the App: Build your Cordova app for Android.

    cordova build android --release

  4. Sign the APK: Sign your APK with your release key.

    # Generate a keystore if you don't have one
keytool -genkey -v -keystore release-key.keystore -alias your-key-alias -keyalg RSA -keysize 2048 -validity 10000

# Sign the APK
jarsigner -verbose -sigalg SHA1withRSA -digestalg SHA1 -keystore release-key.keystore platforms/android/app/build/outputs/apk/release/app-release-unsigned.apk your-key-alias

# Optimize the APK
zipalign -v 4 platforms/android/app/build/outputs/apk/release/app-release-unsigned.apk YourApp.apk

  5. Upload to Google Play: Upload your signed APK to the Google Play Console.

    • Go to the Google Play Console.
    • Create a new app and follow the steps to upload your APK and complete the app's listing.

Submitting Your App

Apple App Store

  • App Store Connect: Use App Store Connect to manage your app's metadata, pricing, and availability.
    • Create an app listing.
    • Fill in all required information, including app description, keywords, and screenshots.
    • Submit your app for review.

Google Play Store

  • Google Play Console: Use the Google Play Console to manage your app's metadata, pricing, and availability.
    • Create an app listing.
    • Fill in all required information, including app description, keywords, and screenshots.
    • Submit your app for review.

Conclusion

Deploying your Apache Cordova app to app stores involves several steps, from preparing your app and building it for the target platform to signing and submitting it for review. By following the guidelines and using the tools provided by Cordova, you can ensure a smooth deployment process. Remember to thoroughly test your app and adhere to the app store guidelines to increase the chances of a successful submission.
