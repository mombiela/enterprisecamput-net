Introduction

The practical evaluation in a course on Conflict Resolution is essential to ensure that participants not only understand theoretical concepts but can also effectively apply them in real situations. This section is designed to provide a structured guide on how to assess conflict resolution skills through practical exercises, case studies, and simulations.

Objectives of the Practical Evaluation

  • Measure the ability to identify and analyze conflicts.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness in applying conflict resolution techniques.
  • Observe the ability to communicate and negotiate in conflict situations.
  • Determine the ability to work as a team to resolve conflicts.

Evaluation Methods

Practical Exercises

  • Role-playing: Participants assume different roles in a simulated conflict to practice conflict resolution in a controlled environment.
  • Case Studies: Detailed analysis of real or hypothetical conflicts to apply resolution techniques.
  • Debates: Structured discussions on contentious topics to evaluate the ability to argue and negotiate.

Simulations

  • Group Simulations: Teams of participants work together to resolve a complex conflict, evaluating both group dynamics and individual skills.
  • Individual Simulations: Each participant faces a simulated conflict individually to assess their resolution and decision-making abilities.

Evaluation Criteria

| Criterion | Description | Maximum Score | |----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------| | Conflict Identification | Ability to identify the causes and parties involved in the conflict. | 20 | | Conflict Analysis | Skill in analyzing the situation and understanding the dynamics of the conflict. | 20 | | Application of Techniques | Effective use of conflict resolution techniques learned in the course. | 30 | | Communication and Negotiation | Effectiveness in communication and negotiation during conflict resolution. | 20 | | Teamwork | Ability to collaborate and work as a team during conflict resolution. | 10 |

Practical Examples

  • Example 1: Role-playing in an Office Conflict

    • Situation: Two employees have a disagreement over task allocation.
    • Roles: One participant acts as a mediator, while two others assume the roles of the conflicting employees.
    • Objective: Resolve the conflict using mediation and negotiation techniques.

  • Example 2: Case Study of an International Conflict

    • Situation: A conflict between two countries over natural resources.
    • Analysis: Participants must analyze the causes of the conflict, the parties involved, and propose a solution.
    • Objective: Apply conflict resolution techniques at a macro level.

Conclusion

Practical evaluation is an essential tool for measuring participants' effectiveness in conflict resolution. Through practical exercises, simulations, and case studies, participants can demonstrate their ability to apply learned techniques and improve their skills in a controlled environment. This evaluation not only reinforces theoretical learning but also prepares participants to face and resolve conflicts in their real work environments.
