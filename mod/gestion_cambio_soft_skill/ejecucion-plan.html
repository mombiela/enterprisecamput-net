The execution of the change plan is a crucial phase in change management, where previously designed strategies and plans are put into practice. This stage requires effective coordination, clear communication, and continuous monitoring to ensure that the change is implemented successfully and sustainably.

Objectives of the Execution of the Change Plan

  • Implement the planned strategies: Ensure that all actions and activities designed in the change plan are carried out as planned.
  • Monitor progress: Continuously evaluate the progress of the change to quickly identify and resolve problems.
  • Communicate effectively: Keep all stakeholders informed about the progress, challenges, and successes of the change.
  • Adjust as necessary: Be flexible and make adjustments to the change plan in response to feedback and observed results.

Key Steps in the Execution of the Change Plan

  1. Assignment of Roles and Responsibilities

    • Clearly define who is responsible for each task and activity.
    • Ensure that all team members understand their roles and expectations.

  2. Deployment of Resources

    • Ensure that all necessary resources (human, financial, technological) are available and accessible.
    • Efficiently manage resources to avoid waste and maximize effectiveness.

  3. Continuous Communication

    • Establish clear and effective communication channels.
    • Provide regular updates to all stakeholders.
    • Foster an environment of open and constructive feedback.

  4. Training and Support

    • Provide the necessary training for employees to adapt to the change.
    • Offer continuous support to resolve doubts and problems that may arise.

  5. Monitoring and Evaluation

    • Implement metrics and KPIs to measure the progress of the change.
    • Conduct periodic evaluations to identify areas for improvement.
    • Adjust the change plan based on the results of the evaluations.

Tools and Techniques for the Execution of the Change Plan

  • Gantt Charts: To plan and visualize the schedule of activities.
  • Kanban Boards: To manage and visualize the workflow.
  • Follow-up Meetings: To review progress and resolve problems in real-time.
  • Surveys and Feedback: To collect opinions and suggestions from employees.

Practical Example

Let's imagine a company implementing a new project management system. The change plan includes:

  • Training for all employees on the use of the new system.
  • Data migration from the old system to the new one.
  • Establishment of a support team to resolve technical issues.

Execution of the Plan

  1. Assignment of Roles and Responsibilities

    • The IT team is responsible for data migration.
    • The Human Resources department organizes the training sessions.
    • A technical support team is established to handle queries and problems.

  2. Deployment of Resources

    • A budget is allocated for training and technical support.
    • External consultants are hired to assist with data migration.

  3. Continuous Communication

    • Weekly newsletters are sent with updates on the progress of the change.
    • Bi-weekly meetings are organized with team leaders to discuss progress and challenges.

  4. Training and Support

    • Training workshops are held for all employees.
    • A help line and an email for technical support are established.

  5. Monitoring and Evaluation

    • KPIs such as the number of trained employees and the time to resolve technical issues are used.
    • Satisfaction surveys are conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of the training and support.

Conclusion

The execution of the change plan is a dynamic and critical phase in change management. It requires meticulous planning, effective communication, and the ability to adapt to overcome challenges and ensure the success of the change. By following the steps and using the appropriate tools, organizations can implement changes effectively and sustainably, thus achieving their strategic objectives.
