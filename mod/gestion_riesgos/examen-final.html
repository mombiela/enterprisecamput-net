Introduction

The final exam for the Risk Management course is designed to assess your understanding and application of the key concepts learned throughout the course. This exam will include theoretical questions, case studies, and practical exercises covering the following topics:

  • Risk identification
  • Risk assessment and analysis
  • Risk response planning
  • Risk monitoring and control
  • Risk management tools and techniques

Exam Structure

The exam will be divided into three main sections:

  1. Theoretical Questions
  2. Case Studies
  3. Practical Exercises

Theoretical Questions

This section will evaluate your knowledge of the fundamental concepts of risk management. Below are some examples of questions you might encounter:

  • Define the term "risk" in the context of project management.
  • What are the main differences between qualitative and quantitative risks?
  • List and briefly describe the stages of the risk management process.

Case Studies

In this section, you will be presented with hypothetical scenarios where you must apply your knowledge to identify, evaluate, and propose solutions to risks. Here is an example of a case study:

Case Study Example:

A software development company is working on a critical project with a tight deadline. During a project review, the following risks are identified:

  1. Delays in the delivery of key components by an external supplier.
  2. Lack of team experience with a new technology being used.
  3. Potential security issues in the software due to the integration of third-party modules.

Questions:

  • How would you prioritize these risks?
  • What mitigation strategies would you propose for each of them?
  • What key performance indicators (KPIs) would you use to monitor these risks?

Practical Exercises

This section will include exercises that allow you to demonstrate your ability to use risk management tools and techniques. Below is an example of a practical exercise:

Practical Exercise Example:

Use the Impact and Probability Analysis technique to evaluate the following risks in an ERP system implementation project:

Risk Probability (1-5) Impact (1-5)
Data migration failure 4 5
Resistance to change by staff 3 4
Budget overruns 2 3

Tasks:

  1. Calculate the risk level for each of the mentioned risks.
  2. Prioritize the risks based on their risk level.
  3. Propose a mitigation strategy for the risk with the highest level.

Conclusion

The final exam is an opportunity to demonstrate your understanding and skills in risk management. Be sure to review all course materials, practice with the provided examples and exercises, and be prepared to apply your knowledge in practical situations. Good luck!
