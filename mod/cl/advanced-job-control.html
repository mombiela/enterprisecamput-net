Introduction

In this section, we will delve into advanced job control techniques in Control Language (CL). This includes managing job queues, controlling job priorities, and handling job logs. By mastering these concepts, you will be able to optimize job processing and ensure efficient system performance.

Key Concepts

  • Job Queues: Understanding how to manage and manipulate job queues.
  • Job Priorities: Setting and adjusting job priorities to control execution order.
  • Job Logs: Analyzing job logs for troubleshooting and performance tuning.
  • Job Scheduling: Automating job execution using scheduling techniques.

Managing Job Queues

Job queues are essential for organizing and controlling the execution of jobs. You can create, delete, and manage job queues using various CL commands.

Creating a Job Queue

To create a job queue, use the CRTJOBQ command:

CRTJOBQ JOBQ(MYJOBQ) TEXT('My custom job queue')
  • JOBQ(MYJOBQ): Specifies the name of the job queue.
  • TEXT('My custom job queue'): Provides a description for the job queue.

Deleting a Job Queue

To delete a job queue, use the DLTJOBQ command:

DLTJOBQ JOBQ(MYJOBQ)
  • JOBQ(MYJOBQ): Specifies the name of the job queue to delete.

Displaying Job Queues

To display the status and details of job queues, use the WRKJOBQ command:

WRKJOBQ JOBQ(*ALL)
  • JOBQ(*ALL): Displays all job queues.

Controlling Job Priorities

Job priorities determine the order in which jobs are executed. Higher priority jobs are processed before lower priority ones.

Setting Job Priority

To set the priority of a job, use the SBMJOB command with the JOBPTY parameter:

SBMJOB CMD(CALL PGM(MYPGM)) JOB(MYJOB) JOBPTY(5)
  • CMD(CALL PGM(MYPGM)): Specifies the command to run the job.
  • JOB(MYJOB): Names the job.
  • JOBPTY(5): Sets the job priority (1 is highest, 9 is lowest).

Changing Job Priority

To change the priority of an existing job, use the CHGJOB command:

CHGJOB JOB(MYJOB) JOBPTY(3)
  • JOB(MYJOB): Specifies the job to change.
  • JOBPTY(3): Sets the new job priority.

Handling Job Logs

Job logs provide detailed information about job execution, which is crucial for troubleshooting and performance analysis.

Displaying Job Logs

To display a job log, use the DSPJOBLOG command:

DSPJOBLOG JOB(MYJOB)
  • JOB(MYJOB): Specifies the job whose log you want to display.

Printing Job Logs

To print a job log, use the PRTJOBLOG command:

PRTJOBLOG JOB(MYJOB)
  • JOB(MYJOB): Specifies the job whose log you want to print.

Job Scheduling

Automating job execution can significantly enhance efficiency. You can schedule jobs using the ADDJOBSCDE command.

Adding a Job Schedule Entry

To add a job schedule entry, use the ADDJOBSCDE command:

ADDJOBSCDE JOB(MYJOB) CMD(CALL PGM(MYPGM)) FRQ(*WEEKLY) SCDDATE(*NONE) SCDTIME(120000)
  • JOB(MYJOB): Names the job.
  • CMD(CALL PGM(MYPGM)): Specifies the command to run the job.
  • FRQ(*WEEKLY): Sets the frequency to weekly.
  • SCDDATE(*NONE): Indicates no specific start date.
  • SCDTIME(120000): Sets the scheduled time to 12:00 PM.

Removing a Job Schedule Entry

To remove a job schedule entry, use the RMVJOBSCDE command:

RMVJOBSCDE JOB(MYJOB)
  • JOB(MYJOB): Specifies the job to remove from the schedule.

Conclusion

Advanced job control in CL involves managing job queues, setting job priorities, analyzing job logs, and scheduling jobs. By mastering these techniques, you can optimize job processing and ensure efficient system performance. Practice these commands and explore their parameters to gain a deeper understanding and control over job management in your CL environment.
