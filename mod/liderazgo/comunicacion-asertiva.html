Introduction

Assertive communication is an essential skill for any leader. It involves expressing our ideas, feelings, and needs clearly and respectfully, without being aggressive or submissive to others. In this module, we will explore the principles of assertive communication and how to apply them in the context of leadership.

Principles of Assertive Communication

  • Clarity: Express ideas directly and understandably.
  • Respect: Consider and value the opinions and feelings of others.
  • Balance: Find a middle ground between aggressiveness and passivity.
  • Self-confidence: Have confidence in oneself and in what is communicated.

Assertive Communication Techniques

  • Use of "I": Instead of accusing or blaming, express how we feel and what we need. Example: "I feel that I am not being heard in meetings."
  • Active Listening: Pay full attention to the speaker, showing interest and understanding.
  • Body Language: Maintain an open and relaxed posture, make eye contact, and use gestures that reinforce the message.
  • Constructive Feedback: Offer feedback positively and oriented towards improvement. Example: "I think we could improve this project if we consider these suggestions."

Practical Examples

Example 1: Conflict Resolution

Situation: A team member is not fulfilling their responsibilities.

Assertive Communication:

  • "I have noticed that lately you have not been delivering your tasks on time. I am concerned because it affects the team. Is there something preventing you from fulfilling your responsibilities? How can I help you?"

Example 2: Resource Negotiation

Situation: You need more resources to complete a project.

Assertive Communication:

  • "To ensure this project is completed on time and with the expected quality, we need more resources. Can we discuss options to get the necessary support?"

Exercises to Strengthen Assertive Communication

  1. Role-Playing: Practice common leadership situations with a partner, focusing on using assertive communication techniques.
  2. Communication Journal: Keep a daily record of important interactions, evaluating how assertive you were and how you could improve.
  3. 360° Feedback: Request feedback from colleagues and subordinates about your communication style and work on areas for improvement.

Conclusion

Assertive communication is a powerful tool for any leader. By practicing and perfecting this skill, you will not only improve your work relationships but also foster a more collaborative and respectful work environment. Remember that the key is to find a balance between expressing your own needs and respecting those of others.
