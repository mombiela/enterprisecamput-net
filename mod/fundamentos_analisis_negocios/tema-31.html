Introduction

In this section of the course on Fundamentals of Business Analysis, we will focus on identifying strategic opportunities. This practical exercise will help you apply the concepts learned in real situations, allowing you to develop critical skills for business analysis.

Exercise Objectives

  • Understand how to identify strategic opportunities in a business environment.
  • Apply analysis techniques to evaluate the feasibility of these opportunities.
  • Develop an action plan based on the analysis performed.

Key Concepts

  • Strategic Opportunity: A situation in which a company can gain a significant competitive advantage.
  • SWOT Analysis: A tool that evaluates the Strengths, Opportunities, Weaknesses, and Threats of an organization.
  • PESTEL Analysis: Assessment of the Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Ecological, and Legal factors that can affect an organization.
  • Benchmarking: The process of comparing business practices and performance metrics with the best in the industry.

Practical Exercise

Step 1: Selection of a Company

Choose a company that you are interested in analyzing. It can be a company you work for, a well-known company in your industry, or a fictional company for the purposes of this exercise.

Step 2: Conducting a SWOT Analysis

Conduct a SWOT analysis for the selected company. Complete the following table with relevant information:

| Strengths | Opportunities | |-----------|---------------| | - Example: Recognized brand | - Example: Expansion into new markets | | - Example: Advanced technology | - Example: Strategic alliances |

| Weaknesses | Threats | |------------|---------| | - Example: Dependence on a single supplier | - Example: Growing competition | | - Example: Lack of diversification | - Example: Regulatory changes |

Step 3: Conducting a PESTEL Analysis

Conduct a PESTEL analysis for the selected company. Complete the following table with relevant information:

| Factor | Description | |--------|-------------| | Political | Example: Changes in trade policies | | Economic | Example: Fluctuations in exchange rates | | Social | Example: Changes in consumer preferences | | Technological | Example: Advances in artificial intelligence | | Ecological | Example: Stricter environmental regulations | | Legal | Example: New labor laws |

Step 4: Identification of Strategic Opportunities

Based on the SWOT and PESTEL analyses, identify at least three strategic opportunities for the company. Describe each opportunity and explain why it is viable.

Step 5: Development of an Action Plan

For each identified strategic opportunity, develop an action plan that includes:

  • Objective: What is expected to be achieved.
  • Strategy: How the objective will be achieved.
  • Necessary Resources: What resources will be needed.
  • Timeline: Deadlines for implementation.

Example of an Action Plan

Strategic Opportunity 1: Expansion into New Markets

  • Objective: Increase market share in the Asia-Pacific region.
  • Strategy: Establish partnerships with local distributors and adapt products to regional preferences.
  • Necessary Resources: Market research team, marketing budget, local staff.
  • Timeline:
    • Market research: 3 months
    • Establishing partnerships: 6 months
    • Product launch: 9 months

Conclusion

The identification of strategic opportunities is an essential skill for any business analyst. Through this practical exercise, you have learned to use analysis tools such as SWOT and PESTEL to evaluate the business environment and develop effective action plans. Practicing these methods will enable you to make informed and strategic decisions in your professional career.
