Introduction

In this section of the course on Fundamentals of Business Analysis, we will focus on a practical exercise to map and analyze a business process. This exercise will allow you to apply the theoretical concepts learned and develop essential practical skills for a business analyst.

Exercise Objectives

  • Understand the importance of process mapping in business analysis.
  • Learn to identify and document the key steps of a process.
  • Analyze a process to identify areas for improvement.

Step 1: Process Selection

The first step is to select a business process that you want to map and analyze. For this exercise, we will use a common process: the "Order Management Process."

Step 2: Process Mapping

Identification of Process Steps

  1. Order Reception: The customer places an order through the sales system.
  2. Order Verification: The sales team verifies the availability of the product.
  3. Order Confirmation: The order is confirmed to the customer.
  4. Order Preparation: The warehouse team prepares the order.
  5. Order Shipment: The order is shipped to the customer.
  6. Order Reception by the Customer: The customer receives the order.
  7. Order Closure: The order is closed in the system.

Process Flow Diagram

Below is a simplified flow diagram of the order management process:

Step 3: Process Analysis

Identification of Problems and Areas for Improvement

  • Delays in Order Verification: There may be delays in verifying the availability of the product.
  • Errors in Order Preparation: Errors in preparation can lead to incorrect shipments.
  • Lack of Communication: Lack of communication between the sales team and the warehouse can cause problems.

Improvement Proposals

  • Automation of Order Verification: Implement an automated system to verify product availability.
  • Warehouse Staff Training: Improve staff training to reduce errors in order preparation.
  • Improved Communication: Implement a more efficient communication system between the sales team and the warehouse.

Practical Exercise

Instructions

  1. Select a Process: Choose a business process in your organization or a fictitious one.
  2. Map the Process: Identify and document the key steps of the process.
  3. Create a Flow Diagram: Use a diagramming tool to create a flow diagram of the process.
  4. Analyze the Process: Identify problems and areas for improvement in the process.
  5. Improvement Proposals: Develop proposals to improve the process.

Example Code for Automation

If you decide to automate the order verification, you could use a simple Python script to check product availability:

# Example Python code to check product availability
available_products = {
    'product_1': 10,
    'product_2': 0,
    'product_3': 5
}

def check_availability(product):
    if available_products.get(product, 0) > 0:
        return "Product available"
    else:
        return "Product not available"

# Product verification
product = 'product_2'
result = check_availability(product)
print(result)  # Output: Product not available

Conclusion

Process mapping and analysis is a fundamental skill for business analysts. Through this practical exercise, you have learned to map a process, identify problems, and propose improvements. These skills will allow you to optimize processes in your organization, improving efficiency and customer satisfaction.
