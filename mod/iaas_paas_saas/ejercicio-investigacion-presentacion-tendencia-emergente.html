Introduction

In this exercise, students will research an emerging trend in the field of IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service), PaaS (Platform as a Service), or SaaS (Software as a Service). The goal is for students to understand how these trends are shaping the future of cloud technology and present their findings clearly and concisely.

Exercise Objectives

  • Develop research skills: Students will learn to search for and analyze relevant information about emerging trends.
  • Improve presentation skills: Students will practice organizing and presenting technical information.
  • Foster critical thinking: Students will evaluate the potential impact of emerging trends in the IT sector.

Instructions

  1. Selection of the Trend:

    • Choose an emerging trend in IaaS, PaaS, or SaaS. Some examples include:
      • IaaS: Infrastructure automation, Serverless infrastructure.
      • PaaS: AI-based application development platforms, Containers and Kubernetes.
      • SaaS: AI-powered SaaS applications, Industry-specific vertical SaaS.

  2. Research:

    • Conduct thorough research on the selected trend.
    • Consult reliable sources such as academic articles, industry blogs, analyst reports, and white papers.

  3. Analysis:

    • Analyze how the trend is currently impacting the market.
    • Evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of this trend.
    • Identify use cases and examples of companies that are already adopting this trend.

  4. Presentation Preparation:

    • Create a presentation that includes:
      • Introduction: General description of the trend.
      • Development: Details on how it works, its benefits, and challenges.
      • Use Cases: Practical examples of implementation.
      • Conclusion: Summary of findings and their potential impact on the future.

Example Presentation

Introduction

  • Selected Trend: Serverless infrastructure
  • General Description: Serverless infrastructure allows developers to run code without managing servers. Cloud providers manage the underlying infrastructure.

Development

  • Functioning:

    • Developers write functions that execute in response to events.
    • Cloud providers automatically scale resources according to demand.

  • Benefits:

    • Reduction in operational costs.
    • Automatic scalability.
    • Greater focus on code development rather than infrastructure management.

  • Challenges:

    • Cold start latency.
    • Limitations in runtime and resources.

Use Cases

  • Example 1: An e-commerce company uses serverless functions to process transactions in real-time.
  • Example 2: A data analytics application runs data processing tasks in response to file uploads.

Conclusion

  • Summary of Findings:

    • Serverless infrastructure offers an efficient and scalable way to run applications.
    • Despite some challenges, its adoption is rapidly growing.

  • Future Impact:

    • Serverless infrastructure is expected to continue evolving and integrate more deeply into companies' IT strategies.

Conclusion

This exercise allows students to explore and understand an emerging trend in the field of IaaS, PaaS, or SaaS. Through research and presentation, students will develop critical skills that will be useful in their professional IT careers.
