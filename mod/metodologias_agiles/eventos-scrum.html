Introduction

In Scrum, events are fundamental to structuring work and ensuring transparency, inspection, and adaptation of the process. The main events in Scrum are:

  • Sprint
  • Daily Standup
  • Sprint Review
  • Sprint Retrospective

Next, we will explore each of these events in detail.

Sprint

The Sprint is the heart of Scrum. It is a fixed period, generally 2 to 4 weeks, during which a potentially deliverable product increment is created.

Characteristics of the Sprint

  • Fixed duration: The duration of the Sprint is constant and does not change once established.
  • Clear objective: Each Sprint has a specific objective that must be achieved.
  • Immutable: Once the Sprint begins, its duration cannot be changed.

Example of a Sprint

Sprint 1: 2 weeks
Objective: Implement the user login functionality.

Daily Standup

The Daily Standup, also known as Daily Scrum, is a 15-minute daily meeting where the development team synchronizes their activities and creates a plan for the next 24 hours.

Structure of the Daily Standup

  • Duration: 15 minutes.
  • Participants: Development team, Scrum Master, Product Owner (optional).
  • Format: Each team member answers three questions:
    1. What did I do yesterday?
    2. What will I do today?
    3. Are there any impediments in my way?

Example of a Daily Standup

Developer A:
- Yesterday: Worked on the authentication API integration.
- Today: Will continue with integration tests.
- Impediments: None.

Developer B:
- Yesterday: Fixed bugs in the registration module.
- Today: Will start working on the password recovery functionality.
- Impediments: Need access to the test database.

Sprint Review

The Sprint Review is a meeting held at the end of the Sprint to inspect the increment and adapt the Product Backlog if necessary.

Objectives of the Sprint Review

  • Demonstrate completed work: The development team shows what has been achieved during the Sprint.
  • Receive feedback: Stakeholders provide feedback on the increment.
  • Update the Product Backlog: Based on the feedback, the Product Backlog can be adjusted.

Example of a Sprint Review

- Demonstration of the login functionality.
- Stakeholder feedback: Request improvements in the user interface.
- Product Backlog update: Add tasks to improve the user interface.

Sprint Retrospective

The Sprint Retrospective is a meeting held after the Sprint Review and before the next Sprint Planning. Its objective is to inspect how the last Sprint went in terms of people, relationships, processes, and tools.

Objectives of the Sprint Retrospective

  • Identify what worked well.
  • Identify what did not work well.
  • Create an improvement plan.

Example of a Sprint Retrospective

- What worked well: Effective communication among team members.
- What did not work well: Delays in API integration.
- Improvement plan: Implement a more rigorous code review to avoid delays.

Conclusion

Events in Scrum are essential to maintaining transparency, inspection, and adaptation of the process. Each event has a specific purpose and contributes to the success of the Scrum team. By understanding and correctly applying these events, teams can continuously improve and deliver high-quality products efficiently.
