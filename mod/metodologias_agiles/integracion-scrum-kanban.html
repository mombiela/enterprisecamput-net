Introduction to Scrumban

Scrumban is an agile methodology that combines elements of Scrum and Kanban to leverage the strengths of both methodologies. This integration allows development teams to be more flexible and efficient in project management.

  • Scrum: It is an agile framework that focuses on the incremental delivery of products through iterations called sprints.
  • Kanban: It is an agile methodology that focuses on visualizing the workflow and continuous improvement, allowing for continuous delivery of value.

Benefits of Scrumban

  • Flexibility: Combines the structure of Scrum with the flexibility of Kanban.
  • Continuous Improvement: Utilizes the continuous improvement practices of Kanban.
  • Work Visualization: Allows for better visualization of the workflow.
  • Adaptability: Ideal for teams that need to quickly adapt to changes in requirements.

Implementation of Scrumban

Step 1: Define the Workflow

  • Visualization: Use a Kanban board to visualize the workflow.
  • Columns: Define the board columns according to the stages of the development process (e.g., To Do, In Progress, Done).

Step 2: Establish Work in Progress (WIP) Limits

  • WIP Limits: Set limits on the amount of work that can be in progress in each column to avoid overload and improve flow.

Step 3: Planning and Review

  • Planning: Conduct planning sessions at the beginning of each sprint, similar to Scrum.
  • Review: At the end of each sprint, conduct a review of the completed work and a retrospective to identify areas for improvement.

Step 4: Prioritization and Backlog Management

  • Backlog: Maintain a prioritized backlog of tasks and features.
  • Pull System: Teams select tasks from the backlog according to priority and available capacity.

Comparison between Scrum, Kanban, and Scrumban

Feature Scrum Kanban Scrumban
Structure Iterations (sprints) Continuous flow Iterations with continuous flow
Roles Product Owner, Scrum Master, Team No specific roles Optional Scrum roles
Planning Sprint planning Continuous planning Sprint planning
Visualization Scrum board Kanban board Kanban board
WIP Limits Not applicable Yes Yes
Continuous Improvement Retrospectives Continuous improvement Retrospectives and continuous improvement

Example of a Scrumban Board

| To Do       | In Progress | Testing    | Done       |
|-------------|-------------|------------|------------|
| Task 1      | Task 2      | Task 3     | Task 4     |
| Task 5      | Task 6      |            | Task 7     |
| Task 8      |             |            |            |
  • To Do: Tasks pending to start.
  • In Progress: Tasks in the development process.
  • Testing: Tasks in the testing phase.
  • Done: Completed tasks.

Conclusion

Scrumban is a hybrid agile methodology that combines the best of Scrum and Kanban, providing flexibility and efficiency in project management. By implementing Scrumban, teams can benefit from the structure of Scrum and the visualization and continuous improvement of Kanban, better adapting to changes and continuously improving their workflow.
