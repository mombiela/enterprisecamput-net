Quotations and Citations in HTML
Preformatted Text ►

Quotations and citations are essential for referencing external sources, providing context, and adding credibility to your web content. In HTML, there are specific tags designed to handle these tasks effectively.

Block Quotations

Block quotations are used for longer quotes that are typically displayed as a separate block of text, often indented.

The `
` Tag

  • The <blockquote> tag is used to define a section that is quoted from another source.
  • It is typically displayed as an indented block of text.

Example:

<blockquote>
  "The only limit to our realization of tomorrow is our doubts of today."
  - Franklin D. Roosevelt
</blockquote>

In this example, the quote from Franklin D. Roosevelt is displayed as a block quotation.

The `cite` Attribute

  • The cite attribute can be used within the <blockquote> tag to provide a URL to the source of the quotation.

Example:

<blockquote cite="https://www.example.com/roosevelt-quote">
  "The only limit to our realization of tomorrow is our doubts of today."
  - Franklin D. Roosevelt
</blockquote>

Here, the cite attribute specifies the source URL of the quotation.

Inline Quotations

Inline quotations are used for shorter quotes that are part of a larger sentence or paragraph.

The `` Tag

  • The <q> tag is used for short, inline quotations.
  • Browsers typically render the content of the <q> tag with quotation marks.

Example:

<p>Franklin D. Roosevelt once said, <q>The only limit to our realization of tomorrow is our doubts of today.</q></p>

In this example, the quote is part of a larger sentence and is enclosed in quotation marks.

Citations

Citations are used to reference the source of a quote, a piece of information, or a work.

The `` Tag

  • The <cite> tag is used to define the title of a work, such as a book, a website, or a research paper.
  • It is typically rendered in italics by browsers.

Example:

<p>One of the most famous quotes by Franklin D. Roosevelt can be found in <cite>The Public Papers and Addresses of Franklin D. Roosevelt</cite>.</p>

In this example, the title of the work is enclosed in the <cite> tag and is rendered in italics.

Combining Quotations and Citations

You can combine block quotations and citations to provide a complete reference.

Example:

<blockquote cite="https://www.example.com/roosevelt-quote">
  "The only limit to our realization of tomorrow is our doubts of today."
  - Franklin D. Roosevelt
  <footer>Source: <cite>The Public Papers and Addresses of Franklin D. Roosevelt</cite></footer>
</blockquote>

In this example, the block quotation includes a citation within a <footer> tag, providing a complete reference to the source.

Summary

  • Use the <blockquote> tag for longer, block-level quotations.
  • Use the cite attribute within <blockquote> to provide a source URL.
  • Use the <q> tag for shorter, inline quotations.
  • Use the <cite> tag to reference the title of a work.
  • Combine quotations and citations to provide complete references.

Understanding how to properly use quotations and citations in HTML helps ensure that your web content is credible, well-referenced, and easy to read.
