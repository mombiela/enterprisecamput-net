Introduction

In the context of keyword research, relevance and pertinence are two fundamental concepts that determine the success of an SEO strategy. Understanding these concepts will allow you to select the most effective keywords to attract qualified traffic to your website.

Relevance

Relevance refers to how well a keyword aligns with your website's content and the users' search intentions. A relevant keyword is one that:

  • Reflects the content of your page: The keyword should be directly related to the main topic of your content.
  • Satisfies the user's search intention: The keyword should match what users are searching for and what they expect to find on your page.

Example of Relevance

Suppose you have a blog about cooking recipes and you are writing an article on "Healthy Dessert Recipes." Some relevant keywords could be:

  • "Healthy desserts"
  • "Low-calorie dessert recipes"
  • "Sugar-free desserts"

These keywords are relevant because they are directly related to the content of the article and to what users interested in healthy desserts might search for.

Pertinence

Pertinence, on the other hand, refers to the importance and usefulness of a keyword in the context of your SEO strategy and business objectives. A pertinent keyword is one that:

  • Has a significant search volume: The keyword should be searched by a considerable number of users.
  • Has manageable competition: The keyword should have a level of competition that is achievable for your website.
  • Generates qualified traffic: The keyword should attract users who have a high probability of becoming customers or meeting your conversion goals.

Example of Pertinence

Continuing with the previous example, suppose you research the following keywords:

| Keyword | Search Volume | Competition | Pertinence | |--------------------------------|---------------|-------------|------------| | Healthy desserts | 10,000 | High | High | | Low-calorie dessert recipes | 5,000 | Medium | High | | Sugar-free desserts | 2,000 | Low | Medium |

In this case, "Low-calorie dessert recipes" could be a very pertinent keyword because it has a good balance between search volume and competition, and it is highly relevant to your content.

Practical Exercise

To consolidate these concepts, perform the following exercise:

  1. Select a topic for your content: Choose a specific topic for which you want to conduct keyword research.
  2. Identify potential keywords: Use keyword research tools like Google Keyword Planner, SEMrush, or Ahrefs to identify keywords related to your topic.
  3. Evaluate relevance: Analyze how well each keyword aligns with your content and the users' search intention.
  4. Evaluate pertinence: Consider the search volume, competition, and the keyword's ability to attract qualified traffic.
  5. Select the best keywords: Choose the keywords that are both relevant and pertinent to your SEO strategy.

Conclusion

Relevance and pertinence are essential pillars in keyword research. While relevance ensures that the keywords align with the content and the users' search intentions, pertinence guarantees that these keywords are effective in terms of search volume, competition, and ability to attract qualified traffic. By mastering these concepts, you can optimize your SEO strategy and significantly improve your website's performance.
