Introduction

The final exam of the Agile Methodologies course is designed to assess your understanding and application of the key concepts we have covered throughout the course. This exam will include theoretical questions, case studies, and practical exercises to ensure that you can apply what you have learned in real-world situations.

Exam Structure

The exam will be divided into the following sections:

  1. Theoretical Questions
  2. Case Studies
  3. Practical Exercises

Theoretical Questions

In this section, you will be asked to answer multiple-choice, true/false, and short-answer questions about the fundamental concepts of agile methodologies.

  • Key Concepts:
    • Principles of the Agile Manifesto
    • Differences between Scrum, Kanban, and XP
    • Roles in Scrum (Scrum Master, Product Owner, Development Team)
    • Scrum Artifacts (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment)
    • Scrum Events (Sprint, Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective)

Case Studies

Here you will be presented with real-world scenarios in which you must apply your knowledge of agile methodologies to solve problems or improve processes.

  • Case Study Example:
    • Scenario: A software development company is facing communication problems and project delivery delays. The team is considering adopting Scrum to improve their workflow.
    • Questions:
      1. How would you implement Scrum in this company? Describe the initial steps.
      2. What roles and responsibilities would you assign to the team members?
      3. How would you measure the success of the Scrum implementation?

Practical Exercises

In this section, you will be asked to complete practical tasks that demonstrate your ability to apply agile methodologies in a software development environment.

  • Exercise 1: Create a Product Backlog

    • Instructions: Given a project to develop a mobile application, create a Product Backlog with at least 10 items. Make sure to prioritize the items and describe them clearly.
    • Example: 
      | ID  | Description                     | Priority | Estimation |
|-----|---------------------------------|-----------|------------|
| 1   | As a user, I want to register with my email to create an account | High      | 5 points   |
| 2   | As a user, I want to log in with my Google account               | Medium    | 3 points   |
| 3   | As an admin, I want to see a dashboard of active users           | Low       | 8 points   |

  • Exercise 2: Sprint Planning

    • Instructions: Using the Product Backlog created in the previous exercise, plan a 2-week Sprint. Select the items that the team will work on during the Sprint and justify your selection.
    • Example: 
      | ID  | Description                     | Priority | Estimation | Selected for the Sprint |
|-----|---------------------------------|-----------|------------|-------------------------|
| 1   | As a user, I want to register with my email to create an account | High      | 5 points   | Yes                      |
| 2   | As a user, I want to log in with my Google account               | Medium    | 3 points   | Yes                      |
| 4   | As a user, I want to reset my password                           | High      | 5 points   | Yes                      |

Conclusion

The final exam is an opportunity to demonstrate your understanding and ability to apply agile methodologies in practical situations. Make sure to review all key concepts, study the use cases, and practice the exercises to be well-prepared. Good luck!
