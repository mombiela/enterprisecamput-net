In this section, we will cover the process of publishing workbooks in Tableau. Publishing workbooks allows you to share your visualizations and dashboards with others, either within your organization or publicly on the web. We will start with the basics and move towards more advanced topics.

Introduction to Publishing Workbooks

Publishing a workbook in Tableau involves uploading your workbook to Tableau Server, Tableau Online, or Tableau Public. This makes your visualizations accessible to others and allows for collaboration and sharing.

Key Concepts

  • Tableau Server: An on-premises solution for sharing Tableau workbooks within an organization.
  • Tableau Online: A cloud-based solution for sharing Tableau workbooks.
  • Tableau Public: A free platform for sharing Tableau workbooks publicly on the web.

Steps to Publish a Workbook

1. Preparing Your Workbook

Before publishing, ensure your workbook is ready:

  • Clean Data: Ensure your data is clean and accurate.
  • Finalize Visualizations: Make sure all visualizations are complete and polished.
  • Check Permissions: Verify that you have the necessary permissions to publish.

2. Publishing to Tableau Server or Tableau Online

To publish a workbook to Tableau Server or Tableau Online, follow these steps:

  1. Open Tableau Desktop: Open the workbook you want to publish.

  2. Sign In: Go to Server > Sign In and enter your Tableau Server or Tableau Online credentials.

  3. Publish Workbook: Go to Server > Publish Workbook.

    • Project: Select the project where you want to publish the workbook.
    • Name: Enter a name for the workbook.
    • Data Sources: Choose whether to embed data sources or use live connections.
    • Permissions: Set permissions for who can view or edit the workbook.
    • Extracts: Decide if you want to create extracts for faster performance.

  4. Publish: Click Publish to upload the workbook.

3. Publishing to Tableau Public

Publishing to Tableau Public is similar but with a few differences:

  1. Open Tableau Desktop: Open the workbook you want to publish.
  2. Sign In: Go to Server > Tableau Public > Save to Tableau Public and sign in with your Tableau Public account.
  3. Publish Workbook: Enter a name for the workbook and click Save.

Example

Here is an example of publishing a workbook to Tableau Server:

1. Open Tableau Desktop and load your workbook.
2. Go to `Server` > `Sign In`.
3. Enter your Tableau Server credentials.
4. Go to `Server` > `Publish Workbook`.
5. Select the project, name the workbook, and set permissions.
6. Click `Publish`.

Managing Published Workbooks

Once a workbook is published, you can manage it through Tableau Server or Tableau Online:

  • Edit: Make changes to the workbook directly on the server.
  • Permissions: Adjust who can view or edit the workbook.
  • Version Control: Keep track of changes and revert to previous versions if necessary.

Example

To edit a published workbook:

  1. Sign In: Go to Tableau Server or Tableau Online and sign in.
  2. Navigate: Find the workbook you want to edit.
  3. Edit: Click on the workbook and select Edit.

Best Practices for Publishing Workbooks

  • Optimize Performance: Use extracts and optimize queries to ensure fast performance.
  • Set Appropriate Permissions: Ensure that only the right people have access to view or edit the workbook.
  • Regular Updates: Keep your workbooks updated with the latest data and visualizations.

Conclusion

Publishing workbooks in Tableau is a crucial step for sharing your insights and collaborating with others. Whether you are using Tableau Server, Tableau Online, or Tableau Public, understanding the publishing process and best practices will help you effectively share your work. Remember to prepare your workbook, set appropriate permissions, and manage your published workbooks to ensure they remain useful and relevant.
