Introduction to SCAMPER

SCAMPER is a creativity and innovation technique that helps generate new ideas and solutions by modifying existing products, services, or processes. The acronym SCAMPER represents seven different strategies: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, and Rearrange.

SCAMPER Strategies

Substitute

  • Description: Consists of replacing a part of the product, service, or process with another.
  • Example: Changing an ingredient in a recipe to make it healthier.

Combine

  • Description: Involves uniting two or more elements to create something new.
  • Example: Merging two mobile applications to offer a more complete experience.

Adapt

  • Description: Refers to adjusting a product, service, or process to work in a new context.
  • Example: Modifying software designed for large companies to also be useful for small businesses.

Modify

  • Description: Changing the size, shape, color, or other characteristics of the product, service, or process.
  • Example: Redesigning a package to make it more attractive and functional.

Put to other uses

  • Description: Using the product, service, or process for a different purpose than the original.
  • Example: Using a glass bottle as a decorative lamp.

Eliminate

  • Description: Removing parts of the product, service, or process to simplify it.
  • Example: Eliminating unnecessary features from an application to make it easier to use.

Rearrange

  • Description: Changing the order or arrangement of the components of the product, service, or process.
  • Example: Rearranging the steps of a manufacturing process to make it more efficient.

Practical Exercises

  1. Substitute Exercise: Identify a product in your work environment and think about how you could substitute one of its components to improve its functionality.
  2. Combine Exercise: Choose two services your company offers and create a proposal to combine them into one.
  3. Adapt Exercise: Take a process that works well in another department and adapt its principles to improve a process in your own department.
  4. Modify Exercise: Select a product and suggest three modifications that could make it more attractive to customers.
  5. Put to other uses Exercise: Think of a product that your company regularly discards and find a new use for it.
  6. Eliminate Exercise: Review a complex process and eliminate unnecessary steps to simplify it.
  7. Rearrange Exercise: Rearrange the workflow in your team to improve efficiency and reduce delivery time.

Conclusion

The SCAMPER technique is a powerful tool to foster innovation and creativity in any work environment. By applying these seven strategies, professionals can discover new opportunities and solutions that might otherwise go unnoticed. Regular practice with SCAMPER can help develop more flexible and open thinking, essential for facing the challenges of the modern work world.
