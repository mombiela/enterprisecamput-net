Introduction

In budget management, it is crucial to understand and comply with the financial norms and regulations that govern the sector. These norms ensure transparency, integrity, and responsibility in financial management. This topic will cover the main norms and regulations that affect budget management, providing examples and practical exercises to facilitate understanding.

Main Financial Normatives

Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX)

The Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002 is a U.S. regulation that establishes strict requirements for financial management and auditing of public companies.

  • Objective: Improve the accuracy and reliability of corporate disclosures.
  • Key Requirements:
    • Section 302: Executive responsibility for the accuracy of financial reports.
    • Section 404: Assessment of the effectiveness of internal control over financial reporting.

International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS)

The International Financial Reporting Standards are a set of accounting standards developed by the International Accounting Standards Board (IASB).

  • Objective: Provide a common language for business so that accounts are understandable and comparable internationally.
  • Key Requirements:
    • IFRS 9: Financial instruments.
    • IFRS 15: Revenue from contracts with customers.

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

The General Data Protection Regulation is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy.

  • Objective: Protect the personal data of EU citizens and regulate its processing.
  • Key Requirements:
    • Consent: Obtaining clear and explicit consent for data processing.
    • Right to be forgotten: Right of individuals to have their data erased.

Comparison of Normatives

| Normative | Main Objective | Scope of Application | Key Requirements | |-----------|----------------|----------------------|------------------| | SOX | Transparency and accountability in financial reporting | Public companies in the U.S. | Section 302, Section 404 | | IFRS | Uniformity and comparability of financial reports | International companies | IFRS 9, IFRS 15 | | GDPR | Protection of personal data | EU citizens | Consent, Right to be forgotten |

Practical Examples

Example 1: Implementation of Internal Controls (SOX)

# Example of implementing a basic internal control in Python
def verify_transaction(transaction):
    # Verify that the transaction has all the necessary fields
    necessary_fields = ['id', 'amount', 'date', 'description']
    for field in necessary_fields:
        if field not in transaction:
            raise ValueError(f"Missing field {field}")
    # Verify that the amount is positive
    if transaction['amount'] <= 0:
        raise ValueError("The amount must be positive")
    return True

# Example of usage
transaction = {
    'id': 1,
    'amount': 100.0,
    'date': '2023-10-01',
    'description': 'Purchase of supplies'
}

try:
    verify_transaction(transaction)
    print("Transaction successfully verified.")
except ValueError as e:
    print(f"Transaction error: {e}")

Example 2: GDPR Compliance in Data Handling

# Example of obtaining consent in a web application using Flask
from flask import Flask, request, jsonify

app = Flask(__name__)

@app.route('/obtain_consent', methods=['POST'])
def obtain_consent():
    data = request.get_json()
    if 'consent' not in data or not data['consent']:
        return jsonify({'error': 'Consent not given'}), 400
    return jsonify({'message': 'Consent given'}), 200

if __name__ == '__main__':
    app.run(debug=True)

Conclusion

Understanding and complying with financial norms and regulations is essential for effective and responsible budget management. Norms such as SOX, IFRS, and GDPR set the standards that organizations must follow to ensure transparency, uniformity, and data protection. Through practical examples and exercises, professionals can apply these principles in their daily work, ensuring compliance and integrity in financial management.
