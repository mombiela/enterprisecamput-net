Job Control
Scheduling Tasks with cron ►

Job control in Bash allows users to manage multiple processes (jobs) within a single shell session. This is particularly useful when running long or multiple commands simultaneously. This section will cover the basics of job control, including starting, stopping, and managing jobs.

Key Concepts

  • Foreground Job: A job that is currently running and taking input from the terminal.
  • Background Job: A job that is running but not taking input from the terminal.
  • Job ID: A unique identifier assigned to each job.
  • Process ID (PID): A unique identifier assigned to each process by the operating system.

Starting Jobs

Foreground Jobs

By default, when you run a command in Bash, it runs as a foreground job.

$ ls -l

Background Jobs

To run a job in the background, append an ampersand (&) to the command.

$ sleep 30 &
[1] 12345
  • [1] is the job ID.
  • 12345 is the process ID (PID).

Managing Jobs

Listing Jobs

To see a list of all jobs, use the jobs command.

$ jobs
[1]+  Running                 sleep 30 &

Bringing a Job to the Foreground

To bring a background job to the foreground, use the fg command followed by the job ID.

$ fg %1
sleep 30

Sending a Job to the Background

To send a foreground job to the background, first suspend it using Ctrl+Z, then use the bg command.

$ sleep 30
^Z
[1]+  Stopped                 sleep 30
$ bg %1
[1]+ sleep 30 &

Stopping and Killing Jobs

To stop a job, use the kill command followed by the job ID or PID.

$ kill %1

To forcefully kill a job, use the kill -9 command.

$ kill -9 %1

Practical Examples

Example 1: Running Multiple Jobs

$ sleep 30 &
[1] 12345
$ sleep 60 &
[2] 12346
$ jobs
[1]-  Running                 sleep 30 &
[2]+  Running                 sleep 60 &

Example 2: Bringing a Job to the Foreground and Stopping It

$ sleep 30 &
[1] 12345
$ fg %1
sleep 30
^Z
[1]+  Stopped                 sleep 30
$ jobs
[1]+  Stopped                 sleep 30

Example 3: Restarting a Stopped Job in the Background

$ sleep 30 &
[1] 12345
$ fg %1
sleep 30
^Z
[1]+  Stopped                 sleep 30
$ bg %1
[1]+ sleep 30 &

Summary

Job control in Bash is a powerful feature that allows you to manage multiple processes within a single shell session. By understanding how to start, stop, and manage jobs, you can efficiently handle long-running tasks and multitask within your terminal. Key commands include jobs, fg, bg, and kill, which provide comprehensive control over your shell processes.
