Introduction

In this module, we will address the "Project Definition" in the context of Programmatic Advertising. This is a crucial step to ensure that all stakeholders understand the objectives, scope, and expectations of the project. Programmatic advertising involves the use of technology to automate the buying and selling of online advertising spaces, allowing for greater efficiency and precision in advertising campaigns.

Project Objectives

Before starting any programmatic advertising project, it is essential to clearly define the objectives. These objectives must be specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART).

  • Specific: Detail exactly what you want to achieve.
  • Measurable: Define how success will be measured.
  • Achievable: Ensure that the objectives are realistic.
  • Relevant: Align the objectives with the overall business goals.
  • Time-bound: Establish a timeframe to achieve the objectives.

Project Scope

The project scope defines the boundaries and deliverables of the project. This includes:

  • Stakeholder definition: Identify who will be involved and their roles.
  • Necessary resources: Determine what resources (human, technological, financial) will be needed.
  • Deliverables: Specify what will be delivered at the end of the project.
  • Project boundaries: Define what is not included in the project to avoid misunderstandings.

Programmatic Advertising Strategy

Once the objectives and scope are defined, it is crucial to develop a clear strategy. This includes:

  • Audience segmentation: Identify and define the target audience.
  • Platforms and channels: Select the appropriate platforms and channels for the campaign.
  • Creativity and messages: Develop the ads and messages to be used.
  • Budget and bids: Establish the budget and bidding strategy.

Example of a Programmatic Advertising Strategy

Below is an example of how a programmatic advertising strategy could be structured:

### Objective:
Increase online sales by 20% in the next 6 months.

### Scope:
- Stakeholders: Marketing team, IT team, advertising agency.
- Resources: $50,000 budget, 2 programmatic advertising specialists, data analysis tools.
- Deliverables: Monthly performance reports, campaign optimization, ROI analysis.
- Boundaries: Does not include traditional media campaigns.

### Strategy:
- Audience segmentation: Women aged 25-45 interested in fashion and beauty.
- Platforms and channels: Google Ads, Facebook Ads, Instagram Ads.
- Creativity and messages: Visual ads with special offers and customer testimonials.
- Budget and bids: $10,000 monthly, automatic bidding strategy to maximize conversions.

Conclusion

Clearly defining the project is a fundamental step in any programmatic advertising campaign. By setting clear objectives, defining the scope, and developing a well-structured strategy, you can ensure that all stakeholders are aligned and that the project has a higher probability of success. Always remember to review and adjust the strategy as necessary to adapt to changes in the market and in the behavior of the target audience.
