Introduction

In the context of 3D mathematics, eigenvalues and eigenvectors are fundamental concepts in linear algebra that have applications in various fields such as physics, engineering, and computer science. These concepts allow us to understand how linear transformations affect vectors in a three-dimensional space.

Basic Definitions

  • Eigenvalue: It is a scalar \(\lambda\) such that there exists a non-zero vector \(\mathbf{v}\) that satisfies the equation \(A\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v}\), where \(A\) is a square matrix.
  • Eigenvector: It is a non-zero vector \(\mathbf{v}\) that satisfies the equation \(A\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v}\) for some scalar \(\lambda\).

Properties of Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

  • Eigenvalues can be real or complex.
  • Eigenvectors corresponding to different eigenvalues are linearly independent.
  • If a matrix \(A\) is symmetric, all its eigenvalues are real and its eigenvectors are orthogonal.

Calculation of Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

To find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of a matrix \(A\), the following procedure is followed:

  1. Find the eigenvalues:

    • Solve the characteristic equation \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\), where \(I\) is the identity matrix.
    • The values of \(\lambda\) that satisfy this equation are the eigenvalues of \(A\).

  2. Find the eigenvectors:

    • For each eigenvalue \(\lambda\), solve the system of equations \((A - \lambda I)\mathbf{v} = 0\) to find the corresponding eigenvectors.

Example

Consider the matrix \(A\):

\[ A = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 1 & 2
1 & 3 & 0
2 & 0 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \]

  1. Find the eigenvalues:

    • The characteristic equation is \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\).

    \[ \det \begin{pmatrix} 4 - \lambda & 1 & 2
    1 & 3 - \lambda & 0
    2 & 0 & 2 - \lambda \end{pmatrix} = 0 \]

    Solving the determinant, we obtain the characteristic polynomial:

    \[ (4 - \lambda)((3 - \lambda)(2 - \lambda)) - 1 \cdot 0 \cdot 2 + 2 \cdot 1 \cdot 0 = 0 \]

    Simplifying, we find the eigenvalues \(\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \lambda_3\).

  2. Find the eigenvectors:

    • For each eigenvalue \(\lambda\), solve \((A - \lambda I)\mathbf{v} = 0\).

    For example, if \(\lambda_1 = 5\):

    \[ (A - 5I)\mathbf{v} = 0 \implies \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 & 2
    1 & -2 & 0
    2 & 0 & -3 \end{pmatrix} \mathbf{v} = 0 \]

    Solving this system, we obtain the corresponding eigenvector.

Applications in 3D

  • Linear Transformations: Eigenvalues and eigenvectors help to understand how a linear transformation affects vectors in a three-dimensional space.
  • Tensor Analysis: In material mechanics, the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of a stress tensor provide information about the principal stress directions and the magnitudes of the principal stresses.
  • Computer Graphics: In 3D graphics, eigenvalues and eigenvectors are used in techniques such as dimensionality reduction and data compression.

Practical Exercise

Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the following matrix:

\[ B = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 & 0
0 & 3 & 4
0 & 4 & 9 \end{pmatrix} \]

  1. Calculate the eigenvalues by solving the characteristic equation.
  2. For each eigenvalue, find the corresponding eigenvectors by solving the system \((B - \lambda I)\mathbf{v} = 0\).

Conclusion

Eigenvalues and eigenvectors are powerful tools in the analysis of linear transformations in three-dimensional spaces. Understanding these concepts allows professionals to apply advanced techniques in various fields, from engineering to computer science. Practicing the calculation of eigenvalues and eigenvectors is essential to mastering their use in practical applications.
