Introduction

In this exercise, we will analyze the current brand architecture of a company. Brand architecture is the organizational structure of the brands within a company, and it is crucial for the coherence and effectiveness of marketing. This analysis will allow us to understand how the brands are presented to the market and how they relate to each other.

Objectives of the Exercise

  • Understand the structure of the current brand architecture.
  • Identify the strengths and weaknesses of the brand architecture.
  • Propose improvements based on the analysis conducted.

Steps to Conduct the Analysis

  1. Identification of Brands:

    • List all the brands that belong to the company.
    • Classify the brands into relevant categories (e.g., main brands, sub-brands, product brands).

  2. Structure of the Brand Architecture:

    • Determine the type of brand architecture the company uses:
      • Monolithic Architecture: A single parent brand that encompasses all products.
      • Endorsed Architecture: Individual brands that are backed by a parent brand.
      • Independent Brands Architecture: Each brand operates independently without a visible relationship with the parent brand.
    • Represent the structure using a diagram or table.

  3. Analysis of Brand Coherence:

    • Evaluate the coherence in the presentation of the brands (e.g., logos, colors, messages).
    • Analyze consumer perception of the relationship between the brands.

  4. Identification of Strengths and Weaknesses:

    • List the strengths of the current brand architecture.
    • Identify weaknesses or areas for improvement.

Example of Analysis

Identification of Brands

| Brand | Category | |---------------|-------------------| | Brand A | Main | | Sub-brand A1 | Sub-brand | | Sub-brand A2 | Sub-brand | | Brand B | Product | | Brand C | Product |

Structure of the Brand Architecture

Suppose the company uses an Endorsed Architecture. The structure could be represented as follows:

Brand A (Main)
  ├── Sub-brand A1
  ├── Sub-brand A2
Brand B (Product)
Brand C (Product)

Analysis of Brand Coherence

  • Logos: All sub-brands use a logo similar to the main brand, reinforcing the relationship.
  • Colors: The sub-brands maintain a color palette consistent with the main brand.
  • Messages: Marketing messages are consistent and aligned with the vision of the main brand.

Identification of Strengths and Weaknesses

  • Strengths:

    • Visual and message coherence that reinforces the identity of the main brand.
    • Clarity in the relationship between the main brand and the sub-brands.

  • Weaknesses:

    • Possible confusion between sub-brands if not adequately differentiated.
    • Risk of dilution of the main brand if the sub-brands do not maintain quality.

Conclusion

The analysis of the current brand architecture is a fundamental step to understand how the brands are presented to the market and how they relate to each other. Identifying the strengths and weaknesses of the current structure allows for proposing improvements that can strengthen the brand identity and its perception in the market. In the next exercise, we will explore how to design a more effective brand architecture based on the findings of this analysis.
